Ghostcrawler Tweets
Ghostcrawler still occasionally talks about WoW. Remember that he no longer works for or speaks for Blizzard.
"Entitlement risk" is a bizarre phrase to use for non-RNG (the lack of RNG undermining the concept of "risk").
Yeah I hesitated to use it but it Twitter isn't great for nuance. (OccupyGStreet (https://twitter.com/OccupyGStreet/status/685193226416861184))
"Entitlement" can carry connotations for being a spoiled brat, which isn't at all the context I intended. (OccupyGStreet (https://twitter.com/OccupyGStreet/status/685193443824422912))
But when a reward is not very RNG or too tied to very high skill, you can cause players to expect they WILL earn it. (OccupyGStreet (https://twitter.com/OccupyGStreet/status/685193893919342592))
Extreme case: play 10,000 games to win something. The intention may be that makes the reward rare. (OccupyGStreet (https://twitter.com/OccupyGStreet/status/685194060261269504))
Bit the reality may be that you strongly encourage many players to go engage in something that really isn't that fun. (OccupyGStreet (https://twitter.com/OccupyGStreet/status/685194265555648512))
By contrast most players won't farm a 0.001% drop chance. They don't have the expectation that they WILL get the item. (OccupyGStreet (https://twitter.com/OccupyGStreet/status/685194490643021827))
Why is it bad to be able to work reliably toward a goal?
Actual skill challenge or RNG make things rare. Long grinds can make players hate their lives (and you). (OccupyGStreet (https://twitter.com/OccupyGStreet/status/685218152087605248))
The goal may be for a small percent of players to achieve a goal or reward, which is also fine. (OccupyGStreet (https://twitter.com/OccupyGStreet/status/685217191231262720))
The risk is if devs naively say "We will make this rare by making it super grindy." (OccupyGStreet (https://twitter.com/OccupyGStreet/status/685217630651715584))
Long term achievable goals can be fun but excessive length to promote scarcity usually doesn't feel good. (OccupyGStreet (https://twitter.com/OccupyGStreet/status/685217895186481152))

Hey GC, what are your thoughts as a Dev on SWTORs level-sync system? Frankly, I LOVE it. I can play any "zone" at any level!
The risk of level sync is if players feel no sense of accomplishment. "Oh, I am level 15 now but so are those rats." (OccupyGStreet (https://twitter.com/OccupyGStreet/status/685248572246200321))
Not saying SWTOR has this problem and it's not an Achilles' heel problem, just a consideration. (OccupyGStreet (https://twitter.com/OccupyGStreet/status/685248774067781632))


Computers - Setup of the Month
Each month or so we will post a couple of hardware setups for those of you who are thinking of upgrading their computer!

This month
The current SSD prices (http://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2016/january/ssdgraph.gif) are fairly flat right now, with most SSDs under $0.50 per gigabyte when on sale. New GPUs may be coming towards the middle of this year

Depending on how much you want to overclock, you may need to spend a bit more for a better CPU cooler like the NH-D15 (http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00L7UZMAK?ie=UTF8&tag=mmochamp-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=390957&creativeASIN=B00L7UZMAK).

Unless you are doing a lot of video rendering or streaming, you do not need the 6700K and should spend that money elsewhere.

Make sure to keep your AMD (http://sites.amd.com/us/game/downloads/Pages/downloads.aspx) and Nvidia (http://www.nvidia.com/Download/index.aspx?lang=en-us) drivers up to date. Don't forget to visit the Computer Forum (http://www.mmo-champion.com/forums/290-Computer) for any extra questions!
