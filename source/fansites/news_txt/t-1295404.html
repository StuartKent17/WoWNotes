Patch 5.3 - Dungeon Journal UI
Patch 5.3 finally makes it faster to move through the Dungeon Journal by putting boss selections for an instance on the left always, and loot, abilities, and model on the right, making it much faster to switch between bosses to find the right loot list.


http://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2013/may/thumb/dungeonJournal01.jpg (http://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2013/may/dungeonJournal01.jpg)


Otis - Dungeon Journal Addon
If you don't want to wait a few weeks for Patch 5.3, you can install the Otis addon (http://www.curse.com/addons/wow/otis) to make life a little bit easier. It adds arrows to the side of the Dungeon Journal that allows you to quickly move up and down through the boss list.

Can you reconcile your tweet yesterday about yr friend being "just a druid" w/ the "Bring the player not the class" mentality?

And how about "bring player - not spec" argument?

How much would you like Crit, Haste and Mastery to be just as good as one another for all specs, allowing freedom for style?
It's tricky. If they are all interchangeable, then you don't look at the stats at all. Better would be if you can feel the delta. (Source (https://twitter.com/Ghostcrawler/status/330062615797719040))

I was looking at simulation numbers on noxxic. Can't believe I'm alone in thinking it's demotivating to see my class so low.

So even if e.g. the hunter simming is strong, it may not mesh with the shaman simming. (Source (https://twitter.com/Ghostcrawler/status/330443160398475264))

I have even seen in blue posts where you or someone else says normal raiders run LFR! Pls get stories straight!!!

In 5.2, LFR offers much less to N raiders unless you've been unlucky or are impatient to complete a set or something. (Source (https://twitter.com/Ghostcrawler/status/329761270549069824))
As I've said, if it's fun for you, go for it. I wouldn't consider it mandatory though. (Source (https://twitter.com/Ghostcrawler/status/329761437834702849))

It comes down to your error rate on mechanics. Improving ilevel can gives you more room to have more errors and still win. (Source (https://twitter.com/Ghostcrawler/status/329761797718540288))

Computers - Setup of the Month (by chaud (http://www.mmo-champion.com/members/523085-chaud))
Each month or every 2 months, depending on the hardware evolutions, the lovely chaud (http://www.mmo-champion.com/members/523085-chaud) will work on a couple of hardware setups for those of you who are thinking of upgrading their computer!

This month
The current SSD prices (http://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2013/may/ssdgraph.gif) seem to be moving back up slowly, with almost all SSDs under $1 per gigabyte when on sale. No new GPUs or SSDs worth waiting for are expected before the third quarter this year. RAM prices have been increasing for the past few months, so if you were thinking about adding RAM anytime soon, now is the time.

The new Haswell CPUs from Intel are almost here and should bring a mild performance improvement, power usage reduction, and significantly better integrated graphics.

Unless you are doing a lot of video rendering or something that is similarly multithreaded, you do not need the 3770K and should spend that money elsewhere.

Make sure to keep your AMD (http://sites.amd.com/us/game/downloads/Pages/downloads.aspx) and Nvidia (http://www.nvidia.com/Download/index.aspx?lang=en-us) drivers up to date.

Don't hesitate to post any feedback in the comments of that news post, and don't forget to visit the Computer Forum (http://www.mmo-champion.com/forums/290-Computer) for any extra questions! If you are interested in Folding@home, take a look in our team's thread (http://www.mmo-champion.com/threads/948557-MMO-C-folding-team).
