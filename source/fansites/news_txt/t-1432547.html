Class Balance
The reactions to tweets in the MMO-C DK community are making me giggle.
Referring to my tweet about them misusing "Scaling"? Yeah.. Such a misunderstood word. (Source (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/410884235709276160))
Pro Tip: If you argue that you need buffs, and say that it's because of "scaling" issues, your argument loses credibility. (Source (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/410884905023705089))
There are a few exceptions to this, such as deep analysis such as Theck's. But most people use it as an "I feel weak" fallback (Source (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/410885206870999040))
Is "I feel weak" necessarily useless? I mean it's not a reason to buff since feeling != actuality and some ppl are bad but...?
No. I'm just saying that "I feel weak" and "I'm weak because scaling" are the same thing. "Scaling" isn't helping your argument. (Source (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/410948082465898496))
So, if you do not want us to call that an issue with "scaling", what word would you like us to use to convey that issue?
Trust me, I totally understand how EXACTLY what you describe is the impression you get. Missing piece is all the buffs/nerfs... (Source (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/410954444956635137))
...that happened in between. Those likely had as much to do with power changes as the change in ilvl. (Source (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/410955200778604544))
I think it's important to remember that I am not, AT ALL, disagreeing with your feeling of weakness. Suggesting feedback types. (Source (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/410955841160740865))
So if we don't have a PhD in math we shouldn't post our concerns/opinions. Wow you lost all credibility right there.
Didn't say that at all. Said that you aren't using scaling properly. Feedback still useful. (Source (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/410900588499443712))
So key points for using scaling are: Do you provide context? Is it a quantifiable issue? Do you have evidence to support that?
Yep! (Source (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/410896578606411776))

It's hard and frustrating to understand them, but we should try and not dismiss them just b/c "scaling"
Not dismissing the feedback, just pointing out that people use "scaling" to make their argument sound credible. Actually opposite. (Source (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/410892698669703170))
