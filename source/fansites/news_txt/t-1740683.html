Game Design
It's been said before - an idea is only as good as your ability to execute on it, or get other people passionate about it. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/576968152963112960))
In my experience, real talent often comes in recognizing the great ideas that will fit cleanly with the rest of your game. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/576968227445592064))
And good designers know how to let ideas go quickly and keep looking, rather than getting attached to something with too much baggage. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/576968360178536449))
This year, I'm going to challenge myself to get a lot of ideas out, to learn to sell them in a small space (gg twitter), and to let them go. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/576968612033863681))
They won't all be great and I don't care if you don't like them. Hopefully some inspire something. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/576968739502968832))

It's all or nothing. One guided goal sets an expectation for structure vs. fully free-form.
Yeah, that's usually the case. You can tutorialize without specific goals though. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/577214263258800128))
And I personally think there are ways to set long term goals without explicitly spelling out the game along the way. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/577214428036218880))
I know you can't talk WoW but this is the exact issue why I don't like the Apexis hubs. No framing device (text). No fun.
Yeah, WoW has set an expectation for information that makes open POIs and puzzles prone to feel uncomfortable. Hard problem. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/577215581918646272))
I think THAT comes down to pace. When you have a cinematic flow driven by story, sudden halts in game play are jarring.
That's fair. Taking control of the camera from the players is easy to make painful if done in the wrong place or too often. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/577219703543717889))

Adding explicit hand-held goals to an open world game fundamentally changes the way players experience it. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/577212119692619776))
It changes the pace, the potential emotional investment, the expectations. It's a legitimate approach, but it's a dramatic design choice. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/577212885681586176))
I'm very interested in seeing more devs hold back in the hand-holding. On-board players gently, but let them unfold the game on their own. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/577213249613000704))
I wish the dev teams for CoD and BF would understand that. they don't even let you open your own doors.
Hah! Yeah. It's somewhat core identity for them though. Extremely approachable and very cinematic. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/577217060096241664))
Yeah, I'm probably not the target audience for those games. I prefer long SP campaigns because I don't do MP apart from WoW/HS.
Yeah, I can identify with that. I like CoD campaigns, but I LOVE a nice long campaign of Fallout: New Vegas. (craig_amai (https://twitter.com/craig_amai/status/577221579223867392))
