Warlords of Draenor - Gathering Professions
Today we are taking a look at the changes to Mining, Herbalism, and Skinning in Warlords of Draenor, as well as the garrison buildings that accompany them.

Mining

Two new types of ore: Blackrock Ore (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109118-blackrock-ore) and True Iron Ore (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109119-true-iron-ore).
Warlords of Draenor removes the smelting step and uses ores directly.
No need to go to a trainer to increase your Mining cap, just go to Draenor and start playing, A Treatise on Mining in Draenor (http://www.wowdb.com/items/111349-a-treatise-on-mining-in-draenor) will drop from a mob or appear when gathering something.
You can now mine any node at any level and you will receive Nuggets (http://www.wowdb.com/items/trade-goods/metal-stone?filter-search=Nugget) instead of the normal ore if your skill level is too low. Nuggets can be combined (http://www.wowdb.com/spells/157517-true-iron-nugget) to form ore.
You will also receive Draenic Stone (http://www.wowdb.com/items/115508-draenic-stone) when mining in Draenor, which is used for work orders at your garrison's mine (http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/1477-Warlords-of-Draenor-Garrisons-Lunarfall-Excavation-Frostwall-Mines).
Ore is acquired in several ways:

Drops from Goren Protector (http://www.wowdb.com/npcs/85294-goren-protector) while mining.
Mined from deposits (http://www.wowdb.com/objects/mining-veins?filter-id-val=222000&filter-id-op=1&filter-search=Deposit) found in the world.
Everyone can collect some ore daily from their garrison's mine (http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/1477-Warlords-of-Draenor-Garrisons-Lunarfall-Excavation-Frostwall-Mines).
Purchased (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109118-blackrock-ore?reagent-for-page=2#related:sold-by;_!) with Garrison Resources (http://www.wowdb.com/currencies/824-garrison-resources) from the Trading Post (http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/893-Warlords-of-Draenor-Garrisons-Trading-Post) in your garrison.
Contained in salvage containers (http://www.wowdb.com/items/consumables/consumable?filter-search=Salvage) from your garrison's Salvage Yard.

The Goren Protector (http://www.wowdb.com/npcs/85294-goren-protector) that will occasionally spawn and attack you has a low chance to drop the Red Goren Egg (http://www.wowdb.com/items/118919-red-goren-egg) (Ore Eater (http://www.wowdb.com/npcs/85667-ore-eater)) pet.



Herbalism

Seven new herbs: Talador Orchid (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109129-talador-orchid), Starflower (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109127-starflower), Nagrand Arrowbloom (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109128-nagrand-arrowbloom), Gorgrond Flytrap (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109126-gorgrond-flytrap), Frostweed (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109124-frostweed), Fireweed (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109125-fireweed), Chameleon Lotus (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109130-chameleon-lotus).
No need to go to a trainer to increase your Herbalism cap, just go to Draenor and start playing, A Compendium of the Herbs of Draenor (http://www.wowdb.com/items/111350-a-compendium-of-the-herbs-of-draenor) will drop from a mob or appear when gathering something.
You can now herb any node at any level and you will receive fragments (http://www.wowdb.com/items/trade-goods/herb?filter-expansion=6&filter-ilvl-min=2&filter-ilvl-max=90) instead of the normal herb if your skill level is too low. Fragments can be combined (http://www.wowdb.com/spells/157022-broken-frostweed-stem) to form herbs.
You will also receive Draenic Seeds (http://www.wowdb.com/items/116053-draenic-seeds) when gathering herbs in Draenor, which is used for work orders and purchasing items at your garrison's herb garden (http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/1478-Warlords-of-Draenor-Garrisons-Herb-Garden).
Herbs are acquired in several ways:

Drops from Disturbed Podling (http://www.wowdb.com/npcs?filter-search=Disturbed+Podling) while gathering herbs.
Gathered from nodes (http://www.wowdb.com/objects/herbs?filter-id-val=222000&filter-id-op=1) found in the world.
Everyone can collect some herbs daily from their garrison's herb garden (http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/1478-Warlords-of-Draenor-Garrisons-Herb-Garden).
Purchased (www.wowdb.com/items/109129-talador-orchid#related:sold-by;_!) with Garrison Resources (http://www.wowdb.com/currencies/824-garrison-resources) from the Trading Post (http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/893-Warlords-of-Draenor-Garrisons-Trading-Post) in your garrison.
Contained in salvage containers (http://www.wowdb.com/items/consumables/consumable?filter-search=Salvage) from your garrison's Salvage Yard.

The Disturbed Podlings (http://www.wowdb.com/npcs?filter-search=Disturbed+Podling) that will occasionally spawn and attack you have a low chance to drop the Nightshade Sproutling (http://www.wowdb.com/items/118595-nightshade-sproutling) (Nightshade Sproutling (http://www.wowdb.com/npcs/83594-nightshade-sproutling)) pet.



Skinning

One new skin: Raw Beast Hide (http://www.wowdb.com/items/110609-raw-beast-hide).
No need to go to a trainer to increase your Skinning cap, just go to Draenor and start playing, A Guide to Skinning in Draenor (http://www.wowdb.com/items/111351-a-guide-to-skinning-in-draenor) will drop from a mob or appear when gathering something.
You can now skin any skinnable animal at any level and you will receive fragments (http://www.wowdb.com/items/trade-goods/leather?filter-expansion=6&filter-ilvl-max=90) instead of the normal skin if your skill level is too low. Fragments can be combined (http://www.wowdb.com/spells/159069-raw-beast-hide-scraps) to form useful skins.
You can also obtain skins from your garrison's barn (http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/885-Warlords-of-Draenor-Garrisons-Barn)
Skins are also contained in salvage containers (http://www.wowdb.com/items/consumables/consumable?filter-search=Salvage) from your garrison's Salvage Yard.



Warlords of Draenor - Garrisons: Herb Garden
The Herb Garden (http://www.wowdb.com/garrison/buildings/137-herb-garden) has its own plot that no other buildings can be placed upon. Everyone with a garrison has access to this building and is able to gather from the nodes, even without the Herbalism profession.

Abilities
This building is good for access to Herbalism nodes, even without the Herbalism profession.


Level 1 - Grows Draenic Seeds into herbs each day and herbalism nodes are available in the garden.
Level 2 - Allows followers with the Herbalism trait to work here, granting a unique bonus. Also increases the amount of herbs that can be harvested at the garden and allows up to 14 work orders at one time.
Level 3 - Grow a unique Draenor fruit tree in your garden that grants food buffs. Also allows up to 21 work orders at one time.



Level 3 Unlock
The Level 3 version of this building is unlocked by completing the Draenic Seed Collector (http://www.wowdb.com/achievements/9454-draenic-seed-collector) achievement, which requires you to collect 500 Draenic Seeds (http://www.wowdb.com/items/116053-draenic-seeds) from herbalism nodes.


Herbalism Output
Each node has a chance to spawn a Disturbed Podling (http://www.wowdb.com/npcs?filter-search=Disturbed%20Podling), which will attack you and drop extra herbs.


Level 1 - 6 herbalism nodes
Level 2 - 11 herbalism nodes
Level 3 - 16 herbalism nodes



Work Orders
The Herb Garden allows you to collect Draenic Seeds (http://www.wowdb.com/items/116053-draenic-seeds) from herbalism nodes. You can then trade these seeds for additional herbs.

For an added bonus and the ability to select what herbs grow every day, you can assign a follower (http://beta.wowdb.com/garrison/followers/180-shadow-hunter-rala-fiona) with Herbalism (http://beta.wowdb.com/garrison/abilities/53-herbalism) to your garden.

You can purchase a battle pet or items with farming tool models from the vendor in your garden.

Warlords of Draenor - Garrisons: Mines
The Lunarfall Excavation / Frostwall Mines (http://www.wowdb.com/garrison/buildings/61-lunarfall-excavation) has its own plot that no other buildings can be placed upon. Everyone with a garrison has access to this building and is able to mine the nodes inside, even without the Mining profession.

Abilities
This building is good for access to mining nodes, even without the Mining profession.


Level 1 - Allows players to mine Draenic Stone from mineral nodes in Draenor. Draenic Stone can be converted to work orders for Draenor minerals.
Level 2 - Allows followers with the Mining trait to work here, granting a unique bonus. Also opens a new mineshaft, granting access to additional mining nodes and allows up to 14 work orders at one time.
Level 3 - A final mineshaft allows access to rich mining nodes. In addition, garrison guards will patrol your mine, keeping it clear of hostile goren. Also allows up to 21 work orders at one time.



Level 3 Unlock
The Level 3 version of this building is unlocked by completing the Draenic Stone Collector (http://www.wowdb.com/achievements/9453-draenic-stone-collector) achievement, which requires you to collect 500 Draenic Stone (http://www.wowdb.com/items/115508-draenic-stone) from mining nodes.


Mining Output
Players can extract resources from nodes in the mine, even without the Mining profession. Nodes will show up on the map, just like they would if you had Mining.

There are also Mine Carts (http://www.wowdb.com/objects/232541-mine-cart) in the Level 2 and Level 3 mine, which contain Warlords Archaeology Fragments, Ore, Preserved Mining Pick (http://www.wowdb.com/items/118903-preserved-mining-pick), and Miner's Coffee (http://www.wowdb.com/items/118897-miners-coffee). The Mining Pick and Coffee drop frequently enough that you will have at least one to use every day while mining.


Daily Output
Keep in mind that this is a rough idea of how much you can expect each day, not exact numbers.


Level 1 - 20 x True Iron Ore (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109119-true-iron-ore), 10 x Blackrock Ore (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109118-blackrock-ore), and 15 x Draenic Stone (http://www.wowdb.com/items/115508-draenic-stone).
Level 2 - 35 x True Iron Ore (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109119-true-iron-ore), 15 x Blackrock Ore (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109118-blackrock-ore), and 30 x Draenic Stone (http://www.wowdb.com/items/115508-draenic-stone).
Level 3 - 55 x True Iron Ore (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109119-true-iron-ore), 25 x Blackrock Ore (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109118-blackrock-ore), and 40 x Draenic Stone (http://www.wowdb.com/items/115508-draenic-stone).



Work Orders
The Mine allows you to collect Draenic Stone (http://www.wowdb.com/items/115508-draenic-stone) from mining nodes. You can then trade 5 of these for some ore.

For an added bonus, you can add a follower (http://www.wowdb.com/garrison/followers/157-lantresor-of-the-blade-lantresor-of-the-blade) with the Mining (http://www.wowdb.com/garrison/abilities/52-mining) ability.

Blue Posts
EU Feedback Thread Comment in Documentary
We do get valuable feedback from the EU forums, both directly from the English forums and translated/summarized by members of our Community team from the other language forums. I winced a little bit at that quote in the documentary as well, just because I knew it might be taken the wrong way.

For full context, for those who weren't participating or around during the early days of our Warlords beta testing, the first day of raid testing was a mess. We scheduled a Butcher test as our first raid test of the expansion and the massive number of people trying to zone in at once was chain-crashing our instance servers (due to a bug that we found and fixed within a few hours, thanks to the data obtained from monitoring testers). I had made specific feedback threads on both the NA and EU forums (as I always do) for the boss, and unsurprisingly the threads ended up being dominated by comments about server stability with little useful info about the bosses themselves. That's why I said "there is actually some genuine feedback..." - we'd written off the test as something that wasn't likely to yield anything constructive at all.

In general, we get (and use) tons of useful feedback from our EU raid tests. As I said on Twitter, that brief clip was about this one specific thread, and was not a general statement.

You have to admit scrolling past the Picard facepalm ASCII made for some fine comic timing alongside my comments, though - kudos to the editing team for that pairing. (Blue Tracker (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/topic/348870-if-you-go-see-the-eu-feedback-thread/) / Official Forums (http://eu.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/12618233516?page=2#31))

World of Warcraft: Warlords of Draenor available for pick-up (final sales receipt required).
Blizzard developer signing tables open.
