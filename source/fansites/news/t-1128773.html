Bringing Achievements to the Account Level
We recently added account-level changes to achievements in World of Warcraft: Mists of Pandaria, and there’s still a lot of work left before we’re done. The plan here is to illustrate the intent behind our design, which should in turn help you figure out the answers to many of your questions.

Overall, we never want you to play Character A instead of Character B because of achievement concerns. If Character A had the Violet Proto-Drake, then you might not play Character B. If Character A was only one holiday away from the Violet Proto-Drake, then you may not play Character B. If Character A had completed most of the raid achievements from Dragon Soul, you may not want to bring Character B for one fight and miss out on the achievement. Having alts is cool and working on achievements is cool, but we don’t want the two systems to work against each other.

This goal is paramount and drives everything else. If we allowed you to earn extra achievement points from completing an achievement on two different characters, then you might only want to play the character with the most points and you’d feel like you had to grind through all the achievements with every alt, thus defeating the purpose of having account-level achievements.

Most achievements are account-wide

This means you only earn the points once. If you have earned an achievement on one character, you can see it on all your characters. However, and this is important, you will still see the achievement toast (the pop-up notification) if a second or subsequent character completes the achievement. We think it’s still important to recognize milestones like reaching level 80, maxing out a profession, or killing a raid boss for the first time. It's fun to have everyone congratulate you when you get the toast. Nonetheless, this will just be a new character of yours completing the achievement that you’ve already earned on your account. You won’t double up on points.

Most criteria are not account-wide

If you start an achievement on one character, you can't then finish the achievement on another (there are exceptions, so please keep reading). We didn't think it made sense for one character to get level 60 and another to get level 20 and then see the "level 80" achievement toast. If you start to explore Thousand Needles on one character, you’ll probably want to finish that achievement on the same character.

Some achievements are "meta achievements."

These are achievements that require you to get other specific achievements. An infamous example is "What a Long, Strange Trip It's Been." These achievements generally ARE account-wide. If one character earned Noble Gardener and another character earned The Flame Warden, then your account would get Long Strange Trip. (Otherwise it would be weird: it would look as if you had the sub-achievements, but you wouldn't have the meta-achievement). Even though you need Explore Thousand Needles on one character, all of your characters can contribute to Explore Kalimdor.

A very few achievements are account-only.

There are two categories of account-only achievements. One is achievements that are not possible to earn on one character. If we made an achievement to level every class to level 90, it would be account-only. (I'm not sure we will, but it's a good example.) The other category are achievements that are just brutal to complete on one character (and you’d never want to do for multiples), such as 2500 daily quests or 250K honorable kills. In these cases, the cumulative work of all your characters on those criteria will count.

We hope that rewards granted from achievements (pets, mounts, titles, and tabards) will be shared at the account level.

I'm not going to promise this yet, because a lot of magic has to happen for that to work, but it's our intent. We have discussed having a character level requirement for some rewards, so that your level-2 gnome couldn’t walk around with the “Defender of a Shattered World” title just because your level-90 shaman earned it. If the gnome made it to level 80, though, you could proudly show off your title. You also won’t be able to use a faction-specific pet, mount, or title on the wrong faction. The achievement wouldn’t go away -- you just wouldn’t be able to show it off on that character. There are probably additional exceptions and details we’ll find as we dig deeper into the system.

This is the kind of system that will evolve over time, and we don’t think this has to be the way achievements work forever. As beta players start experimenting with it and offering feedback, I am certain that we will end up making even more changes.

Greg “Ghostcrawler” Street is Lead Systems Designer for World of Warcraft. He loves you very, very much. Yes, you.

Unfortunately, account wide achievements, pets and mounts do create a pretty significant farming problem. I relate it to the 10 mans being "optional" to 25 man guilds back in Wrath of the Lich King. Seeing as these account wide things do not effect character power (or do they? read on) I don't see it as a significant problem just yet! Let's look at this in depth.

Imagine the Darkmoon Faire dailies from a min-maxing perspective. I do the daily quests and profession quests on 8 different characters. 6 of those characters buy the available, non-combat pets, 2 of them buy the mounts. If I want to get these things at the quickest pace, I am forced to play different characters. This may be required for competitive pet battling. DMF isn't the only place this exists. This goes against the philosophy of 'play the character you want to play.'
- Darkmoon Faire
- Orphan Holiday x 3 (Shat, Dal & Home City)
- Argent Tournament
- Any Daily Hub in general because of the daily cap (25 / day)
- Old Man Barlow Fishing Daily (Crocklisk in the city)
- Really, any daily quest that has a 'chance' at a non-combat pet reward

What about rare mounts? I can solo exclusive raid / dungeon lockouts up to 50 times / day (or week for raids).
- Anzu
- White Hawkstrider
- Firey Warhorse
- Kael'Thas, although this was already able to be done because it's BoE
- Alysrazor (Soloed by shadow priest and boomkin)
- Any daily quest which has a 'chance' at a mount reward

Certain Meta-Achievements could be artificially made easier as well in this system, greatly benefiting characters with ALTs.
- Glory to the Raider style achievements could be done on an ALT while the heroics are completed on a main
- Achievements in heroic dungeons could be attempted multiple times in the case of failure without relying on RNG of the dungeon finder to take you there. I'm looking at you, speed run of Deadmines Dream! Jump that electricity!
- If a certain faction has a quest chain or other achievement that's easier like Loremaster in some zones.

For avid collectors of pets, mounts and achievements, ALTs are no longer 'optional', they are a requirement. In essence, it gives them a competitive edge that a solo-character player does not have. Some assumed retorts to this are as follows.

1) The time commitment is still the same - This is very true, and probably what makes most of the above statements okay. The DMF dailies I did 6 times, 3 months in a row and, as a result, I now have all 6 pets. I was lazy and only got the turn ins on one character who has one of the mounts. Still, this doesn't solve the problem of earning the rewards too quickly, which means that I will probably not be doing the DMF any more until there are new rewards. It also doesn't change the fact that I had to do these on characters I didn't want to play, especially ones who don't have epic mounts yet! Argent Tournament dailies (again, these had BOE pets, and I did farm these on one character to give to my main) would be an even bigger burden if I didn't want to play certain characters, but wanted to earn the pets and mounts at a faster rate.

2) Character power isn't effected, so although you can farm more, it doesn't make you overpowered! - This is still up in the air to me. In pet battles, for one, your power is effected. A variety of pets means a variety of options when fighting opponents. What's interesting is that reputations will not be cross-character just yet, but the head enchants are already BoA. This very slight effect on power is a great quality of life option that effects character power in a positive way. So aside from pet battles which don't have a ladder and are just a mini-game... for now, what character benefits could you get?
- Certain mounts are power benefits, including the new mount that can walk on water, mounts which can swap between land / ground water mounts and passenger mounts
- When leveling a new character in Vash'ir, a second character will have a sea horse right away!
- The economy will take a hit. You only need to get that expensive mount on one character now, so overall character wealth will go up.

3) The good outweighs the bad! - It's a slippery slope to a power benefit being available by having ALTs. Already, characters can farm valor points on their ALTs and pass off BoE items to their mains, though this will be "fixed" when valor points provide only character upgrades. We have to be careful what we wish for with account wide character rewards. Even if they aren't power related, burn out is a possibility which lowers the number of players in the player base. Carrots on a stick good, but chase too many carrots and your neck starts hurting!

Fact is, we have to tread lightly as more of these options are added to a game where they weren't in the original design. The moment there's a weekly, sustainable reason to play ALTs for power is the moment things have gone too far (see frost badges and http://www.wowhead.com/item=49908/primordial-saronite). The old days where power was related to game time spent is the most important fallacy of MMOs gone by. The last thing we need is for anyone to even have the option of farming so much, they burn out!
