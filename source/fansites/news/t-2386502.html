Empire Strikes Back had bombs dropping in space too. See the asteroid belt scene when the Empire is searching for the Falcon; you'll see TIE Bombers doing bombing runs. And who's to say the bombs aren't propelled rather than just "dropped"?

Well, no. The TIE bombers don't actually carry "bombs". They carry concussion missiles, proton torpedoes, grenades (thermal detonators), and mines. If you watch Empire Strikes Back, you can see the ordinance are blue (the actual weapon in flight, not just the blast) and they "streak" towards the asteroid, heavily implying that they're not being dropped - they're being fired (covered in some kind of plasma sheath like the proton torpedoes from A New Hope). https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=phGlo_TNDp0

Compare that to Last Jedi, where it very clearly appears that the bombs are just rolling off a rack and slowly tumble towards the dreadnought "below". https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_oiT7Kkx630 The Visual Dictionary says they're using electromagnets to leave the bay, and then are pulled to their target magnetically. Technically, since they're being "launched" off the rails (like a really weak rail gun), the weapon functions more like a mortar firing shells. If you consider magnetic attraction a sort of guided propulsion, then then it's essentially a missile launcher. I don't know how the bombs differentiate a target they pull towards from other bombs or what they were launched from (the bomber), but I presume there's some sci-fi handwaving involved.

Bombs and depth charges are dropped, though. That's what makes them bombs, and not missiles, rockets, torpedoes, mortar shells, or mines.

Mostly I think Last Jedi wanted to create a WWII impression to inject some gravitas into a scene that really didn't have much (a theme that would replay throughout the movie's run). Whatever explanation they use, it still ended up looking stupid as hell.
