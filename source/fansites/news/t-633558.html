I didn't report the Arena Matchmaking System FAQ (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/11/14910422788-arena-matchmaking-system-faq.html) on the front page yesterday, but Kalgan made a very interesting reply here. There's still plenty of room for some items to be pretty hard to get without causing most players to be left out of good gear. As I mentioned earlier, with the start of the next season we expect new teams and personal ratings to start much lower than they have before (not 1500). That way, everyone's experience is to climb toward the rating they're successfully playing at. So, knowing that, here's what our current thinking is on rating requirements for season 6.

Disclaimer: all of this is subject to change!

Other notes/caveats:
Items with no rating listed would have no rating requirement
It won't be quite as easy to get deadly items via emblems of conquest as hateful items currently are via emblems of valor
The new Archavon boss (a new boss located in another wing of the Vault of Archavon), will have slightly different drops than the current Archavon (he can drop Furious Gloves/Legs, or any possible random honor item in addition to tier 8 pve set items similar to Archavon's t7 pve set drops, but cannot drop the Furious Chest piece)
Honor/Arena costs aren't listed but Hateful costs would be similar to current Savage, Deadly costs similar to current Hateful, and Furious costs would be similar to current Deadly
You might note that the Furious weapon is still at a high requirement, 2k, the thinking being that most players are encouraged to get their weapon upgrade through pve, although hardcore arena competitors have an outlet to get an equivalent weapon through arenas (if you're hardcore enough for 2k, you can forego raiding for a weapon)
Additional note, "deadly trinket" refers to the current battlemaster's trinkets, "furious trinket" refers to an upgraded medallion of the alliance/horde

If new teams will start from 1200, won't those people be @ 900 then?


No, the rating you see on your char sheet is only used for allocating of arena points and determining the overall rankings. That number does not seem to be used at all in the matchmaking system. At all times your team rating will tend to move closer to the hidden rating.

So if everyone started at 1500 then teams who should be at 1200 will gain little and lose a lot in games until they end up around the 1200 point. If they started at 900 then they will gain a lot and lose very little until they end up around the 1200 mark. The hidden rating in both cases would progress in exactly the same manner and as such matchmaking would work exactly as it does when you start at 1500.

So presumably now when you make a new team your team rating and hidden rating both start at 1500. They could change it so that your team rating started at 0 and your hidden rating started at 1500 and from a matchmaking perspective things will work exactly as they do now. The only difference would be that a 1200 team would gain a lot of rating until they hit 1200 as opposed to now where they lose a lot of rating until they drop to 1200.

One potential benefit however from starting teams at low ratings and having everyone gain is that it actually encourages even poor players to play more arena. Right now if you are a 1200 team the best thing you can do is make a new team and lose 10 games each week (assuming you just want arena points). You will drop to about 1400 rating but still be doing better than if you keep playing until your rating adjusts to your skill. If they change that however and start all ratings at 0 (or 500 or 900 or whatever) and your rating will be rising until you reach your skill level then that actually changes the system and low skilled teams are better off if they keep playing arena until their rating stabilizes around 1200 or wherever it belongs.

In effect this solves the biggest problem with the current arena system. 1500 is the rating intended to represent an average level of skill. Presumably the average player is supposed to gain some rewards. However when everyone starts at that points the rewards have to be placed at 1600+ ratings, in effect the rewards are only available at a level that indicates an above average skill level to accomodate the fact that even really bad players can reset themselves to the average point. By making everyone start low and work up then average is a point you actually have to get to, presumably 50% of teams should make 1500 or higher but by having them start lower they can actually put rewards at that level without worrying that everyone can get them.

Lastly, the hidden ratings gives one other benefit to Blizzard that nobody has mentioned. Since the rating is hidden they can change it in the background without any of us noticing anything. What this means is that they can re-normalize the hidden ratings at any point they want. So for example, as part of the weekly maintainance they can work out the average hidden rating and if it is 1450 on a week then they can just increase everyones hidden ratings by about 50 points each and the team ratings will adjust to match that level next week, similarly they can use this to adjust the average rating downwards if the average hidden rating ended up at 1500 or so. In effect this insulates the game from most of the "but if all the poor players stop playing it will break arena" issues that people bring up. It gives them a knob to tweak if they ever want to trend team ratings either up or down overall.
