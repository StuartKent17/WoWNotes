Patch 6.1 - Twitter Integration
The implementation is really slick and out of the way. If you're using Twitter it helps you share things with an in-game UI you can choose to bring up. If you don't use Twitter just don't attach a Twitter account and I don't think you'll see anything related to it. The goal isn't to start socializing everything, it's really just to make it more convenient for people that already are sharing their gameplay. If you know anything about our UI and philosophy on simplicity I think you'll have a good idea of how subdued the implementation is, but it could get fun as Addons hook in and interact with it.

Can you supply a screenshot of the feature (in what I assume is a pop-up window) and a screenshot of the UI WITHOUT the pop-up. Like, at a time where it won't be triggered.
I cannot as I am at home and don't have access to an internal test client. To answer what I think is your question though, the ways you bring up the UI are by typing /twitter (I believe that's correct) or by clicking a small "w)))" icon that's next to existing UI elements. There's no minimap button or popup telling you to Tweet about things.

And that is why I won't be using this feature at all. Having Addons 'hook in' on a feature that is tied to my twitter account is not one I would trust. Too much chance of Addons starting to spam up my twitter feed with their own 'subtle' or not so subtle advertising.
An addon cannot tweet something for you; you have to hit the button.

World of Warcraft 10th Anniversary Ending
The 10th anniversary celebration (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/topic/355646-anniversary-event-extension/) is coming to an end soon. You have until Tuesday, January 13, 2015 at 10 a.m. PST / Tuesday, January 13, 2015 at 10:00 a.m. CET to get anything you may have missed.

If you logged in at all during the event, the Molten Corgi (http://www.wowdb.com/npcs/84915-molten-corgi) would have arrived in your mail. A successful run of the 40 player Raid Finder version of Molten Core rewarded a nice Core Hound (http://www.wowdb.com/items/115484-core-hound-chain) mount. The Hatespark the Tiny (http://www.wowdb.com/items/118574-hatespark-the-tiny) pet had a 10% drop rate and the Flames of Ragnaros (http://www.wowdb.com/items/118572-flames-of-ragnaros) weapon enchant transmog had a 2% drop rate (http://www.wowdb.com/npcs/11502-ragnaros?drops-item-page=2&drops-item-subtab=18#related:drops-item). Anyone that killed Ragnaros also got a spec appropriate item level 640 helm (http://www.wowdb.com/items/armor?filter-icon=inv_crown_02&filter-ilvl-min=640&filter-ilvl-max=640&filter-source=8&filter-slot=2).

Master Plan Addon Updated
The incredibly useful Master Plan addon (http://www.curse.com/addons/wow/master-plan) has been significantly updated since the last time we looked at it (http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/4533-Master-Plan-and-Garrison-Mission-Manager-Addons-Blue-Tweets-DLC-466). Some of the new features include:


More compact layout
Auto suggesting groups to maximize experience, garrison resources, and chance for success
A list of the missions everyone is interested in, such as Highmaul missions, high garrison resource missions, legendary quest item missions, and more. The Blackrock Foundry missions are coming soon (http://wow.curseforge.com/addons/master-plan/tickets/229-add-blackrock-foundry-missions-to-moi-tab/).
Auto completion of missions with a nice summary screen
Easier upgrading of followers with an upgrade button on each follower's page
