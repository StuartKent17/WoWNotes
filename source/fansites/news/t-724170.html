You've clearly never done a full hardware/software upgrade on a server cluster. You can't do this stuff in hour or two dribs and drabs across a week or two. You take it all down, do the upgrades and test. If you've got the brains of a fruit fly, you leave yourself wiggle room for the inevitable problems. You fix the inevitable problems and test some more. Once you're confident that everything works you bring it all back online. Depending on what they're doing it may be that upgrade itself will take a long time. For instance, if they're migrating the main databases to new hardware you're talking about probably terabytes of data that will have move across the network.

There's all know of redundancy, reliability, and such you can build into a computing cluster, but for some things they just have to come down for a bit. When reliability is the only standard by which you're judged there are ways to perform such upgrades "real time", but they would be prohibitively expensive for the numbers of servers Blizz has running this game, and frankly it's not anyone's life or death whether the servers are unavailable for a day.
