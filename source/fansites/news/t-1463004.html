Upon closer inspection, I can see the differences in the faces. It's mostly through the shading on the texture to simulate differences in facial structure (more pronounced bone structure, a heavier brow, etc). However, the differences just aren't going to read well unless you zoom your camera right up a character's nose. They're going to need to really punch it up if they want to avoid "sameface" complaints. A major stumbling block there (especially with the orc skins) is that there's not much complexity to the colors, giving them a "modeling clay" sort of look. One thing I've learned from Face Off is that you lose a lot of detail with a paintjob that's too simple for your sculpt. Enhancing the shadows/highlights on those faces would go a long way, especially if the colors were a little more nuanced (different complexions for different faces, a little more color blending, etc). One thing that would help (and a suggestion I will give if I ever have the good fortune to be invited to the beta someday) is to add an "other facial features" slider to some of the races, similar to the Goblin ear/nose/chin options. Things that slightly alter the physical sculpt of the face. That might be more difficult with the newer more expressive faces, but it would help add a lot of character to them. The more outlandish races already have that of course (Tauren horns, Troll tusks, Draenei horns/squidbeards, undead "attachments"), and those are the primary way you can tell one character of those races from another. Aside from hairstyle and beard/jewelry, which a number of races have in addition to the extra features, a good example for orcs would be things like mismatched tusks, different ears, scars and tattoos, noses, war paint, etc.

I feel like this won't be as much of a problem with the more "human-like" races, since the complexion and shading palette is more well established for human skin and thus differences in those aspects will be more apparent, but I see all of the above as potential issues for the races with weird skin tones and/or facial anatomy, including Draenei and perhaps Trolls and Night Elves.
But, just adding scars/tattoos/warpaint to the existing faces, as well as punching up the shading and improving the color complexity, would do a lot to differentiate one orc from another.
