Cataclysm Badge and PvP Point Changes Update
As an update to the original announcement, there will be two tiers of PvP weapons in Cataclysm. The lower tier weapons will correspond in quality to items found in the latest raid tier on normal difficulty and cost only Conquest Points. The higher tier weapons will correspond in quality to items found in the latest raid tier on Heroic difficulty and will have a minimum personal rating requirement in addition to costing Conquest Points. All of the highest-stat PvP armor, including shoulder and head pieces, will cost only Conquest Points and have no rating requirements. We have updated our original announcement text to reflect this clarification.

If those examples were hard to comprehend, ill try with something different. In Counter Strike the wining team gets more resources and thus gaining better weapons faster. At some point both teams will be with same resources but by that time the team that got the first win or the first few wins will end in first place for that round.

The problem with PvP in WoW arenas has never been one of resource acquisition. If all gear were available to everyone who ground out enough points, then yes, the first team to grind out enough points would have an advantage for a time, but that advantage would go away the second another team got the same number of points, and there would be nothing that having the best gear could do to prevent that.

By the end of the season, the most skilled team (modulo comps, latency, hardware, and other environmental issues) would be on top, and that would continue right down to the bottom with a few exceptions where teams formed too late to progress to their natural spot in the rankings.

So the problem is not the items, but the fact that there is a feedback function. You get a certain rating (not points, but rating, mind you) and you now have access to better weapons. Now you have the "high ground" and can defend your position more effectively than those with a lower ranking can attack because they don't have the higher rating gear.

If the other team is much better than you, they will win because the difference made by the gear isn't sufficient to allow you to win, but if they're just a bit better than you, then your gear can make up the difference and you appear to be better than them, even though you are not. In fact, it's likely true that if you measured all teams as a number (larger number = more skill) and laid them out in order or arena rating for last season, I'm willing to bet it would look a bit like this:

1 2 4 3 5 6 8 7 9 13 14 15 10 11 12 16 17 18 19 20

You see where there are numbers out of sequence? 1, 2, 19 and 20 are all where you expect them to be, but 13, 14, 15 come before 10, 11, 12. Why? Because between those two is one of the plateaus created by gear restrictions and the relatively small difference in skill between 10 and 15 can be overcome by the restrictions on 10, 11 and 12 getting the gear that 13, 14 and 15 happened to get first.

One of the arguments against this is that 13, 14 and 15 will move up in rankings, allowing you to progress, but in reality they've found their spot, and they're not going to move up unless someone above them stops playing. Until that happens, you're stuck breathing their exhaust.

Now, the new setup where only weapons of the max tier will be restricted might solve this because the advantage created by gear discrepancies might not be large enough to overcome semi-random factors like latency and just a bad day for one team or the other. It will entirely depend on the ilvls and itemization budgets of those levels.
