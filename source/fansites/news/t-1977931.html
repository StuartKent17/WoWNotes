Computers - Setup of the Month
Each month or so we will post a couple of hardware setups for those of you who are thinking of upgrading their computer!

This month
The current SSD prices (http://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2016/april/ssdgraph.gif) are fairly flat right now, with most SSDs under $0.50 per gigabyte when on sale. New GPUs may be coming later this year.

Depending on how much you want to overclock, you may need to spend a bit more for a better CPU cooler like the NH-D15 (http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00L7UZMAK?ie=UTF8&tag=mmochamp-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=390957&creativeASIN=B00L7UZMAK).

Unless you are doing a lot of video rendering or streaming, you do not need the 6700K and should spend that money elsewhere.

Make sure to keep your AMD (http://sites.amd.com/us/game/downloads/Pages/downloads.aspx) and Nvidia (http://www.nvidia.com/Download/index.aspx?lang=en-us) drivers up to date.

Don't forget to visit the Computer Forum (http://www.mmo-champion.com/forums/290-Computer) for any extra questions!

I just want to say that these builds are pretty bad, and that you can get something much better for the same price (if not less)
as an example, I'll go through all the faults of the Puppy build

Case: No problems here apart from no usb 3.0 ports, though I suggest the fractal design core 1100 (smoother look, has usb 3.0 ports, same price)

Power Supply: Admittedly not a bad choice, but it is non modular - horrible for cable management and if you care about aesthetics at all you wouldn't want the ketchup and mustard colored cables that come with this unit

CPU: The biggest fault in this entire build, FX processors are very outdated and use a dead socket, have high power usage and get very hot, and you will have no upgrade route - You might see 3.5 ghz and 6 cores and go "whoa!" but any new i3 will outperform it by quite a bit because of ipc and per-core performance (the i3 being a dual core doesn't matter - it's hyperthreaded and will run everything like a quad core would)

Heatsink: No complaints here, good value

Motherboard: Good quality motherboard (though using dead socket) But, You definitely do not need a motherboard that costs the same as your CPU, I understand it is for overclocking but at this pricerange you shouldn't be thinking about that

Memory: Nothing to say here, ram is ram and 8gb is enough for everything

Graphics card: Not a bad card, but for the same price you can grab a GTX 950 which performs better (not by a noticeable amount, but more is better)

Hard drive: while WD Black drives perform better than other 7200rpm harddrives, the extra $30 is not worth it if you are on a budget this low, a better choice would be a WD Blue or a Seagate Barracuda to save money and spend the extra $30 on another part

DVD: Nothing wrong with it but I don't know why you would want an optical drive, windows can be installed off of a usb stick


I highly recommend pcpartpicker (website) for anything to do with building a pc/pc components.

my personal suggestion for Puppy:

Case: Corsair carbide 88r - $50

Power supply: Seasonic M12II EVO 620w - $70

CPU: Intel core i3 6100 - $112

Heatsink: none (the i3 comes with one) - $0

Motherboard: Gigabyte GA-H110M-A - $54

Memory: Kingston HyperX Fury Black 8GB - $39

Graphics card: PowerColor Radeon R9 380 4GB PCS+ - $209

Hard drive: Seagate Barracuda 1TB 7200RPM - $44

DVD: None (install windows from a usb stick)

Total: $578

Basically, much better performance for the same price
