Druids changes in latest PTR build
It seems that Remove Curse is now usable in Tree of Life form for Druids in the latest PTR build.

Mages : Ice Block trainable
Ice block will be trainable in the future, but the change might not be included in the 2.3 patch. Also, the change made to hypothermia in the latest PTR Build is confirmed. Mana issues in long fight will also be looked at.
It's a deliberate change. We initially felt like it was too quick at 30s and decided to test it out at 45s, but after playing with it for a while we felt like it opened mages to focus fire a little too much so we decided to keep it at 30s. It's worth pointing out that making changes like this and later un-doing them is not at all uncommon, it's just most of you generally don't see that process in action since it often happens before the patch even gets to the PTR.

Since I'm here, I'll also make a small pre-emptive strike against the incoming "what about other mage concerns!" replies I'm about to get and say the following.

Two things we'll be making improvements to in the near future (although these aren't likely to make it in on time for 2.3) are having iceblock be trainable (to make sure all mages have it as a tool for pve encounters and to open up other trees as being more viable options for pvp), and to-be-finalized improvements to mana issues in longer fights.

We do plan to buff mana gems, although I don't see that as a "fix", just an improvement that needs to be made regardless.


Hunters : Aimed shot cast time reduced

Well, it is not only class combos, but to a certain extent class/spec combos (specially in hybrids). As you have two similarly classes (melee dmg dealing) you have a rather specialized team - that in principle will fare well against teams composed by classes that are more vulnerable to melee and badly against balanced and very badly against counter teams (imagine a dual frost mage team). Think of it as a one trick pony and your counter team is also a one trick counter pony - that doesn't mean they are OP.

To the more general question, class combos are one "cardinal" point in team PVP. Even well balanced, pvp focused PVP games (GW) had "winning combos/skills" (IWAY, ...) that used particular team composition and skills to win. The winning strategies (and combos) differ along the time, in accordance to skill and game changes, but usually there are a few that prevail. That doesn't mean that if your team mimics the top one combo it will achieved the same results, as different compositions require different strategies (and according execution). Some of these strategies become so dominant that require a "fix" from the games designers as they resulted from "exploits" - unforeseen effects of class/skill/gear combination.
