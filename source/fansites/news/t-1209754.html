In 10 man, we got the same number of barrages as 25, the same debuff for soaking them, and they did the same amount of damage (I didn't believe that last bit until I researched it myself, I naturally thought 25s must take more damage from barrages, but they didn't). The difference was, you couldn't soak all of them in 10 man because you would stack your debuff too high because it would never fall off because you had less soakers. Thus, the meta turned out to be vastly different in 10 man. Afflic locks, boomkins, spriests, fire mages were practically required for progression kills on this fight because you HAD to burn the drakes before too many barrages went out - whereas on 25 the tank debuffs were more of a concern so melee adds got more attention, and the fight was more balanced.

In Hagara, we had less totems, yes. But it was still impossible to chain all of them at once, as you had to use two ppl between each totem, unless you wanted to put healers in the mix and outrange people pretty badly. Also, consider how hard it was to get nature resist across that whole platform in 10 man. Consider what we had to do in our 10 man on H-Hagara progression, pre-nerf: We had to chain one whole chain just with pets (my hunter pet, our warlock pet) which meant intense pet micro to get them in the proper spots while we were in different spots. I had to cover my side of the platform with aspect of nature while our shaman had to drop nature resist on the other side (a totem in the middle wouldn't reach the people on the edges), and on the opposite side where we didn't have pets to chain, we had to have two high mobility classes on the first chain just so they could race over to chain the other totem (in our case, a rogue and a warrior using leap). Keep in mind, we had to 1 tank this fight, and have our resto druid swap to bear and taunt during assaults. In fact, a lot of progression fights in 10 man usually end up as 1 tank/2 heals, which is infinitely harder than what the stated design goals of 2 tanks/3 heals are (which only ends up as feasibly once people have been farming gear for awhile).

H-Ultra is designed for 3 healers on 10 man - yet no progression guild in 10 man even contemplated using 3 heals. It would have been more impossible than the 25 enrage, during progression at least. With 2 heals, because the buffs were so OP with certain classes, the enrage on H10 Ultra seemed (and was) much easier than 25.

H-Madness, during progression, mainly saw a mix of 1 tank fights with DPS cheesing an impale (vanish, feign, dispersion), or 1 1/2 tank fights with a cat soaking the first impale as bear and then DPSing the rest of the fight (Edit: or, 2 tanks with 2 heals). With 2 tanks and 3 heals, again, it would have been impossible.
