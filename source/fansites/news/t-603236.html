Season 3 Delayed
Postponed to November 27th. This also include new Vindicator honor items, season 1 items buyable with honor points, and of course the season 3 items themselves.

Everyone is preparing for the start of the third arena season and we’re excited to get new items, rewards, and fresh competition going for everyone participating in the arenas.

As we approach the previously announced start date we’ve had growing concerns of what the new season requires of us to actually launch, and its proximity to the Thanksgiving holiday. Everyone wants the launch go as smoothly as possible, and in order to have resources available to tend to any issues as soon as they may spring up, we’ve decided to delay the Season 3 start date to November 27. One week after the previously announced date.

This allows us to have the quickest response time to any issues should they occur, and allows everyone to enjoy the holiday and the days leading up to it. While making everyone wait another week obviously isn’t the optimal outcome, in the end we decided it would be much worse to start Season 3 so close to the holiday and potentially make players wait longer until a problem can be resolved. Ultimately it allows us to provide the best possible service for everyone as we launch the new season.

Funny how negative all you lot seem about the day. I for one had a huge amount of fun playing wow today. I logged on still specced Arms from doing my 10 arena fights yesterday, hopped into a quick AB and got an extra 400 honor and 25g for my efforts, came out, quick respec to prot so we could do a quick run to ZA before Karazhan, its a new guild, we are all fairly poorly geared and haven't even made a run at Gruul yet but we went to ZA just for fun. The instance looks great, the bear models are truly fantastic (I really want that mount). We got bear boss to 5% on our first attempt, then wiped twice more before we had to adjourn for Karazhan.

The badges make a great change in Karazhan, it means that everybody gets some reward every time you kill a boss and certainly adds a better sense of achievement even for bosses you have seen downed lots of times before. We cleared front half of Kara in our 2 hours (we keep raids fairly short). After that was off to Heroic Ramparts. Ramparts proved to be an object lesson in the vagaries of pugging. At first the group looked great, 2 rogues both well geared, well geared mage, full epic resto shammie and prot warrior me, looked like an easy peasy run but after one of the rogues managed to wipe us twice the epic shammie quits the group in a huff, the rogue who wiped us goes at the same time and the search for replacements begins. About half an hour and 7 replacement group members later all 4 of the original members are gone and we have a new group, me, a cloth wearing holy paladin, a shadow priest with a couple of kara items, an enhancement shammie and a rogue, the rogue, shaman and paladin all bought their honored reputation keys just before we started. In short it looked like a disaster waiting to happen but turned out to be one of the fastest, cleanest ramparts runs I have ever seen. A group of players who despite not having the best of gear just have fun with their classes and know the subtle nuances of play, the rogue using blind to perfection, a shadow priest who knows exactly when and how to use mind control, an enhancement shaman who knows exactly when to chip in with a heal and a paladin who calmly keeps us all afloat. It is groups like this that keep me PUGging, yeah you occasionally get stuck with a bunch of morons (and as proven today, gear is no indicator of who is and isn't a moron) but sometimes you get groups that just shouldn't work but end up working to perfection all because the players don't take the game too seriously, play for fun instead of accomplishment and get genuine enjoyment out of doing the little things as well as they can.

After collecting my 2 bonus badge of honor and an extra 25g for my efforts it was time to make my new engineering mount, I crafted the basic version and what can I say, it is a thing of beauty. The transition from flying to landed mode, the way it constantly stutters and pops even while you are landed, such a wonderful creation. Unfortunately they seem to have created a shortage of primal fire on the AH so I wasn't able to get it upgraded to epic yet but its just something else to look forward to.

Lastly, it was off to do my daily quests and the last wonderful experience of the day, my prot warrior can actually grind fairly quickly and efficiently now thanks to the changes to devastate and whirlwind. Considering that both my dual wield weapons are fast daggers (1.5 and 1.8 speed) I can only expect it to get even better as I replace them with slower more suitable alternatives. In fact I suspect that with a couple of decent 2.6 speed weapons the damage would be decent enough for some BG PvP, I know I won't be doing top end damage but it will be decent enough to not be frustrating, plus of late I have been playing with the idea of turtling by equipping a shield and going into defensive stance when outnumbered at objectives and am finding it a fun and effective way of having an impact. In AB for example if you can stall people for even 5 or 10 seconds from a flag capture it can buy enough time for another round of graveyard respawns.

In short, Blizzard have added a lot of fun improvements to the game this patch and I think that if so many of you can't get anything positive out of today and what it has done for the game then you must not really be enjoying your time playing WoW anymore and may need to take a step back and rethink your reasons for playing.
