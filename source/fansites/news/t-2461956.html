Ok, look, this is Blizz trying to fix something they can't. And that is - make top guilds unable to benefit from what they consider 'unfair advantages'.

It's not necessarily that they're unfair, it's just that developers design raids and systems, expecting them to be played in a certain way.

For example:
- you're supposed to beat a boss, get some gear drops, get a minor improvement for your raid, advance to next boss
- do this for the reset until you can't kill bosses anymore
- come next week, start all over - repeat as many times as required till your raid can beat the boss
- accumulate titan residuum over a period of time and get upgrades slowly
This is how your average guild does things.
And then you have your top guilds who do things a bit differently. For example:
- they make split runs so they funnel gear to their "main" players. The fact that the ones who give their gear don't get anything is a non issue; once theykill the boss, those who helped will get their kill (also, those who helped might be alts, so, again, non issue)
- this way, having the best upgrades in the first week means you can kill the boss a whole lot faster than it was designed
- top guilds need to do things fast - having to do the things they do requires a lot if investment on their part: time, money, everything; which means the boss really has to go down fast fast; a top guild doesn't want systems where you need to play long to grind (ex: legendaries, artifact power, azerite), because their purpose is not to play for long. They are also disadvantaged by RNG systems (again, legendaries and titanforges).

So all in all, this isn't meant to ruin things for random guilds. It's meant to ruin things for top guilds. But those will find their ways anyway - until it's getting so cumbersome that they decide it's better to quit.
