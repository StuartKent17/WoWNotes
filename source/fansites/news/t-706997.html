Holy crap, that's a lot of announcements for today.
Beginning in just a few days, players who participate in the Recruit-A-Friend program will receive a whole new reward for showing a friend the ropes in Azeroth: the X-53 Touring Rocket, a two-seater flying mount that automatically increases its speed as your mount skill improves (up to 310% speed if you already have a 310% mount). If you've already claimed a Zhevra (or claim one prior to the change), you’ll be able to hang on to it, of course. We’ll have more details on the new Recruit-A-Friend mount once it becomes available.

Are all players eligible to "Recruit-A-Friend"?
Players with a World of Warcraft account from the following countries are able to "Recruit-A-Friend": Austria, Finland, France, Germany, Italy, Holland, UK, Norway, Sweden, Greece, Ireland and Spain. Players from outside of these countries; players with trial accounts, or with full accounts with five referrals from the "Recruit-A-Friend" currently pending, are ineligible.

Blizzard Poster Aeus has already made a statement regarding the country issue 2 years ago:



"I can sincerely tell you, from an insider's point of view, that we try extremely hard to include as many countries as possible. When our hand is forced, this is generally due to legal restrictions in certain countries.

There is a way to circumvent this, but I may not be held responsible for any actions you do to your account!!!
You should ponder over this thought very carefully, as changing your country might lock your account and trying to convince the billing team that you are from Denmark, whereas your account says United Kingdom, might get you some headache.
The option is to change your address to any of the countries that are eligible for the RAF program.


2.) Wooohooo, I am from country XY, where I can use the RAF!! But....what do I have to do now?
Log into your Battle.Net account. Choose your WoW account and in case you have multiple WoW accounts tied to the Battle.net account choose the one, where you want the mount sent to.
On the right side you will see the "Recruit a friend" option, click it and follow the steps to send an invitation. Send it to yourself. If you have troubles sending it to the same email address you use for your main account use a free email service like hotmail etc.

Sign up for a trial account via the link you have sent to yourself - THIS IS MANDATORY in order for your main account to receive the mount. You then can also check the status of the RAF account via your main account.
Take the trial key provided in the email address and add it to your Battle.Net account. A new WoW copy will show up with the remaining days of the trial. If you want to check, your main account will now have a link to a "friend" who activated the 10-days trial (Status is yellow).

Go to the newly created Trial account. There you will be presented with the option to upgrade online, either by entering a fresh unused key of Vanilla WoW you got from a CD or by purchasing one directly online in the Blizzard shop.
When you have finished you will then have converted the trial account to a paid account.
This account is now of the same type as your main account.

Now the only thing you still have to do is to add 60 days of game time to this account. Note that in order to get the mount as soon as possible it might be better to use the game card option. Reason for this is when you use the credit card option that Blizzard will not charge it until the free 30 days, which are given to each new paid WoW account, are used up and even after that they will only charge one month (if you would choose the monthly subsciption), which means a 3 months wait time until you receive the mount.

This makes your main account eligible to receive the RAF mount.

30 Days Gamecard - 12,99€ (Blizzard Online Shop)
