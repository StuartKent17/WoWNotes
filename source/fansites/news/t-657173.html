WoWArmory.com now on Iphone
I'll make sure to write an extensive review of this wonderful tool as soon as Apple offers to send me a free iPhone.
Ever wanted to check your raid schedule, look up a piece of PvP gear, or concoct a new talent spec while on the go? The new World of Warcraft Mobile Armory app for iPhone and iPod touch lets you access the official Armory database from anywhere you have a mobile connection, and features much of the same functionality as the Armory website (www.wowarmory.com (http://www.wowarmory.com)), including:

* Search for and view any character, item, guild, or Arena team in World of Warcraft.
* Seamlessly import your own character, guild, and Arena team profiles.
* Access your in-game calendar of events.
* View achievement-point leaderboards.
* Plan out talent specs using the fully functional talent calculator.

The World of Warcraft Mobile Armory is available now as a free download from the App Store on iPhone and iPod touch or at www.itunes.com/appstore/ (http://www.itunes.com/appstore/).

For more information, visit www.wowarmory.com/iphone.xml (http://www.wowarmory.com/iphone.xml).

The fact that the iPhone/iTouch armory is faster than the browser variant is not all that surprising really when you think about it.

When you browse armory on your computer browser; the armory constantly has to send you detailed information on how the page should look like, and how to handle character information (how to display it), whilst finally receiving the actual character data of course. Theres also a lot more 'misc' stuff on each page as well. This is all done in Adobe Flash too, remember.

The iPhone app skips all of the above except for the actual character data - and that data is nothing more than plain text; very compact and easy to transfer.

A 'regular' armory page probably requires a few hundred kilobytes per page - an iPhone armory page requires perhaps 5-10 kb.

The fact that the client comes preloaded with all of the essentials, images, etc is also an important besides for being convenient and fast. Many carriers still charge based on the amount of data transfered - reducing the data transfered drastically increases the usability of the service.

Of course - this also means that the servers are less strained by each request, so they in turn are able to complete each request made by an iPhone much faster and the chance of getting stuck mid-loading is as such greatly reduced.
