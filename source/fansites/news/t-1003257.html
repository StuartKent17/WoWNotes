Well, you played Wotlk with a "viking themed" mmo exp, what's the difference?

I don't strictly agree with the standpoint of the person you quoted, but I think I can better articulate his (and people that share a similar viewpoint's) argument

Wotlk wasn't really 100% "viking themed..." obviously, we had the Vrykul, but they weren't the main focus of the expansion, nor was their architecture. Now, personally, I flipping love everything Nordic, but blizzard made a good move, as Northrend was much more... diverse... in its themes, both architectural and landscape-wise. We had the Taunka and Tuskarr, who's culture was based (loosely) on that of the Inuits/Native Americans, the Nordic-Inspired vrykul, the trippy, almost sci-fi blue dragonflight, the ancient, frost bound meso-american kingdom of the drakkari, the fanciful hanging Nerubian buildings, the steampunk-infused roman halls of the titans, and the stark, LotR-esque architecture of the scourge's most formidable battlements. And while all of these themes have numerous influences undoubtedly rooted in the real world, each one nonetheless had its own unique, distinguishing stylistic flare that truly tied them to their respective races and clearly established them as their own unique culture. Just because it was brought up, the Vrykul, for example, had their sharp, perching harpoon nests dotting the cliffsides and their (quite original) stone fortresses that separated them quite clearly from real life vikings.

Now, I'll be the first to admit that Pandaria is definitely one of the most beautiful environments in all of WoW. But the problem is... the Chinese architecture doesn't feel exclusively "Pandaren." And this isn't necessarily Blizzard's "fault," nor is it due to "laziness" on their part (I think I could safely say that the architecture of the Pandaren took a HELL of a lot of work.) Chinese architecture is already very fanciful and stylized. As creative as blizzard is, it's understandable that the threshold couldn't be pushed that much further without making it look completely cartoony. Moreover, this is the central theme of an expansion that already (well, it's only been a day) feels excluded from Warcraft's general "grand design" in terms of lore.
