1) Raiding has started over.
You are not continuing from Kil'Jaeden. You are starting over in the raiding progression. Every expansion resets the difficulty so that others that are leveling up and haven't seen the content don't have to backtrack through old and stale content just to be able to do something.

This is a minor and mostly understood point but it bears stating since many people come straight off of the micro that was M'uru and the CF that KJ could be at times and feel "let-down" by how easy some of this content is now.

2) Key time-sinks and cock-block mechanics have been removed.
In TBC, in order to advance to the true 25man content (SSC & TK), you had to squeeze 25 people through a 10 man, then 3 5mans before you could take them to the 2 25mans to finally get attuned. God help you if you're missing a shaman and you have another one on standby but he never did the SH timed run. It was a massive CF and many people rightly complained to blizzard about it. Blizzard rightfully responded by creating a straight path for the 25man guilds to tread - you can jump right in and start in on content without worrying about flagging 25 people through the most asinine hoops.

Time sinks are there because they take ... time. (Duh) When you remove them and set people loose on the content right away, content will get cleared much faster. The individual elements of TBC were beated incredibly quickly; karazhan was dead within a week and gruul (broken though he was) and mags were short to follow. What followed thereafter was not the next tier of raiding but the timesinks/cockblocks. No longer.

3) Naxx is the new Karazhan, not the new SSC/TK
This expands on point 1 a little further; Naxxramas is the introductory raid; the place where you build your raidforce and gear them up; teach them basics if they've never been or just take it easy. Karazhan fulfilled that same role in TBC and it was a phenomenal success in that regard.

Imagine then if you will if the 10/25 man system was in place for TBC - 25man Karazhan would have still consisted of a tank&spank attumen with more decursers; more tanks for moroes making him essentially an HKM fight; more assigned raidhealing for maiden, ect. Aran would have been fun for 25man - think of all the idiots that didn't move from doomfire ;). Point being that it still would have been easy and just as readily cleared by those who already know how to raid and have their raid synergy already established.

Naxxramas fulfills that role now; it's a place for taking recruits, for building your raid synergy and to generally get a good feel for raiding. If you come from a well-established guild, this will be easy; if not, now is the chance to build.

4) Naxx is already well-known by every hardcore raiding team
There is almost nothing blizzard could have done to make naxxramas challenging for the hardcore apart from tuning it in a fashion that would have locked out everyone except for the hardcore. All the encounters are already known; the work in figuring them out already done. It's now just a matter of executing, which is something that is the least of the hardcore's problem.

Furthermore, the naxx mechanics, while new and "revolutionary" for their times have been implemented many times over in other encounters. When people engaged patchwerk for the first time, many were confounded by the idea of a hateful strike and how it worked. Nowadays, patchwerk is simply explained as Gruul w/o shatters. Easy as hell for anyone to grasp. The same goes for most encounters.

The difficulty in half the encounters comes with its tuning and this is what made sunwell rather difficult. The only really technical fights were Kalecgos/Twins and KJ a distant 3rd. Brutallus & Felmyst were nothing but glorified tank&spanks with alternate elements (move up, move down, collect loot) and M'uru was all about raid micro. Tuning made that instance what it was; not mechanics. Set the tuning lower ala 3.0 and gg casual guilds clearing sunwell in 2 weeks.
