http://static.mmo-champion.com/mmoc/images/icons/24x24/deathknight.jpg Upcoming Death Knight Changes
The layout of the original post has been slightly changed to make it more readable
We have talked about making some of these changes and wanted to provide more details. You should be able to try these out yourselves on the PTR before they go live.

Our reason for most of these changes should be obvious, but some common themes are:
Making tanking still rely on cooldowns, but rely on them a little less.
Make Blood a more attractive spec, and particularly for tanking.
Chill out some of the defensive capabilities all DKs have in PvP.
Avoid having to use Death and Decay as an out-of-combat runic power generator.



General
All multi-rune abilities generate 15 runic power.
New runeforge rune -- Rune of the Stoneskin Gargoyle (two-handed only) now grants 25 Defense and 2% Stamina.
Many death knight glyphs have been changed. In many cases the negative consequences were removed.
New death knight sigils are now available, primarily from vendors, including a tanking-oriented sigil.

Blood
Rune Strike (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=56815) -- damage decreased from 200% to 150% but threat increased to 150% from 100%.
The healing of Blood Aura (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=50371), Blood Presence (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=48266) and Death Pact (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=48743) has been doubled.
Heart Strike (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=55262) – we overhauled this ability. It no longer has a haste debuff but will now be able to strike two targets like a cleave. It still hits for more than Blood Strike, but you can still use Blood Strike if e.g. you don’t want to break CC.
Bloody Strikes (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=49395) – the bonus damage from Pestilence has been moved here to help Blood AE slightly.
Vampiric Blood (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=55233) -- in addition to its current effects, also adds 20% health temporarily.
Will of the Necropolis (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=50150) -- now reduces the damage of any attack that takes the DK below 35% health by 5 /10/15% instead of boosting armor when wounded,
Dancing Rune Weapon (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=49028) – cost reduced from 60 to 40 runic power.

Frost
Icebound Fortitude (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=48792) – now reduces damage by 20% instead of 50%. The amount of damage reduced increases with bonus Defense (to about 35% for 540 Defense, but it can go higher). The stun immunity is intended to be its primary role in PvP.
Frost Presence (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=48263) -- bonus armor increased from 60 to 80% and magic damage reduction increased from 5 to 15%. We wanted to reduce the effectiveness of cooldowns but bring up base mitigation to reduce damage spikiness.
Horn of Winter (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=57623) -- now has no cost and grants 10 runic power in addition to its stat buff, but has a 30 sec cooldown.
Hungering Cold (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=49203) – cost reduced from 60 to 40 runic power.

Unholy
Anti-Magic Shell (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=48707) -- cooldown lowered to 45 sec from 60 sec.
Bone Shield (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=49222) -- mitigation reduced from 40% to 20%.
Corpse Explosion (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=51328) -- damage increased substantially, added 5 sec cooldown, and changed cost to 40 runic power.
Night of the Dead (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=55623) -- now grants 40/70% passive area spell avoidance to your pet in addition to its current effects.
Outbreak (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=55237) – this talent no longer receive bonus damage from Pestilence. The bonus from Plague Strike and Blood Boil has been increased slightly.
Pestilence (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=51429) -- no longer has a 10 sec cooldown.
Raise Dead (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=46584) -- now split into two spells: Raise Dead now raises a ghoul or pet ghoul (if talented). Raise Ally now raises a fallen party member (at no reagent cost).
Shadow of Death (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=49157) -- duration reduced from 45 seconds to 25 seconds.
Unholy Blight (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=51379) – cost reduced from 60 to 40 runic power.

If 540 defense gives you a ~35% damage reduction (up from 20%), then you're getting about 15% mitigation for 140 defense. This boils down to about 0.1% mitigation per defense. Defense enchants run in the 20ish rating range, meaning dropping a defense enchant means you lose around 0.3% damage reduction on a CD with ~15% uptime. That means a hit that would normally do 6500 damage w/ IBF up will now do 30 less damage. I really doubt IBF will be good reason to stack defense past 540.

Plus, by making the reduction offered by the abilities less spectacular it pushes them into being used even moreso as passive reduction than they are at present, basically doing the exact opposite of the intended effect.

In PvP the changes are a very distinct nerf. Yeah, DKs were relatively strong against melee (though some people who think ganking people while they quest is PvP really are overstating that strength). The problem is that the DK is the easiest class to kite for any half competent ranged DPS. Having a 35 (or 25) second cooldown on our only closing ability means that pretty much every ranged class can use their "get out of melee" ability more often than DKs can use their "get into melee" ability. Our snares are all dispellable with no way to increase chance to resist dispel on them. The damage reduction seemed to be how DKs were supposed to deal with kiters, they just have to suck up the damage for the 25 seconds until you have a chance to close again, now the only way to do that is to go to a presence where you lose your increased movement speed, a catch 22.

With these changes there is pretty much no way a DK can last 25 seconds to close distance again against any half competent ranged DPS which will make them a free HK for most of the classes in the game not to mention gutting them as an arena class. I expect DKs will struggle in arena though, single target DPS, no CC, a very long cooldown on the only range closing ability (though the most fun one it has to be said), dispellable snares and a dispellable combat system. Being good at shutting down holy paladins is hardly a viable niche. Melee burst DPS is a role much better done by both warriors and rogues.

On the other hand its still way too soon to judge I guess. What is good to see is that Blizzard are being quick to fix class issues so if it does show as a problem hopefully it will get sorted out then.
