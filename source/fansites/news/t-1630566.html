Warlords of Draenor - Garrisons: Profession Buildings
Many players have been asking how the small profession buildings work or if they have to build them recently, so today we are taking a look at the Alchemy Lab as an example. The full details are below, but the short version is:


If you build a profession building without the profession, you can craft most of the lower level things, such as weaker flasks and weaker armor and weapons.
If you build a profession building with the profession, you can build all the things for that profession, and you get extra materials that are crafted with a daily cooldown.
In the Alchemy example below, you cannot make the best flasks without having Alchemy, but you can make weaker flasks with the building.
In the Alchemy example below, you get Alchemical Catalyst (http://beta.wowdb.com/items/108996-alchemical-catalyst) (daily cooldown) from the daily cooldown as an Alchemist. You can also get some from Work Orders with the building. If you have both the profession and the building, you will get more per day than you would with just the profession. If you only have the building, you can still get some of the daily cooldown material through work orders.
You can purchase some recipes for gold and some for an item from a separate daily cooldown. These were sold by a vendor in the profession building (beta.wowdb.com/npcs/77363-mary-kearie#related:sells;_!) and vendors in Ashran (http://beta.wowdb.com/npcs/87542-joshua-alvarez#related:sells;_!) on beta.



Warlords of Draenor - Garrisons: Alchemy Lab
The Alchemy Lab (http://beta.wowdb.com/garrison/buildings/120-alchemy-lab) takes up a Small plot, which can also be used for profession buildings, a Salvage Yard (http://beta.wowdb.com/garrison/buildings/140-salvage-yard), and a Storehouse (http://beta.wowdb.com/garrison/buildings/143-storehouse). Once upgraded to Tier 3, your garrison will have three Small plots available.

Abilities
This building is good for producing Alchemy items without the profession or producing Alchemy items faster with the profession.


Level 1 - Allows the production of Alchemy items and work orders.
Level 2 - Allows followers with the Alchemy trait to work here, granting a unique bonus. Also allows up to 14 work orders at one time.
Level 3 - Allows up to 21 work orders at one time.



Level 1 Unlock
If you don't have Alchemy as a profession, you can wait until you upgrade your Garrison to Level 2 and all of the Level 1 blueprints will unlock.


Level 3 Unlock
The Level 3 version of this building is unlocked by completing the Working More Orders (http://www.wowdb.com/achievements/9406-working-more-orders) achievement, which requires you to complete 250 work orders in your garrison.


Work Orders
The Alchemy Lab allows you to turn in 5 x Frostweed (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109124-frostweed) for a Alchemical Catalyst (http://www.wowdb.com/items/108996-alchemical-catalyst).

For a bonus, you can assign a follower with the Alchemy (http://www.wowdb.com/garrison/abilities/54-alchemy) ability to your building. The follower will also give you a daily quest every day that gives you a free Alchemy Experiment (http://www.wowdb.com/items/118759-alchemy-experiment), which contains some extra potions.


Profession Leveling
If you have the Alchemy profession, you will loot Mysterious Flask (http://www.wowdb.com/items/113103-mysterious-flask) from something in Draenor. Doing so will take you through a short quest line to Avenge and Reclaim (http://www.wowdb.com/quests/35106-avenge-and-reclaim), which rewards the scroll (http://www.wowdb.com/items/109558-a-treatise-on-the-alchemy-of-draenor) to increase your max alchemy level and the blueprints (http://www.wowdb.com/items/111812-garrison-blueprint-alchemy-lab-level-1) for the level 1 building.

If your Alchemy isn't capped at 600 yet, be on the lookout for profession fragments (http://beta.wowdb.com/items/misc/other?filter-ilvl-min=510&filter-ilvl-max=510&filter-quality=6). These drop from mobs in Draenor and can either be combined with some materials or turned in to NPCs at your garrison for skillups.


Daily Cooldown
One important thing you can craft (http://beta.wowdb.com/spells/175880-secrets-of-draenor-alchemy) is a Secret of Draenor Alchemy (http://beta.wowdb.com/items/118700-secret-of-draenor-alchemy), which you can trade for different Alchemy recipes (beta.wowdb.com/npcs/87542-joshua-alvarez#related:sells;_!) in your garrison or at vendors in Ashran (http://beta.wowdb.com/npcs?filter-search=Alchemy+Recipes).

The other daily cooldown is the Alchemical Catalyst (http://beta.wowdb.com/spells/156587-alchemical-catalyst). Alchemical Catalyst (http://beta.wowdb.com/items/108996-alchemical-catalyst) are used for Greater Flasks and the Alchemy trinket.

This spell will produce 4 to 10 Alchemical Catalyst (http://beta.wowdb.com/items/108996-alchemical-catalyst) depending on your skill level. The closer you get to 700, the more you will produce. Upon reaching 700, you will learn Alchemical Catalyst (http://beta.wowdb.com/spells/168042-alchemical-catalyst), which changes from a single daily cooldown to a daily cooldown that can accumulate up to three charges, allowing you to miss a few days and still make all of your cooldowns.

Keep in mind that work orders will help to produce more Alchemical Catalyst (http://beta.wowdb.com/items/108996-alchemical-catalyst) and if you need even more you can purchase them for 10 x Primal Spirit (http://beta.wowdb.com/items/120945-primal-spirit) each.

Apexis Crystals
Apexis Crystals (http://www.wowdb.com/currencies/823-apexis-crystal) are a new currency added in Warlords of Draenor. They are used for the legendary quest line, can be used for (http://www.wowdb.com/quests/36055-sealing-fate-apexis-crystals) Seal of Tempered Fate (http://www.wowdb.com/currencies/994-seal-of-tempered-fate), and are used to purchase armor, mounts, pets, transmog gear, and other items.


Obtaining Apexis Crystals
Most of your Apexis Crystals will come from completing a daily quest that requires killing mobs in the level 100 part of each of the Draenor zones. Each day in your garrison's Town Hall (http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/1462-Warlords-of-Draenor-Garrisons-Town-Hall) you will be offered a choice between two missions (http://beta.wowdb.com/quests?filter-currency=-1&filter-search=assault&filter-reqlvl-min=100), with solo missions rewarding 800 Apexis Crystal (http://www.wowdb.com/currencies/823-apexis-crystal), group missions rewarding 1000 Apexis Crystal (http://www.wowdb.com/currencies/823-apexis-crystal), and an Ashran mission (http://beta.wowdb.com/quests/36684-battle-in-ashran) rewarding 1,000 Apexis Crystals.

When you arrive at the area, you must fill a progress bar to 100% (http://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2014/october/objectives.jpg) by killing mobs and interacting with objects in the area. Depending on the quest, mobs may give ~1 percent each while objects will give you ~2-3 percent each. The mobs you kill in this area will sometimes drop a few Apexis Crystals each, increasing the total earned from this quest.


http://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2014/october/thumb/dailyQuestTable.jpg (http://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2014/october/dailyQuestTable.jpg)

Another reliable source of Apexis Crystals are the work orders done in your garrison. When picking them up, there are often some Apexis Crystals along with the regular work order output.

If you have an Inn / Tavern (http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/1434-Warlords-of-Draenor-Garrisons-Inn-Tavern) in your garrison, you can pick up a daily dungeon quest that has a chance of giving you a small amount of Apexis Crystals.

There are a few other less reliable sources, starting with The Liberation of Shattrath (http://www.wowdb.com/garrison/missions/336-the-liberation-of-shattrath) mission that rewards 1,000 Apexis Crystals. You can also defend your garrison from invasion (http://beta.wowdb.com/quests?filter-weekly=1&filter-search=victory&filter-reqlvl-min=100) every week and obtain some Apexis Crystals depending on how well you did. Apexis Crystals can also drop from rare spawns / elites, which usually have a small number of crystals on them.


Bonus Rolls
In Warlords of Draenor there are several ways to earn bonus rolls (Seal of Tempered Fate (http://www.wowdb.com/currencies/994-seal-of-tempered-fate)). If you have excess Apexis Crystals you can spend them on seals, with the cost doubling for each turn in per week.


Sealing Fate: Apexis Crystals (http://beta.wowdb.com/quests/36055-sealing-fate-apexis-crystals) - 1,000 x Apexis Crystal (http://www.wowdb.com/currencies/823-apexis-crystal)
Sealing Fate: Heap of Apexis Crystals (http://beta.wowdb.com/quests/37452-sealing-fate-heap-of-apexis-crystals) - 2,000 x Apexis Crystal (http://www.wowdb.com/currencies/823-apexis-crystal)
Sealing Fate: Mountain of Apexis Crystals (http://beta.wowdb.com/quests/37453-sealing-fate-mountain-of-apexis-crystals) - 4,000 x Apexis Crystal (http://www.wowdb.com/currencies/823-apexis-crystal)



Legendary Quest Line
You will need to collect 4,986 Apexis Crystals for part of the legendary quest line that comes after clearing a few heroic dungeons: Draenor's Secret Power (http://www.wowdb.com/quests/36158-draenors-secret-power).


Armor
Apexis Crystals can also purchase armor that can be upgraded to item level 655. There are three tiers of each item, starting at item level 630, then going to 645, and finally 655.


Legs - 5,000 + 11,500 + 20,000 = 36,500 Apexis Crystals for item level 655 legs.
Head - 5,000 + 11,500 + 20,000 = 36,500 Apexis Crystals for item level 655 head.
Hands - 4,000 + 10,500 + 17,000 = 31,500 Apexis Crystals for item level 655 hands.
Waist - 4,000 + 10,500 + 17,000 = 31,500 Apexis Crystals for item level 655 waist.
Wrist - 3,000 + 9,000 + 14,500 = 26,500 Apexis Crystals for item level 655 wrist.
Cloak - 3,000 + 9,000 + 14,500 = 26,500 Apexis Crystals for item level 655 cloak.
