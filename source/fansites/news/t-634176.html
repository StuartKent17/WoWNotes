Love is in the Air! Be careful because it seems that love isn't around for long in World of Warcraft: this event will start on February 11 at 06:00AM and end on February 16 at 06:00AM. (Server time)

The Fool for Love (http://www.wowhead.com/?achievement=1693) meta-achievement is obviously a part of the What A Long, Strange Trip It's Been (http://www.wowhead.com/?achievement=2145) achievement to get the Violet Proto-Drake (http://static.mmo-champion.com/mmoc/images/news/2008/september/8970/mount_violetprotodrake.jpg) (310% Speed Flying Mount).

Most of these achievements are easily completed because they rely heavily on items found in Gifts of Adorations (http://www.wowhead.com/?search=gift+of+adoration) and Pledge of Adoration (http://www.wowhead.com/?search=pledge+of+adoration). You can easily get them and you can also buy them from other players at the Auction House if you're running short on time.

Heartmender (http://www.wowhead.com/?achievement=260)
Use the Unbestowed Friendship Bracelets (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=22259) on 20 players affected by the Heartbroken (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=26898) debuff. You can find the bracelets inside the Gift of Adorations (http://www.wowhead.com/?search=gift+of+adoration) you get when you offer a Love Token (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=21815) to a guard in capital cities.

http://static.wowhead.com/images/alliance-icon.gifNation of Adoration (http://www.wowhead.com/?achievement=1697) / http://static.wowhead.com/images/horde-icon.gifNation of Adoration (http://www.wowhead.com/?achievement=1698)
Get a Gift of Adorations (http://www.wowhead.com/?search=gift+of+adoration) from each capital city.

Tokens (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=21815) are bought from Innkeepers during the event. Giving a token to a guard will affect you with the Adored (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=26680) debuff and will reward you with a Gift of Adorations (http://www.wowhead.com/?search=gift+of+adoration). This won't work all the time, you can also get the Heartbroken (http://www.wowhead.com/?spell=26898) debuff and in this case you just won't get anything. You can cancel the Heartbroken debuff with a Unbestowed Friendship Bracelets (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=22259) ... Which is the whole point of the achievement! Just make other players happy!

Flirt with Disaster (http://www.wowhead.com/?achievement=1279)
Drink enough alcohol to be completely smashed, buy a Perfume Bottle (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=21829) from the closest Innkeeper selling it, and use your Bouquet of Red Roses (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=22206) or Bouquet of Ebon Roses (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=44731) on either
http://static.wowhead.com/images/alliance-icon.gifSraaz (http://www.wowhead.com/?npc=9099) or http://static.wowhead.com/images/horde-icon.gifJeremiah Payson (http://www.wowhead.com/?npc=8403) depending on your faction.

Nearest and Dearest (http://www.wowhead.com/?achievement=1695)
Buy some Perfume (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=21829) or Cologne (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=21833) along with a few Love Tokens (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=21815) and complete the http://static.wowhead.com/images/alliance-icon.gifDangerous Love (http://www.wowhead.com/?quest=8903) or http://static.wowhead.com/images/horde-icon.gifDangerous Love (http://www.wowhead.com/?quest=8904) questline depending on your faction. They're simple delivery quests and you shouldn't have any problem with that.

Sweet Tooth (http://www.wowhead.com/?achievement=1702)
Eat a Buttermilk Delight (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=22236), Dark Desire (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=22237), Sweet Surprise (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=22239) and a Very Berry Cream (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=22238). All these items are found in the Box of Chocolate (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=21812) contained in Gifts of Adorations (http://www.wowhead.com/?search=gift+of+adoration) and Pledge of Adoration (http://www.wowhead.com/?search=pledge+of+adoration). If you plan to do the other achievements this one won't be a problem as you'll have plenty of them available.

My Love is Like a Red, Red Rose (http://www.wowhead.com/?achievement=1703)
Obtain a Bouquet of Red Roses (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=22206) or Bouquet of Ebon Roses (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=44731).
The Bouquet of Red Roses (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=22206) was found on Archivist Galford (http://www.wowhead.com/?npc=10811) (Stratholme), Illyanna Ravenoak (http://www.wowhead.com/?npc=11488) (Dire Maul), Princess Moira Bronzebeard (http://www.wowhead.com/?npc=8929) (Blackrock Depths) and Lorekeeper Polkelt (http://www.wowhead.com/?npc=10901) but is no longer obtainable
The Bouquet of Ebon Roses (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=44731) is found on Maiden of Grief (http://www.wowhead.com/?npc=27975) (Halls of Stone), Prince Keleseth (http://www.wowhead.com/?npc=23953) (Utgarde Keep) and Prince Taldaram (http://www.wowhead.com/?npc=29308).

Shafted! (http://www.wowhead.com/?achievement=1188)

Lonely? (http://www.wowhead.com/?achievement=1291)

This will require a Romantic Picnic Basket (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=34480), this is a rare drop from Gifts of Adorations (http://www.wowhead.com/?search=gift+of+adoration) and Pledges of Adoration (http://www.wowhead.com/?search=pledge+of+adoration). Handful of Petals (http://www.wowhead.com/?item=22218) are found in Gifts of Adorations (http://www.wowhead.com/?search=gift+of+adoration) and Pledges of Adoration (http://www.wowhead.com/?search=pledge+of+adoration).

2009 WoW Arena Tournament Announced
We’re pleased to announce that the 2009 World of Warcraft Arena Tournament is nearly here. Starting next week (February 17, 2009), players will be able to sign up to compete in this year’s exciting competition. The tournament will feature an online qualifier played on our special tournament realms. Top teams will proceed to their respective regional finals and compete for a chance to reach the global world championship – with cash prizes totaling over $200,000.

These realms have been outfitted with level 80 character templates and the latest epic gear from Wrath of the Lich King. As an added bonus, we have included exclusive rewards that players can earn by participating in the tournament, including an Armored Murloc in-game pet. Top teams will also contend for a chance to achieve the “Vanquisher” title for their live characters.

If you would like to demonstrate your arena prowess, or simply want a chance to earn the exclusive loot, please check back next week for more details and register!


Hunter Ammo Changes partially removed from Patch 3.1.0
In our 3.1.0 class updates post, we had announced plans to remove consumable ammunition from the game upon the release of the next major content patch. Our original plans were to change ammunition so that hunters would no longer need to utilize bag space, while adding some new functionality that would continue to make ammunition a compelling element of gameplay. Unfortunately, this intended change will not be completely ready in time for patch 3.1.0.

We still fully intend to move forward with this change when the additional functionality becomes available, and for 3.1.0 we still intend to remove the need for pouches/quivers by greatly increasing the stack sizes on arrows and bullets, and the haste bonus from the bags will be preserved in another fashion. We’ll be sure to inform our players with further updates once they become available.


Blue Posts
Unintentional instance saving
We did speak with some of the developers about this recently. While I'm unable to comment on your specific suggestions, I'll say we were all in agreement that something needs to change with raid IDs. On the one hand, we are happy that pick-up groups have a wider array of options at their disposal, whether it be 5, 10, or 25 man content. On the other hand though, it's currently too easy to end up saved to an instance that's been cleared, or close to it, without knowing ahead of time this will happen.

We've been looking at player feedback in this regard, and are working to develop a better interface to warn players that will be saved to an instance that's been partially, or completely cleared. This is something that might not happen in 3.1, but we'll be discussing this further internally in the near future. (Source (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/1/14990463457-saved-i-hate-raid-ids.html))

As to the instance saving: Maybe they should look at the solution done in the MMO "LOTRO". While the game IMHO is generally not as good
as WoW the lock system in LOTRO I like much better than the one from WoW.

In this game it works like that:

- On the raid tab there is a special section about instance locks. Instead of (like in WoW) just the name of the instance you see behind the
name of the instance a list of "instance steps" (don't remember the exact term they use in LOTRO, but something like that). So for example
if you have a raid instance with 7 bosses, and you killed boss 1-3, it lists: "My Raid Instance: 1,2,3" in your raid UI.
- On another part of the Raid UI it lists "the locks of the raid group". So let's imagine you have killed bosses 1,2,3, but another one of the
group has killed bosses 1,2,3,4. In this case this part of the raid UI lists "My Raid Instance: 1,2,3,4". And if you look at the UI you clearly
see someone killed more bosses than you this id already. This entry shows the "highest" list of bosses killed by all people.
- Now the important part: If someone killed MORE BOSSES this id than the LEADER of the instance a message "incompatible raid locks" comes
up and NOBODY can enter the instance. The only way to fix this is by giving raid lead to the person who has "the most advanced lock" (killed
the most bosses) and then someone (the leader or someone else) enters the instance.
- A big difference to WoW locks is this: Let's imagine raid member A killed 1,2,3 with raid group A. Raid member B killed 1,2,3,4 with Raid Group B
before. In WoW they could not continue the raid together as they are on different locks. On LOTRO they give lead to Raid Member B, the locks
are updated to 1,2,3,4. And now all can enter an instance where bosses 1-4 are dead, the rest still alive. This feature is especially extremely popular
with PUGs. Ever encountered the problem that you killed one raid boss with a bad PUG and now you cannot do the raid this week anymore, as nobody
is willing to skip the first boss? In LOTRO you just tell the other group to invite you once they killed the first boss with 11/12, then you get an invite
and are on the same lock (as boss #1 is dead for both of you).
- total impossible is just stuff like: Member A killed 1,2,3, and Member B killed 1,2,4. They cannot join the same raid unless they find someone who
has killed 1-4, and they both take this guy's lock.
- The downside of this IMHO especially for PUGs great system is that people trade around "only endboss alive" locks a lot to avoid to do the early bosses.
You want your alt raid only to do the final boss? No problem, temporary log the alt into the main raid and "copy their lock". Good thing is that it is
completely transparent which lock you will join and there is no possibility (unless you forget to check on the Raid UI) you get a lock "by mistake".
