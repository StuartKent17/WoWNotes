Feedback: Epic Battlegrounds
We’re working on some hotfixes to Epic Battlegrounds. These changes are not yet live, but should be ready for the game in the next few days.

Alterac Valley

We’re modifying the scaling in the zone to make creatures more difficult for players at high item-levels. Our goal here is to make gameplay feel more similar to the difficulty of Alterac Valley in early Tides of Vengeance.


Isle of Conquest

The health of destructible walls will be increased by 30%. This is intended to offset the power that the docks has given players with recent 8.1.5 improvements.


Battle for Wintergrasp

The damage done by the Catapult’s Flame Breath ability will be reduced. We still want Flame Breath to feel scary and powerful, especially against other players, but we feel that current values are too high.


We’ve been monitoring Wintergrasp closely, and have been pleased to find that win rates for defense and offense are quite even, with a less-than-5% advantage for defenders. The change above to Catapult could have an impact on that, so we’re holding back on making further changes until we see how this one goes.

We have some other improvements to Epic Battlegrounds, including a new UI to track Lokholar and Ivus turn-in progress, that we’re bringing to the game in the Rize of Azshara content update.

Thank you for your continued feedback on Epic Battlegrounds. We really appreciate it.

I'm very surprised that this is their fix for AV. NPCs are already tanky and strong, to the point where doing a boss with less than 4 towers/bunkers down is not possible and galv/balinda being skipped 100% of the time already. The real problem in AV as far as I can tell is that the current meta of defending IB and SH and slowly pushing through is heavily in favor of the Horde, since IB is much easier to defend not only because of tower vs. bunker layout, but because of the chokepoints and high ground. If they keep buffing NPCs, which in this case might be more the case of just keeping things as they are in anticipation of average player ilvl inflation, Horde will just cling on to their map advantage. This really fixes nothing.

The IoC changes don't really have an impact one way or the other, other than making docks even riskier because glaives would have to stay alive longer. If anything this makes hangar even better and even more consistent. The turret on siege will still 2 shot entire raid groups in a few seconds and demos will still be very effective vs. players.

The WG changes I personally find very sad. Catas being so strong allowed players like myself to have an enormous impact on the BG, sometimes getting 50+ killing blows and really hard-carrying the match. Yes Catas are absolutely OP, but simply nerfing them will get ahead of players actually realizing how dangerous they are in melee range and how squishy and rootable the vehicles are. Currently just 2 good dps can absolutely 2v1 a single cata without dying as long as they properly kite and use cc and defensive cooldowns. A single person that knows what they are doing will lose the 1v1 against a cata, but at the very least take most of its health down with him. I don't like this change and I wish Blizzard would give the community a chance to learn how to deal with catas before taking away the high risk and high reward potential of catas. Depending on how big this nerf is, demos might just be the best vehicle now, just sitting at 100 yards shooting at groups of people, vehicles and of course walls.

I wish they were more transparent about winrates across all the epic BGs too btw, this is only half the story they are telling here.
