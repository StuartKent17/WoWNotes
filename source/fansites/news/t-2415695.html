Patch 8.0.1 Hotfixes - July 27
Creatures and NPCs

Melee and spell damage of creatures below level 100 reduced by up to 16%.
Health of creatures between levels 15-100 reduced by up to 24%.

Developers’ note: These changes are live in-game, however unit frames may not fully reflect the new health values until a subsequent companion hotfix that we plan to apply next week.

Yeah, scaling all zones 20-110 would be fine (Legion is over, let's not leave Broken Isles as its own mandatory leveling experience).

I would like if they took a closer look at the scaling system itself. There are definite pros and cons to it, but Blizz has opted for an all-in implementation that really diminishes the RPG power fantasy. Take a look at what they did in Destiny for a more nuanced approach.
Every mob has a single level, that doesn't change. What scales is your power relative to that mob's level. Mobs above you have a scaled power factor against you, that diminishes as you approach their level. As you outlevel them, you gain a power factor over them, but the factor diminishes exponentially as your level grows, eventually flatlining. You only ever grow so strong.

In WoW it might look like this: Stitches is a level 30 Elite. You're level 20, you're hopelessly outmatched. At 25, you might start to think about getting a group together, and around 28-30, class depending, you're ready to take on Stitches solo. As you level further, Stitches gets easier and easier, until at level 40 you could breeze through his hp with half a rotation- 3-4 globals. However, it's the same at 50, and 60, and even 110: he's still a breeze, but not a one shot. A huge pack of Stitches might conceivably ruin your day.

I think it really begets the question, then, what is Blizz's philosophy about outleveled content? Do they want to keep it relevant, as an additional legitimate activity you would dabble for whatever purpose, easy but not trivial? Or should everything be left behind, grey and globaled? For me, Destiny 1 went with the former solution and I thought it worked quite well.
