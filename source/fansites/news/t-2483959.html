Items

Sinister Gladiator's Maledict (https://www.wowdb.com/items/165806-sinister-gladiators-maledict) now immediately displays a new additional debuff on players targeted by the trinket's spell indicating when they will be struck by its damage and healing absorb effects.



Patch 8.2 PTR - Nazjatar Budding Deepcoral Item
Recently, the Budding Deepcoral (https://ptr.wowdb.com/items/169885-budding-deepcoral) was added to the PTR, allowing you to ride your underwater mounts like normal mounts in Nazjatar. This item costs 5 Prismatic Manapearl (https://ptr.wowdb.com/currencies/1721-prismatic-manapearl) and can be purchased from the same vendors that sell the Benthic Gear (https://www.mmo-champion.com/content/8502-Patch-8-2-PTR-Benthic-Gear-Preview-Raid-Testing-Schedule-May-9-10-DLC-677) in Nazjatar. The item is consumed on use and doesn't provide any indication that the item is active. It persists through log outs, but is removed the moment you leave Nazjatar and must be repurchased to use again.

Official Blizzcon AXS Pass Exchange
The second wave of Blizzcon ticket sales sold out yesterday, and Blizzard made a post (https://blizzcon.com/en-us/news/22965341/blizzcon-2019-passes-second-wave-sold-out) linking to the official location on AXS (https://s.axs.com/blizzcon/fs/blizzcon2_soldout.html) where you can transfer or exchange tickets. Some things to note:


If you purchased BlizzCon 2019 passes, you can permanently transfer them to your friends and family before August 25 at 11:59 p.m. PDT.
Official Pass Resale is the place to buy and sell BlizzCon Passes from fans for fans. Highlights of using the Official Pass Exchange include:

Passes bought through the Official Pass Exchange are always authentic.
Fair prices, no hidden fees, guaranteed.

You can safely buy tickets here (http://shop.axs.com/?c=axs&e=16059562), but keep in mind that there is no limit to the price that sellers can ask for their tickets. There are legit requests among many high ones.
Sellers and buyers are subject to extra reselling fees and taxes.

Um, you're wrong.

SWTOR does allow you to cover up your armor, with anything else, via the wardrobe options. It didn't LAUNCH that way, but the limitations were dropped a few years back. My Sith can wear her leather armor all the time, and via wardrobe window - put a slave girl-half-lingerie on display over it and go PVP. The only restriction is, like WoW transmog, only green and above items can be used. Also like WoW, you can't transmog a lightsaber into a gun.

TESO allows you to cover up your armor, via dressing room, with everything else. You just have to learn the motif (receipie) for the gear and you can wear it at any time. TESO also allows you to replace your weapons with any OTHER weapon of the same hand-type. So while Bows are only transmogged to bows - any 1h can be transmogged to ANY other 1h (so your sword can be a hammer or an ax or a dagger). So they allow some weapon transmog but you can't make your bow into a staff. TESO never had armor related restrictions with their wardrobe system.

So actually - while some games do still have some restrictions, like WoW, on "transmogging" (or whatever term) - the two games you actually mentioned - actually allow exactly that. My TESO Nightblade, leather wearing, khajit is right now running around with an "old cloth robe" look.

As a WoW Hunter- it just really ticks me off that Hunters were never allowed to TMOG both leather/mail - even though they could only equip leather for the first 40 levels. (Back when it was a thing...).

I get why Blizz keeps the type restrictions on TMOG because in this game - they've always wanted to be able to identify the class by the set/look. I just don't like it and wish they'd give it up. No one really cares that the mage is wearing plate if the mage doesn't have the plate stats. Let the mage wear plate (battlemages are a thing...). But I get their continuing to stand by their original intent.
