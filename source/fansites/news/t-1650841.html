Hotfixes for December 5th
Garrisons, Followers, and Outposts Garrison BuildingsFishing Shack: Completing a daily Fishing quest now awards 15 skill points to Fishing (up from 1). Gnomish Gearworks / Goblin Workshop: Overcharged Demolisher should now be usable only in the areas it was intended for. Gnomish Gearworks / Goblin Workshop: "Skyterror" Personal Delivery System should now be usable only in the areas it was intended for.

Quests [Requires a realm restart.] Corrected an issue that could cause Bloodmaul Stronghold, Shattrath City, and the Pit to sometimes not drop Apexis Crystals despite being an active quest area for the day.

Creatures and NPCs Fate-Twister Tiklal has relocated to a more prominent location closer to the Apexis Traders in Warspear.

Dungeons, Raids, and Scenarios RaidsHighmaul[Requires a realm restart.] The Butcher: The boars Krush and Smash now deals less damage with their Boar's Rush ability and should no longer kill players in one hit. Tectus: Players should no longer be able to use their bonus rolls twice after defeating Tectus.

PvP AshranTo address a potential pathing issue, Fangraal, Kronus, and faction Captains now continuously push towards the enemy faction's base. Players entering Ashran from a PvE realm should now be correctly flagged for PvP. Characters should no longer be able to resurrect at the King's Road graveyard if their faction isn't in control of that point. Resolved an issue where faction Captains may not be spawning correctly.

Battlegrounds and Arenas Resolved an issue where if a Flag Carrier was standing in the area where their team's flag would be when it is returned, they'd be unable to capture the flag until they leave and return to the area again. Arathi Basin: Characters on the Alliance team in a Rated Battleground should now be able to be resurrected by their Spirit Healer at the Blacksmith graveyard if their team is in control of it. [Requires a realm restart.] Eye of the Storm: Characters in a Rated Battleground will now return to their team's starting position when releasing, regardless of what bases their team controls. The Battle for Gilneas: Resolved issues where Spirit Healers may sometimes not spawn in correctly when a point has been captured.

Professions First AidAntiseptic Bandage no longer requires Sea Scorpion Segments to craft. JewelcraftingCritical Strike Taladite should no longer incorrectly require a Taladite Crystal to craft.

Items Consuming a Draenor potion, elixir, or flash should no longer cause the character to break out of stealth. Mantid Elixir now provides a more reasonable increase to armor. Primal Combatant's Drape of Contemplation now provide a bonus to Multistrike instead of Critical Strike to differentiate it from the Drape of Meditation. Primal Gladiator's Drape of Contemplation now provide a bonus to Multistrike instead of Critical Strike to differentiate it from the Drape of Meditation.

Now one of the primary arguments in favor of no flying is that ground mounts allow the player to see the world in a more immersive manner (which is silly, it's the same as seeing it from the sky, just from a different perspective; you'll see the same tree whether you're on the ground or in the air, you'll just see the 50 other trees surrounding it from the sky in a way you never could on the ground). However, how much time do you really spend on the ground?

When I travel anywhere, 90% of the time I jump on a fp. If I need to go to Spires of Arak, I jump on the fp. If I need to go to Nagrand, I jump on the fp, and so on. So essentially I am already spending a great deal of my game time in the air -- the only difference is, I'm not the one controlling the journey.

The expansion, itself, does its best to limit contact with a great deal of players out in the world, because it basically turns /afk in a major city to /afk in your garrison. In your garrison you have access to all the channels, including the city channel, you have LFR/LFG, plus the new group finder OC-like system, queuing up missions, work orders, bank, etc. -- and the cherry on top, you have a portal directly to the most visited hub in the game right now.

There is almost no reason to leave your garrison other than camping world bosses, doing a fast group for your daily apexis crystals or questing from 90-100 -- so the reasoning behind flying ruining the game is a bit hollow. At cap, you'll barely ever engage in that content that you left behind in other zones unless there isn't much else to do, or you need to grab a follower you didn't get. Also, if Blizzard was so concerned about people circumventing their ground content, why did they add a feather in the game that lets you fly over half a zone?

The best compromise, it seems, would be to do what they did with flying in TBC: make you wait until cap and have it cost a sizable amount of gold to purchase.

The ground game seems tailor made for levelers. I can understand why being grounded from 90-100 makes sense. That is all about ground content. You're not focused on getting to farming locations so much as you're questing and picking up the nodes you find; you're not looking to get to a single objective, you have a handful of objectives that are all bunched up in the same area. It's a different game, therefore there should be different methods of approaching the content.

As I said in the first paragraph, we're already spending a great deal of our game time in the air -- we're just sitting on a slow flight path, probably minimizing the game and listening to music, or leaving the keyboard to get a snack. With flying, you are getting the same experience of moving from point a to b, you're just also taking in the sights around you. You're picking up nodes you see along the way, or getting a rare kill that is half dead as opposed to seeing it from the air and knowing you won't have the time to ride back to it once you land.

Leveling and cap have always been two different games. There is no way around that. It's just how the game was built. Your needs as you level will rarely sync up with your needs once you're at cap and on your 3rd+ alt; flying just allows the capped player more freedom to focus on the aspects they are more likely to engage in that are different from the leveler (instead of grouping up quests, you're just wandering around finding materials to farm -- so there is no reason why a capped player would care about going through a field of mobs that will serve no function other than wasting time -- they're already level capped, they don't need the experience, the gold is negligible, and it just makes an already time consuming task a little more tedious).
