Restoration Druid Feedback -- Build 21232 -- 3-Mar
The minor tuning changes last build were intended to move a small amount of healing from Wild Growth to Rejuvenation, and to slightly increase costs overall due to the class's currently high efficiency, especially on a single target (the latter part is mostly represented by the Swiftmend change). But, we agree the simultaneous healing and cost increase on Rejuvenation looks like it fights against the mastery. Next build will move Rejuvenation's healing and cost slightly back down. Both changes are quite small, but should be noticeable on a spell as frequently used as Rejuvenation. (Blue Tracker (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/topic/403572-restoration-druid-feedback-build-21232-3-mar/) / Official Forums (http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/20742624743?page=1#12))

Computers - Setup of the Month
Each month or so we will post a couple of hardware setups for those of you who are thinking of upgrading their computer!

This month
The current SSD prices (http://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2016/march/ssdgraph.gif) are fairly flat right now, with most SSDs under $0.50 per gigabyte when on sale. New GPUs may be coming towards the middle of this year

Depending on how much you want to overclock, you may need to spend a bit more for a better CPU cooler like the NH-D15 (http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B00L7UZMAK?ie=UTF8&tag=mmochamp-20&linkCode=as2&camp=1789&creative=390957&creativeASIN=B00L7UZMAK).

Unless you are doing a lot of video rendering or streaming, you do not need the 6700K and should spend that money elsewhere.

Make sure to keep your AMD (http://sites.amd.com/us/game/downloads/Pages/downloads.aspx) and Nvidia (http://www.nvidia.com/Download/index.aspx?lang=en-us) drivers up to date.

Don't forget to visit the Computer Forum (http://www.mmo-champion.com/forums/290-Computer) for any extra questions!

In my experience I've always found future-proofing to be a bit of a fallacy, with the possible exception of motherboard and power supply IF you have future considerations of going SLI/crossfire. That's about the only exception that springs to mind. Let's assume you have zero interest in running a dual-card setup. In that case buy the PSU you need and stick with it. It's that simple.

RAM? You can always add more later at any time, IE: when you NEED more RAM, and chances are 2 years down the line another stick will cost less than it does up front, potentially saving money. If it turns out you don't ever need more RAM, congratulations, you just saved money too. I personally stuck with 8GB and 3 years later (today) 8GB still serves my needs, despite friends suggesting I 'future proof' all those years ago.

The problem with future-proofing is it makes the assumption that you're getting a better deal up-front, when in reality you may not be, due to unexpected 'leaps' in technology, or even lack of advancements in how demanding games are 2 years down the line. Another problem with future-proofing is it often involves buying very high-end parts which generally results in very poor value for money. If you have a lot of money to spend, that's fine, but the act of 'future proofing just in case' is questionable at best.

I just want to say that these builds are pretty bad, and that you can get something much better for the same price (if not less)
as an example, I'll go through all the faults of the Puppy build

Case: No problems here apart from no usb 3.0 ports, though I suggest the fractal design core 1100 (smoother look, has usb 3.0 ports, same price)

Power Supply: Admittedly not a bad choice, but it is non modular - horrible for cable management and if you care about aesthetics at all you wouldn't want the ketchup and mustard colored cables that come with this unit

CPU: The biggest fault in this entire build, FX processors are very outdated and use a dead socket, have high power usage and get very hot, and you will have no upgrade route - You might see 3.5 ghz and 6 cores and go "whoa!" but any new i3 will outperform it by quite a bit

Heatsink: No complaints here, good value

Motherboard: Good quality motherboard (though using dead socket) But, You definitely do not need a motherboard that costs the same as your CPU, I understand it is for overclocking but at this pricerange you shouldn't be thinking about that

Memory: Nothing to say here, ram is ram and 8gb is enough for everything

Graphics card: Not a bad card, but for the same price you can grab a GTX 950 which performs better (not by a noticeable amount, but more is better)

Hard drive: while WD Black drives perform better than other 7200rpm harddrives, the extra $30 is not worth it if you are on a budget this low, a better choice would be a WD Blue or a Seagate Barracuda to save money and spend the extra $30 on another part

DVD: Nothing wrong with it but I don't know why you would want an optical drive, windows can be installed off of a usb stick


I highly recommend pcpartpicker (website) for anything to do with building a pc
