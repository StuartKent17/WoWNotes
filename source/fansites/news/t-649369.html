What is the new arrangement like, compared to your previous experiences with Mouseports and SK?

We feel that the major difference here is that we are not just simply becoming a team in a big e-sports company. We don't really think any e-sports company has truly prioritized WoW and had the drive to really make it a central part of what they do, probably since CS and WC3 are still very big. We are really happy to be in a position where we have investment and top end management coming in from above but we are still a WoW guild and a WoW site and we have control over that as both guild masters and the development team. Overall we have a lot more freedom and control over things and that is immensely rewarding in comparison to our past endeavors.




Is there any truth to the rumor that you think all the players that didn't make the cut are bad, unskilled etc.?

That really depends on how you define bad. We had two full guilds of players and we had to make that into one. There were a few players who we felt were not going to be good enough to make it in the new guild, so we had no choice but to cut them. Those players would not be considered bad, but we could only take the absolute best. We also had to consider personal issues and making sure that everyone would be able to work together, so skill wasn’t the only factor in the decision making. We then ended up with a roster of about 44-45 potentials and that has naturally been cut down to a manageable 35 through a process of natural selection. The guild is not a charity, we want the best players and real internal competition for spots. That is working really well right now, some people have left but what we have been left with is a really fantastic roster.
