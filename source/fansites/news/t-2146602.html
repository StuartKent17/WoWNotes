Patch 7.1.5 Arrives on January 10
We’re ringing in the new year with a brand new patch on Tuesday, January 10. Patch 7.1.5 offers a variety of new content, including a new Timewalking event, Micro-Holidays, and the return of the Brawlers’ Guild. Dive into our previews and developer Q&As to learn more about each of them.

Patch 7.1.5 Preview - Mists of Pandaria Timewalking
Join the denizens of Azeroth in a new journey back in time to the days of the lifting of the mists—Timewalking is coming to the islands of Pandaria. [More...] (https://worldofwarcraft.com/en-us/news/20402943/patch-7-1-5-preview-mists-of-pandaria-timewalking)

Patch 7.1.5 Preview: The Return of the Brawler’s Guild
After a quick break to mend some wounds and broken bones, everyone’s favorite fighting series is back. A new set of challenging encounters are coming to town, and it’s up to you to muscle your way past them for glory and prizes. [More...] (https://worldofwarcraft.com/en-us/news/20402944/patch-7-1-5-preview-the-return-of-the-brawler-s-guild)

Micro-Holidays in Patch 7.1.5
Patch 7.1.5 brings with it a variety of updates and new content. One of these new additions is what we’re calling micro-holidays. Each one is meant to make the world feel more alive without making players feel like they need to participate for big rewards—while still providing some more variety and fun. [More...] (https://worldofwarcraft.com/en-us/news/20407403/micro-holidays-in-patch-7-1-5)

I'm sitting here watching peple moan and they clearly don't know the facts. As stated in Blizzcon, the patch delivery system was modified for Legion to allow for more streamlined content release rather than overwhelmingly large chunks.

7.0, 7.2, 7.4 for example were always going to be big raid patches as they have been in the past.
7.1, 7.3 7.6 are to be the smaller content patches with things like karazhan etc as we have seen much similar to MOP was with smaller story arcs

7.1.5 style patches were not meant to be content heavy patches. They're essentially the hotfix/retuning patch and also a chance to implement content that wasn't quite ready in time. Which is why we get mini-holidays and timewalking for example.

At Blizzcon Blizzard said they would rather release patches like this so that rather than hold an entire patch back for months and have a content drought, we have instead a steady stream of content available. So this isn't a content patch, 7.1.5 is meant to be the bigger tuning patch that requires a patch to implement changes. The fact we get time-walking and mini holidays is simply because they were not ready for implementation in time during 7.1.
