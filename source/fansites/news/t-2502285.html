WoW Classic Realm Names and Types
Starting August 12th, 2019 at 3 pm PST, players with an active subscription or game time on their World of Warcraft account can reserve their character names by creating up to three WoW Classic characters. Please note that all of your characters on a PVP realm must be the same faction.

Before creating a character, you’ll choose the realm where you want to play that character. Realms in the Americas will be:


https://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2019/august/thumb/classic_servers_us.jpg (https://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2019/august/classic_servers_us.jpg)

Relative realm population will be visible in-game to help in choosing a realm so you will see Low, Medium, or High in the realm selection screen. If there are high numbers of players congregating on individual realms during name reservation and character creation, we will post warnings about the potential for queues on those realms, giving you time to choose alternate realms.

WoW Classic releases worldwide on August 26th at 3:00pm PST.



WoW Classic Realm Names and Types [Europe]
Starting at 00:01 CEST on Tuesday 13th August, players with an active subscription or game time on their World of Warcraft account can reserve their character names by creating up to three Classic characters. Please note that all of your characters on a PvP realm must be the same faction.

Before creating a character, you’ll choose the realm where you want to play that character. The following realms will be available:


https://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2019/august/thumb/classic_servers_eu.jpg (https://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2019/august/classic_servers_eu.jpg)

Relative realm population will be visible in-game to help in choosing a realm so you will see Low, Medium, or High in the realm selection screen. If there are high numbers of players congregating on individual realms during name reservation and character creation, we will post warnings about the potential for queues on those realms, giving you time to choose alternate realms.

WoW Classic releases worldwide on Tuesday 27th August at 00:01 CEST.

Now, some things to consider:


Layering is being used to handle capacity overflow during the launch window, eliminating the need to open additional servers which could lead to potential server merges down the line.
Server capacity will actually be somewhat flexible in order to accomodate the highest population realms after layering is disabled, as it's likely some realms will remain over capacity even after the launch window.
Blizzard intends to provide a mixture of high, medium, and low population servers to accommodate player preferences, which means that they don't expect all realms to reach capacity in the long term.


I don't think we really have enough information at this point to accurately judge what the long term server populations are going to be, but you can certainly make some generalizations and estimations based on this information.


So, for example, if you're being extremely generous and assuming they'll operate at triple 1.12 capacity and that every single server will be high population, you would have in the neighborhood of 250k players (NA+EU). Now obviously that's well beyond what will actually happen, because they won't operate over capacity in the long term and there will be a mix of high, medium, and low population servers.


If you assume the servers will be operating at around 1.12 capacity (say 3.5k flexing to 5k), and you expect about a third of the servers to be high population, a third to be medium population, and a third to be low population, you're probably more realistically looking at 60-75k players (NA+EU).

Assume also that Asia will have roughly the same population as NA+EU combined, which has historically been the case for WoW.


If you're being overly generous, you're looking at about 500,000 players worldwide. If you're being more realistic, you're looking at about 150,000 players worldwide. Split the difference and you're looking at 325,000 players worldwide.
