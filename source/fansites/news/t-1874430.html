Blizzcon - Engineering Panel
While we don't have the Blizzcon schedule just yet, we now know there will be an engineering panel this year! We rarely hear official things about engineering outside of the very rare Engineer's Workshop blog post (http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/4709-Patch-6-1-Engineer%E2%80%99s-Workshop-Anti-Aliasing-Lighting), so this should be a nice opportunity for players interested in the technical aspects of the game to get some more information!

Patch 6.2.2 Hotfixes - The Frozen Halls

So, how did the Rawr Optimizer progress bar work?
Rawr's Optimizer used a genetic algorithm (mostly the handiwork of to search for potential improvements. That... (Celestalon (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/648987533565685760))
...means that it wasn't an exhaustive search, it was just really good at searching the likely places. But that also means... (Celestalon (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/648987700587053057))
...that it could virtually infinitely keep searching less and less likely options. Solution was to let user specify... (Celestalon (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/648987989549432832))
...a desired 'thoroughness', which controlled how long it'd keep looking if it hadn't found an improvement in a while. The... (Celestalon (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/648988266109227008))
...progress bar was really a timer of how long it'd been since it found an improvement, except never going backward. That's it. (Celestalon (https://twitter.com/Celestalon/status/648988397848121345))

I hate it when they nerf the gold in old instances. Why not ban the botters instead of killing the fun for everyone else?

Botting for gold is done on a large scale by professionals or businesses who operate dozens to hundreds of accounts simultaneously, and banning is simply an operating cost that they factor into their business model. I don't know the actual figures from the countries where this is done, but the idea is that if it costs $5 to set up an account, and you can fence $20 worth of virtual gold using automated scripts before it's banned, that's $15 profit. Do that with 20 accounts simultaneously running on two computers and you've got $300. If they have good underground connections, the $5 base cost per account can be further reduced by paying for stolen credit card numbers which will also be canceled within days - not a concern with for a temporary WoW account.

When I looked into what botters were doing a couple years ago, the standard routine for dungeon botters was to start mass botting on Friday evenings, farm all weekend, and be gone Monday morning. I assume they were taking advantages of Blizzard's work hours, exploiting the reduction in security staff during weekends, and were then banned when Blizzard staff got to work on Monday. Back then, the dungeon of choice was Botanica, with Halls of Lightning as a viable alternative. Dozens to hundreds of paladins would stream in and out of Botanica every minute (you can find old videos of it on YouTube) and every few runs fly to a mailbox to launder their gold. Blizz can change the economics of a particular dungeon/raid, but it's a whack-a-mole problem - botters will just move to the next most profitable location, after a brief period of adjustment to fine-tune and disseminate scripts.

I assume these businesses use their resources (computers & personnel) for other mostly-automated profit-making enterprises during the rest of the week.

People without much technical knowledge often offer easy solutions, like "why not ban their IP?", but professionals use an array of proxy servers to connect from different and ever-changing IP addresses. There are ways to counter the really easy solutions. Blizzard could hire more people to monitor for bots, because it's always easy to spot a couple hundred a day if you know where to look, but I assume the business case for that doesn't work out. Instead they seem to rely more on customer reports, and as a customer, it's a thankless, never-ending task trying to report that so many botters.
