The thing is that neither rogue or hunters are nerfed in the arena, no class really is. It's all about how you compose the team and how you specc your class. There is a vast difference in speccing for PvP and speccing for PvE. I know it's not easy to solve the range problem for hunters in the arena but it is highly doable.
Few classes have the kiting ability / ranged dps that hunters have. I know the new / recent changes to the PvP trinket is a problem but it works both ways. While a rogue can remove trap etc with the trinket a hunter can remove sap and stuns with the very same trinket.
Saying that CS / Kidney Shot doesn't run on the same timer is a weak excuse, you just have to learn to prioritise what you want / need to remove!
In my opinion (I can't speak for anyone else) people need to realize there is a big difference between PvPing and PvEing. You can not just gear up to tier 6 in PvE, enter the arena and expect to own in PvP. You do not have the advantages of a tank taking all the hits while getting healed 24/7, you will have to keep the distance on your own and stay alive.
It's all about adapting to your opponents, I've gotten taken down by hunters with silencing shot multiple times and I've won against them aswell (playing a healing class silencing shot can be a bitch ;)). It might not be a good idea to enter a 2 v 2 arena with hunter / healer while a hunter / rogue can be a bitch since the rogue can keep the other teams dps on a distance.

Ah well, that's just my 2 cents. If you want to pvp with you're class:

1. Specc pvp
2. Get a teammate / mates that matches your specc / class (might take some experimenting)
3. Learn to take advantages of your opponents weaknesses, leech mana from mana classes, dot rogues, etc.
4. You can not win all your matches against all classes, all classes have weaknesses against certain classes! Some classes suck at playing another class while they have an easier time against other classes. (Frost mages vs Warriors, Elemental Shammies vs most mages, etc, etc).

Edit:
Forgot to say that getting good at pvp requires alot of training with your class, learning how to take down other classes takes knowledge about them. Knowing what stunns / attacks / weaknesses / cooldowns other classes have can be a life saver. For example it can be a big help knowing how long CD roges have on kick, hunters on traps, etc, etc. :) But if PvP doesn't get you're steam going stick to killing bosses with your guild or something else ;)
