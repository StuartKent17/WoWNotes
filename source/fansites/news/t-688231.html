Addon Spotlight - Elitist Group
Today's addon is a good way to build raid PUGs (raid weekly quests, I love you so much) without wasting too much time inspecting everyone to make sure that they're not terrible. Once again the original description is just fine and I'll steal it!

Elitist Group (http://www.wowinterface.com/downloads/info15573-ElitistGroup.html)'s goal is to shift focus away from single scores that don't tell you anything about the users choice of gear. Instead of a single score you'll see a variety of information from experience in different tiers of dungeons and raids, to if their choice of equipment, enchants or gems matches their spec.

Slash command: /elitistgroup (/eg) use /elitistgroup help for a list of slash commands.

Equipment/gem/enchant
Items are categorized based on their stats. Spell power is a caster item, hit is DPS, Strength is for melee dps and so on. You can see what type of item the person is wearing, as well as if it's valid for their spec. If your Holy Paladin is wearing block gear, it will flag that they are wearing gear intended for tanks. However, if they were a Protection Paladin it will allow it.

While items will be categorized into their spec type, EG will also tell you if somebody is missing a gem or they are using green gems in epic gear.

Dungeon experience
Quickly see how experienced someone is through their achievements and stats. Everything is broken down by tier, 5 man, 10 man, 25 man and normal vs heroic. For raids, experienced is roughly 3 clears and for 5 mans it's around 6 (combined runs, not 6 of each 5 man). The scoring is setup to reward full clears. Somebody who has done the first 4 bosses in 25-man Trial of the Crusader but never clears it will have to do more runs to be considered experience than someone who clears the entire instance.

Some achievements are also factored into the score to make it easier to be considered experienced. A Tribute to Insanity or The Immortal can count for an entire full clear. Even doing 25-man Ulduar hard modes will increase your 25-man Ulduar normal mode experience.

Summary
For both 5-mans and raids, a summary window is provided through /eg summary. Allowing you to quickly see party members talents and a quick pass/no pass for all of their gear, enchants and gems.

Item level
Item levels are used to give you an idea of where gear is from, as well as the players general gear quality. It is not the sole (or even the primary) stat shown. Items have quality modifiers to stop 200 blues being worth the same as 200 epics: 100% epic, 95% blue, 90% green, 60% white, 50% grey. Heirlooms are considered 187 item level blues at 80 (~2.22 item level/player level)

Suggested dungeons are lowest item level dropped with a 89% modifier for 5-mans, 90% modifier for normal 10 and 25 mans, 91% modifier for heroic/hard mode 10 and 25 mans.

Player notes
Finished a dungeon, was your tank amazing? Player notes will let you rate him 1 - 5 as well as make a note for future reference, or for other people who group with him. You can leave one note per person, with your most recent note overwriting any old ones.

Addon communication
All notes, player data can be synced. You will only send your notes, and people can only request your gear data. This makes sure that there is less potential for people to mess up your database of players and corrupt it.

Communications can be completely disabled, or you can choose to not listen to communications from certain channels to restrict who is allowed to request data. No communications are sent while in combat, they will be queued and processed gradually to prevent any noticeable effect on gameplay.

The problem with Warbringer
The problem with the PvP side of Warbringer is that when you consider prot warrior versus mage (just as an example), there was nothing a mage could do to a well versed warrior. The warrior carries a lot of stuns, silences, and then any attempt to root him is broken by multiple abilities. So then the warrior's teammate (like a hunter) is just doing tons of damage while the target has no defenses.

For the other side of this change, we certainly don't design 5-player or raid content with the assumption that the tank has Warbringer (or a way out of snares and roots in general). We understand that it can be a big help in specific situations but we don't feel it's the only option in any PvE circumstances. (Source (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/10/22418633982-what-shall-the-prot-nerf-be.html))

Patch 3.3.x nerfs for prot warriors
Let's look first at the Warbringer change.

Will it nerf them for PvP? Yes. Aside from stuns and Dismantle, rooting or snaring the Prot warrior is the major way to keep him off of you. Considering all of the stuns and silences that Prot has, when they can also jump out of every Frost Nova with Warbringer, then there's not much in the way of skill a mage can employ to stay alive. They just get countered in every way.

Will it nerf them for PvE? Yes, but really slightly. I'm not sure I can ever remember a case in my WoW-playing history where my warrior or some other tank caused a wipe because of a root, so even if it did happen, it can't happen that much or be that memorable. Warriors certainly won't get sat because of some new Achilles' heel and we don't design encounters around the assumption that tanks can't be rooted. The important part of Warbringer from a PvE perspective, removing the stance restrictions, is still intact.

Now the damage change. As Bornakk posted recently, what we essentially did was look at some of the successful Prot PvP characters and compare them to some of the Prot tanks out there. We nerfed the block conversion to Shield Slam damage so that it would hit the PvP guy without really hitting the PvE guy. (The PvP warriors aren't stacking a ton of Shield Block Value, but they do have a lot of Strength which also converts to block for purposes of making Shield Slam hit harder -- many of these guys are wearing PvE dps plate.)

Will it nerf them for PvP? Yes. We want Prot to be able to hit reasonably hard, but they also need to pay some price for their massive survivability. We thought the Warbringer change alone would nerf the warriors vs. e.g. mages but wouldn't control their damage. Those big Shield Slam crits should go down a lot.

Will it nerf them for PvE? Probably not, or at least not much. If you tank with a lot of Strength or SBV, you might see your Shield Slams go down, but that's why we buffed the threat of Shield Slam to compensate.

Again, keep the changes in perspective. We want Prot to be PvP viable. We don't want Prot warriors to dominate PvP or have everyone feel like they need one for their team. Likewise, we want Prot to do higher damage than the almost trivial damage that they did in BC. But that doesn't mean they need to be competitive with the dps while they're tanking. You see, THAT is the problem with trying to automate things such as determining someone's skill and/or experience. What will you base it on?


Shouldn't you base it on the same thing you would if you manually inspected someone? Sounds like a fairly simple question to me.

IMO, you should break down how good a player is into about 4 catagories.

1) Gear level, this is essentially what GS does right now. Although I like the EG way of displaying the average total ilvl instead of arbitrary numbers that need a key to be deciphered. But it does the same thing, averaging 213 clears you for Ulduar 25, averaging 226 clears you for ToC 25, averaging 245 clears you for ICC. It's not unreasonable to require gear from the previous tier of content to be ready for the next one (GS actually does this in the suggested level of gear for content part but people always ignore it). Before you QQ about greatness or an idol that's a lower ilvl but technically better, remember that it's only 1-2 pieces out of your entire gear set, it's not going to make much of a difference in your score anyway.

2) Gems and enchants. This is a trickier thing to do. Not because an add-on can't measure it but because detailed info on 30 classes and specs needs to be included in the add-on with up to date relevant theorycraft. It's easy to see some things like gemming hit while over hit cap but many other things are class dependent. Namely, some melee classes find it necessary to have the expertise cap while others don't, some set bonuses change how you gem while others don't (2pcT9 ret making str/crit in yellow sockets better then just pure str, but any other situation makes 20 str better). All melee classes should be using 21 agi/3% crit damage as a meta (except enhancement shaman). There's a ton of rules that would need to be involved and you can't expect one person to know exactly how all 30 classes work.

3) Talents. Can easily be evaluated based on the number of deviations from the standards specs. Obviously, where "filler" points go wouldn't effect this score. Bigger amounts would need to be subtracted for missing essential talents then just missing good talents.

4) Experience. Just look at how long it took someone to complete any given content after it was released. If they didn't clear ToC until ICC was out, bad for them. If they managed to do most Ulduar hardmodes before 3.2 was out, good for them. If they have zero raid experience but have full 232-245's, you've got a badge scrub on your hands.

I don't know if you know how your different classes interact with a boss. No one cares what your main has done if you show up on an alt, if you want to bring your toon with the experience, then that will boost your score.

I see a lot of concern about this addon, and I personally dislike all forms of measuring skill through Gearscore-like addons or achievement linking. There's a big But to my opinion though..

[...] There'll always be the lazy scrub who demands achievements and skyrocketing gearscores - the good part about that is that you know right away this person is clueless and going with his/her pug is likely to be a waste of time. This addon is less retarded than gearscore, and sure it'll be misused (as everything, it can and will be misused), but if people are going to judge people based on achievements and retarded scores anyway, rather have them do it in the smartest way possible, which is open their eyes to the more important facets that come to judging a person's worth.

No method to judge a player's worth will ever be foolproof short from just taking someone and experiencing first hand how good he is.
While the addon is similar to Gearscore in terms of gauging someones worth with a numerical value, it goes above and beyond and essentially puts their armory into the WoW client. Sure, the person in full 187-200 blues might not be geared for ICC10 (statistically speaking), but that doesn't mean the guy in near-full 245 gear is experienced enough.

The game now-a-days allows anyone to complete the base content with nearly any combination of gear, knowledge, and awareness. Someone who has no situational awareness can still clear all current content aside from Putricide. Someone without all the phat lewtz can outperform people decked in 245/258 gear.

And, unless you're a DPS, performance is difficult to gauge. Topping the healing meters is as important to a healer as stacking hit is. Healing meters vary wildly from fight to fight and person to person. A "traditional" resto druid might do amazingly well on Twin Valkyrs hard mode because of the massive raid-wide damage, but that doesn't mean the resto druid near the bottom is performing worse than the others. You could have too many healers, or he could be focusing on keeping the tanks up because you lack discipline priests or holy paladins (traditionally tank healers).

Then, you head off to Anub'arak and that druid is topping the meters because the vast majority of the healing is tank healing, with a small amount of short-term, concentrated raid damage. Does this mean that the other druids are underperforming? No!

Similarly, tanks are difficult to gauge based off of threat alone. While one tank might have amazing threat, he might also be habitually turning his back to bosses/groups of mobs, forcing a wipe (or a lot of pain for your healers). Or, he might be slow to pick up adds, forcing your rogues and hunters to abuse their misdirect and tricks of the trade keys, which then reduces their dps.

Even DPS meters aren't the best for gauging whether or not one dps is better than another. Take a fight like Vezax. Rogue 1 is interrupting every time it's his turn/his kick is up. Rogue 2 isn't. Rogue 2 stomps all over Rogue 1 on the Damage meters. Who's the better rogue? The one who saved his energy for kick, of course, but that isn't obvious by using only one tool.

A combination of raid awareness (which comes from experience) and various tools - many of which are addons - help to determine who are your better players and who are not. This addon attempts to consolidate a few of the tools (namely: Heroes, Gearscore, and Armory) into one single in-game tool. Even this addon on its own wont tell you if a player is competent, but it will give you an idea based off of past experience (rating of boss kills), off of your/others past experience with them (via the notes tool, similar to Heroes), and whether or not they understand the gearing requirements of their class/spec (which means raid leaders aren't forced to keep note cards with class gearing/talent requirements handy).
