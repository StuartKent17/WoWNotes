i dont know what it is, but something is off with those stormwind scenes, rest of it looks pretty nice, but those stormwind scenes look so dated and bad.

It's the lighting on the people compared to the backdrop. As someone who does some Photoshop work, I recognized it immediately.

Look at the direction of the light on the castle behind them. It's (the sun) coming from the upper right, behind the camera. It should be hitting the king in the face as he's looking upward.

Now look at the people. They have a sheen of light behind them, and it looks like it's hitting them more from behind and to the right, placing the light source in front of the camera (though still high off screen). This would suggest that the primary light source on most of the people is actually coming from the opposite direction. This is especially noticeable on the front right soldier, who's helmet is actually shaded in the direction (front right) that the sun should be hitting him.

I don't do any work in visual effects, but I would assumed that making sure the light matches up would be a 101 class on the subject. Kind of surprised that they'd screw something like that up, as the human eye will immediately perceive it as "fake" or "wrong", even if you can't figure out why. The best guess I could make is that perhaps when they shot the scene they were planning on a different backdrop, of that that it was changed during the course of production and they couldn't go back and re-shoot. Perhaps the CGI guys felt that the castle backdrop simply looked better with light shining on it despite the fact that it wouldn't match up with the people in front of it.
