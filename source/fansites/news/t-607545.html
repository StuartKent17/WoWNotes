<a name="part2"></a>Wrath of the Lich King Preview - Part 2
You should really definitely check the preview on Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1), this is one of the most interesting published today.
Inscription
Inscribers will create glyphs that allow players to modify their spells and abilities. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))
Going by the first recipe, which requires Peacebloom, they'll rely on the herbalist gathering profession. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))
Every player will get a new spell book page to which they can add six glyphs - currently, four major and two minor. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))
Major glyphs will be effective in combat - adding damage over time or stun to a physical attack, for example - while minor glyphs will give convenient or cosmetic improvements, removing the need for some spell reagents. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))
Glyphs can be sold at the Auction House (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))



Enchanting
Allowing enchanters sell their enchantments at the Auction House is still planned.(Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))



Lake Wintergrasp / Vehicles
The new vehicle technology, which introduces physics and handling characteristics - including inertia, turning circle, grip and suspension bounce - to land and airborne vehicles and special mounts. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))
It also allows for up to eight passengers per vehicle, and brings up bespoke action bars and skills for vehicle and turret operators. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))
It was intended to be used solely for PVP siege weapons - Dwarven steam tanks, Forsaken plague spreaders and Orc demolishers. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))



World
Even if you'll be able to meet him as soon as you reach Dragonblight (LVL 71-75), the final encounter against Arthas won't be until the last patch of the Lich King cycle. The Icecrown Citadel, will appear after launch. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))
Dragonblight will also let you reunite (violently) with the Scarlet Crusade. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))
Players will meet a lot of monsters from Classic WoW in the Grizzly Hills, including Furbolgs, Kobolds, the Venture Company, and Worgen Werewolves. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))
Arugal (http://www.wowwiki.com/Archmage_Arugal) has been resurrected and now leads the Worgen Werewolves in Grizzly Hills. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))
In Grizzly Hills, you will also find an evil sister city to the Alliance capital, Ironforge. (Screenshot ? (http://static.mmo-champion.com/mmoc/images/news/2008/may/evilironforge.jpg)) (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))
The Argent Dawn faction returns in Zul'Drak. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))
In Sholazar Basin, you'll take part in a faction-reputation war between the Wolvar (sentient wolves) and Oracles ("the next evolution of the Murlocs" - a worrying thought). A much lighter take on reputation gaming is promised here, with players actively encouraged to defect to the other side at will. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))
The expansion will contain a lot of "vehicle quests" as an evolution of BC's bombing runs - throwing flaming oil at Worgen from horseback, airlifting supplies in a gyrocoptor, and yes, mammoth wrangling. (Eurogamer.net (http://www.eurogamer.net/article.php?article_id=138018&page=1))

Information summary
Death Knights
All players with a level 55 character will be able to create one Death Knight per realm, per account. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
Blizzard's designers decided to start the class with a full complement of abilities right off the bat, figuring that any player that has advanced a character to level 55 would be able to learn to handle the new class with minimal difficulty. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
Death Knights will have a summonable Skeletal Deatcharger Mount. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
Death Knights aren't using Mana or Energy, the runes are their primary source of power and are slotted into their "blade," which lies underneath their health bar as a visual indicator of what resources are available to the player at any given moment. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
Spent runes automatically refresh after a set period of time, much like a Rogue's energy bar. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
There are six slots in the blade, and the player is free to fill those slots with any given number of Blood, Unholy or Frost runes. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
Death Knights also have a secondary resource called Runic Power generated by using your rune abilities. It will use the same mechanics as rage and increase when you deal damage, or slowly decrease if you're not using it. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
Death Knights will have a lot of abilities that cost all available Runic Power with varying levels of effectiveness based on total Runic Power spent. (Same mechanics as Execute for warriors, or finishing moves for Rogues) (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
Death Knights will have spells equivalent to auras called "Presence", providing them with powerful self-buffs depending on their role in the group. (One for tanking, one for damage dealing, etc ...) (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
An instant lifedrain spell (described as death coil without the fear component) will be available. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
Death Knights will be the first class to use diseases as a source of damage, with abilities that become strong depending on how many diseases are already applied to the target. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
"Army of the Dead" will spawn numerous undead minions to attack nearby hostiles targets. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
Death Knights will also revive an enemy (or ally) as a ghoul to use it as a pet with its own special abilities like a disease-inflicting strike and a stun. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
When an ally is risen as a ghoul, a pop-up will appear and this ally will have the choice to control the ghoul if he wants to. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))
The Death Knight's particular niche will be in tanking magic-damage-dealing bosses. They will have an ability much like a banshee's anti-magic shell, greatly diminishing the amount of incoming magic damage they'll receive in combat (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872730p1.html))



Death Knight Skills
Death Coil -- Depletes all Runic Power, dealing 600 damage to a non-Undead target, or healing 900 damage on a friendly target. (Curse.com (http://wow.curse.com/articles/details/9634/))
Death Grip -- This is the Death Knight's "taunt" ability. It also pulls the target to the Death Knight, forcing them to attack the Death Knight for a short amount of time. Yes, I said pulls the target; Blizzard is going to allow players to move mobs in the expansion both with Death Grip, and other knockback/pull abilities. This works on players too, so PvP balance ahoy! (Curse.com (http://wow.curse.com/articles/details/9634/))
Chains of Ice -- Roots the target in place. When the spell fades, it places a snare on the target that reduces in potency as the duration runs out. (Curse.com (http://wow.curse.com/articles/details/9634/))
Death Pact -- Sacrifices the raised ghouls to heal the Death Knight. (Curse.com (http://wow.curse.com/articles/details/9634/))
Death and Decay -- Targeted, AoE Damage-over-time which pulses similar to the Paladin spell Consecration. Anyone affected by Death and Decay has a chance to be feared. (Curse.com (http://wow.curse.com/articles/details/9634/))
Frost Presence -- Increases Armor by 45% and allows the Death Knight to generate 25% more threat. Only one presence can be active at any time. (Curse.com (http://wow.curse.com/articles/details/9634/))
Unholy Presence -- Increases Attack Speed and Movement speed by 15%. Only one presence can be active at any time. This was described by Tom Chilton as the "PvP" presence. (Curse.com (http://wow.curse.com/articles/details/9634/))
Anti-magic Shield -- Reduces the damage of the next magical spell cast on the Death Knight by 75%. It also converts the damage reduced into Runic Power. (Curse.com (http://wow.curse.com/articles/details/9634/))
Strangulate -- Depletes all Runic Power, dealing minor damage and silencing the target for up to 5 seconds. (Curse.com (http://wow.curse.com/articles/details/9634/))
Summon Deathcharger -- Allows the Death Knight to summon a Deathcharger mount. This mount is acquired through quests, similar to the Paladin and Warlock land mounts. (Curse.com (http://wow.curse.com/articles/details/9634/))
Raise Dead -- Raises a nearby corpse to fight for the Death Knight for 2 minutes. If used on a player corpse, the player has the option to play as the ghoul for the duration -- gaining access to the ghoul's abilities. (Curse.com (http://wow.curse.com/articles/details/9634/))
The ghoul has the ability to do the following:
Leap to the target
Rend for decent damage-over-time
Stun target, and of course more



Raids & Dungeons
All raid dungeons in Wrath of the Lich King will have both 10-person and 25-person versions. So at level 80, every major encounter and every big boss fight can be experienced with a group of 10. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872794p1.html))
25-person raiding progression is not dependent on 10-person raiding progression. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872794p1.html))
There will be no attunements or keys to obtain, and you won't be locked out of a 25-person instance if you decide to attempt the 10-person version, and vice-versa. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872794p1.html))
25-person raids will earn more, higher-level rewards, in what should be the equivalent of one "tier" of loot quality. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872794p1.html))
A new instance focused on the different dragonflights "The Chamber of Aspects" might be added to the game, but this isn't confirmed yet. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872789p1.html))
Zero raid bosses have been designed at this point. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872789p1.html))



Others
Players will unlock their flying mount at level 77. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872789p1.html))
You can use your Outland flying mount, and new flying mounts will be available. (Gamespy.com (http://uk.pc.gamespy.com/pc/world-of-warcraft-wrath-of-the-lich-king/872789p1.html))

No.


Two reasons:

- Organisatorial effort/raid distribution

It is harder to get a 25 man group together than to get a 10 man group together. Yes, it is very much part of the raid progression.
Also, it is harder to get a "perfect" group with a 25 man than with a 10 man. When an encounter favours a certain class/spec combo it is easier to stack a 10 man group than a 25 man group.


- Margin of error

Quite a lot of bosses have abilities where one player error can wipe the raid. I.e. Archimonde and his soul charges. If you have more players there is a higher chance of one triggering such an abilitzy and causing a wipe.
Lets say, in example, a 5% chance of each player in the raid to make a wipe-causing error during a fight. With 10 players thats around a 60% chance that none of them makes that error (0.95^10). With 25 players thats only a 28% chance (0.95^25).

So, if the 25 man and 10 man would really be the same (exept stuff like the mob HPs for obvious reasons) then the 25 man versions would require about twice as many attempts on average with an equal skilllvl.


Not that they will be the same, mind you. Partly because of the reason described above, but also partly because of other reasons. You can design more complex encounters with 25 man raids (Compare the complexity of vanshji or kael with stuff like prince or ZJ. And thats not (only) because they are higher tier. You simply cannot translate the vashji and kael encounter to 10 man without reducing their complexity) and 25 mans can be designed with the assumption that every class is present in the raid (buffs, abilities, etc) while for a 10 man this isn't something you can count on.
