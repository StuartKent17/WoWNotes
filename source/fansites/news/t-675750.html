Northrend's New Kalu'ak Fishing Derby!
Coming in patch 3.3.0, the esteemed tuskarr of the Kalu'ak are proud to introduce to Northrend the Kalu'ak Fishing Derby, a test of fishing skill with a healthy dash of luck! Every Wednesday beginning at 8 PM players can try to catch the elusive Blacktip Shark. Elder Clearwater sits in Dalaran for one hour awaiting the return of the quickest, most experienced fisher to bring him the first catch of the night. This champion fisher will be rewarded in-kind for his or her fishing expertise. Those who aren’t able to return with the first catch will still receive just reward for getting the Blacktip Shark to Elder Clearwater before he takes his leave.

It is said that these sharks’ favorite meal are the bite-sized Pygmy Suckerfish. Perhaps a hook in their waters will do the trick. Because of the increased concentration of fish, you have an increased chance to catch a shark from fishing pools. Don't forget, in patch 3.3.0 you never catch trash from fishing pools, regardless of skill.

The derby winner will receive a substantial chunk of experience (or money at maximum level) and gain 5000 reputation with the Kalu'ak. In addition, the champion will be given a choice between two unique rewards:

I posted the following suggestion on WoW's official forums:

http://forums.worldofwarcraft.com/thread.html?topicId=20677630906&postId=206757811322&sid=1#0

I was reading about the new Kalu'ak fishing tournament and noted the complaints of several at MMO-Champion, which I thought seemed meaningful and applied to both tournaments:

- The reward cap: 52 winners a year, all of which could be one person. (I've heard on some servers, the same person wins BB every week, brags and deletes/DEs/vendors the rewards.)

- The time: Booty Bay Fishing Extravaganza conflicts with many church/family obligations. The Kalu'ak Fishing Derby conflicts with BOTH family/church obligations and raiding for most serious guilds.

- The methods of winning: tends to involve griefing/PvP/teleportation/group effort, all of which run contrary to the spirit of fishing. People who fish generally set out to avoid a lot of these things. The point of fishing is to "get away from it all".

Suggestion: Utilize existing game mechanics to preserve the "feel" of a fishing competition in a way that is more inclusive of people with diverse/fixed schedules, places fewer limitations on winners and places the emphasis more squarely on the fundamentals of fishing.

- Have the fishing tournaments as quests that reset periodically, similar to the Brewfest keg runs which reset in 12 or 16 hours. This allows people to do it on their own schedule without simply being able to do it at any time. My suggestion would be a 3 hour cooldown to allow people to try out various times of day.

- Have the spawns be limited. For instance, maybe only one pool spawns in each zone every 15 minutes which has a chance to contain a shark for the Kalu'ak derby. (However, a new one spawns if the one gets looted.) In turn, BBFE "Tastyfish pools" could spawn at a limited rate, say one every five minutes... With the additional modifier that a new one spawns every time a tastyfish is looted.

- Have the quest items (tastyfish/shark) be items that disappear if you are summoned, if you teleport or hearth. You wanna do it, you have to use traditional travel. This balances things out more between classes and group vs. solo.

- Create a factor similar to the timed Strat runs. Introduce "celebrity" NPC competitors who appear to taunt you. In a random amount of time, somewhere between fifteen and thirty minutes, one of these NPCs will automatically win. You must beat them through luck and skilled pool hunting. While you are competing for players for pool spawns, another player "winning" will not prevent you from "winning". However, an NPC winning WILL prevent you from winning.

- Introduce a grindable currency/badge system for competing and not winning. Winning is always one part luck but competing often shows, at least, dedication. Grinding IS GOOD and worthy of some reward. Obviously, skill/luck will eliminate some of the need for grinding but if grinding doesn't eventually equal the value of the skill/luck-based rewards here, people will ignore the competition and focus on other aspects of the game where grinding is more appropriately rewarded.

I propose "Badge of the Angler" currency be implemented for both fishing contests as a reward you get for completing it, whether or not you do it in the time limit. These badges could be turned in for:

- A Waterwalking FLASK, Binds on Pickup (ie. longer duration)

- High-powered Bait (say, +150 fishing), Binds to Account

- Faction-themed Fishing Poles (The Argent Tournament Lances, reworked a bit, bought with a mix of Angler badges and honor)

- A re-skinned fishing chair. They're releasing the TCG Turtle and Rocket mounts reskinned and they did the same for the picinic basket.

- An heirloom ring, similar to the Kalu'ak Derby winning reward. Only +3% XP increase instead of +5%.

- A murloc pet. Fishing pole and hat equipped.

- A sea lion mount. Aquatic speed +60%.

- "Swifty the Dark War Tortoise of Impending Doom" A 100% ground speed (0% aquatic speed) tortoise, bought for a mix of Angler marks and BG marks. The idea is a tortoise model mount for everyone... But without the special water functionality. Because this turtle is so fast on land, "Swifty" seems like an appropriate name that doubles as a nod to the warrior class PvP "guru" Swifty -- who first gained prominence for using the "Skull of Impending Doom" to zip around at insane speeds, ignoring crowd control. Once learned, the buff for the mount would simply read "Tortoise of Impending Doom."

Once the fishing tournaments are tweaked a bit, I'd also like to see similar competitions of other types incorporated, like a Nessingway Safari and Northrend Nessingway Safari... and maybe a race of some kind for each continent, launching hourly and with key checkpoints, requiring you to race around each continent without zoning, flightpaths, portals or teleportation and arrive at a destination on the opposite end of the continent first.
