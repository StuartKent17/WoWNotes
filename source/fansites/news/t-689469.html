Changes to PPM procs
We changed the way PPM procs work to make sure many of the weapons and trinkets did their expected performance in Icecrown instead of proc'ing too much for some specs or classes or forcing players into unusual behavior to try and fish for the procs. This change also affected some older mechanics in the game too such as the Berserking enchant. For the most part the intent is that these systems all proc off of attacks and they are balanced with that assumption.

At this time we're happy with the damage that characters are now doing after these changes, though we'll continue to monitor and adjust damage overall and that includes the proc-based items. (Source (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/28/22418707360-ppm-behavior-nerf.html))

It's a difficulty setting, really. You'll eventually clear the encounter if you keep at it for a couple weeks, but what it's supposed to distinguish is between raids that learn from their mistakes quickly and adapt and those that don't. It's going to be the same for hard modes too, where the most skilled guilds are going to clear it first because they adapt quicker and don't get locked out--not because they have more time to invest on it. What people need to be on the lookout for, however, is 100% dependency on their attempts system to make an encounter hard. It should go on top of existing difficulty to meter guilds. Otherwise we end up with a disappointment like Algalon all over again.

Personally, I like the idea of what they're trying to do. It's not a bad design because it cockblocks people. From fight mechanics a lot of fights historically cockblocked people for over a month purely by the fight being hard. But it can become a bad design when it becomes the centerpiece and most important aspect of every encounter, where RNG > the raid. (Icehowl + Lag maybe?)

The bigger issue is that some players are too used to clearing normal modes the night they're released. It happened because the last tier was so easy and because the first wing of ICC set expectations about the rest of the tier. Players seem to feel that they should be exempt from the pains of progression altogether, clear the instance instantly, and get out of there so they can jump around in circles at the south bank in their shiny new epics. But I ask this: If you don't like progression, are you bored of raiding? Because that's been the focus of raiding since the beginning of MMORPGs.
