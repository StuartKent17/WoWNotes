Is it just me, or did none of the Blacksmith items have ANY gem slots?

If this is the case, what the hell would be the point of doing any of it since nobody would buy it over random dungeon trash drops that DO contain gem slots?

That's pretty much how it is. They have really jacked up some profs. Engineering got just raped. I'm talking like jailhouse dropped the soap kinda raped. Blacksmithing, and leatherworking don't seem to have fared much better. They are both rng reliant. The cooldowns on the tailoring profession seems prohibitive unless you have multiple alts with tailoring. When it really comes down to it there are only a few really helpful professions and I suggest everyone switch to them. They WILL be the money makers.

Jewelcrafting, no surprise there. If this goes similar to wrath, then there will be a slow process of acquiring recipes. If you manage to team up with an alchemist you can get in early and set the market. Anyone that isn't a JC will either be forced to run around with empty gem slots, or pay the price. I see jewelcrafting as being the profession to level quickly.

Alchemy, just selling uncut gems would be enough. You also get to be a freaking Dragon. Outstanding! This is going to be a great class in cata. In the past though it has been less interesting, they worked on that with wrath. In cata though I think this will be the second best class to go with.

Enchanting, there is no downside to this class. It matches up well with Tailoring allowing you to skip the lesser bonuses of a gathering profession. Being able to Disenchant questing greens, and dungeon blues. Now with true multi class bonuses this profession looks excellent for gold value. Just like with jewelcrafting if there is any kind of buildup to certain enchantments then this will create a fantastic market. People will pay fantastic amounts of money for enchantments, this plus enchanting vellum ensure that the enchanter doesn't even have to stand around in town. Add to this the ability to apparently sell on the AH their pet the Enchanted Lantern.

Those three certainly look to be the absolute best in the crafting market. While gathering professions are always strongest at the start of an expansion. With enchanting having the ability to gather it's own mats from disenchanting things you would normally sell it looks to be a solid investment. For the amount of time, and money spent leveling the profession you would see a very healthy return shortly. Of course Jewelcrafting is a great way to make money, and mixing that with the typical mining makes perfect sense. Green gems are going to be fetching quite a sum of gold in the first 2 to 3 weeks after cataclysm. Considering many people will want to go with alchemy in the expansion, (who doesn't want to be a dragon) then herbalism is a good choice also. The best matches for herbalism are of course mining, or enchanting.
