QUESTION 1: Will we ever see weapon restrictions on abilities for certain specs removed?
EXPLANATION: BM Monks, WW Monks, Frost DKs, and Fury Warriors are mainly the ones it affects the most. All have these arbitrary limitations on weapon use in their spec even though they have those weapon skills. It still seems very clunky after Legion especially in the case of Death Knights, as they have the only dps spec that uses one handed strength weapons which means they have to compete with tanks for loot drops. Meanwhile Guardian Druids and Vengeance Demon Hunters use the exact same weapons as their dps counterparts with no restrictions given, with druids having the only two melee specs in the whole game that are able to switch between dual wielding and 2handers


QUESTION 2: For dungeons, what is the purpose behind still having a heroic and normal difficulty?
EXPLANATION: Heroic in particular has such a small lifespan, it requires an item level of 305 but really only lasts until 320 because of Warfronts and LFR. Also especially due to mythic and mythic+ dungeons they fall into total obscurity for the endgame. And since Warfronts were released, the only time I've ever had reason for an alt to queue for a heroic dungeon was to get that last bit of small item level difference that world quests or the rares in Arathi couldn't get me, and after 4 alts that's only ever happened once. After that, the only reason I ever touch them is if there's a world quest involved, and that's very rare since the time spent doing that usually isn't worth it. Both difficulties should be combined with a similar loot scaling system to world quests added in, which would also benefit leveling players with faster queue times due to the increased queue pool.


QUESTION 3: After the large changes to professions, will racials ever be altered to accommodate for the new system?
EXPLANATION: Having additional points in a profession offers no benefit anymore, especially with gathering professions. Dark Iron, Lightforged, Worgen, Tauren, Dwarves, and Highmountain all have traits that permit additional benefits to their profession racials that are actually very fun and add to the race. Meanwhile Gnomes, Draenei, Blood Elves, Goblins, Nightborne, and even Pandaren are given a racial that feels entirely useless no matter how they are played and add no identity to the race at all, and also in some cases feels like it takes away would could have been another better racial in its place.
