Brutallus PTR Hotfix
Brutallus is now much, much harder.
We're currently hotfixing some additional changes for Brutallus on the PTR. Feedback on the the encounters in the Sunwell has been excellent so far, and we appreciate the assistance provided by players in testing and tuning these encounters.

- Brutallus' health was decreased from around 11 million to 10 million.
- Meteor Slash damage has also been increased from 10000 to 20000, the effect duration has been doubled, and you shall no longer dodge, parry, block the ability. Brutallus can also no longer miss with his Meteor Slash.


PvE tokens for PvP items !
Gates are now opened on the US PvE test realm, a few more vendors are available and it looks like you will be able to :
Use Tier 4 Armor Tokens to buy Season 1 Gladiator set.
Use Tier 5 and Tier 6 Armor Tokens to buy Season 2 Gladiator set.
Use [item=29434] to buy Season 2 "honor" armor. (40 badges for boots and belts, 25 for bracers)
Use your Tier 6 Sunwell Armor tokens to buy season 3 belt/bracers/boots.





Sunwell "badge" loot
It looks like another batch of Sunwell loot is available with the opening of the gate on the US PvE servers. These items are now "activated" on the server but we don't know yet how players are supposed to get them.

Bottom line is that World of Warcraft is, has, and always will be, the casual gamers MMO.

Without the casual playerbase, this game sure as hell wouldn't be as popular as it is. If you're after a game that only rewards the hardcore players, then cancel your subscription, sell your account, do whatever. This game will never cater to you.

And it really is mind boggling as to why the hardcore players are getting angry that a casual MMO is catering to casual players moreso than the hardcore players. I mean really now, you're playing the wrong game.


Hardcore players also have to realize that commitment fluctuates too. It's something I lost sight of for a while and only gained sight of it again as my RL schedule gets busier.

Most casuals inevitably want to step up and go hardcore in some facet of the game if their RL circumstances can permit such a level of commitment. Conversely, you will have hardcore players that burn out and slowly step back into a more casual approach to the game.

Lastly, if you're a hardcore raider still griping about this attunement lift, you should seriously consider your guild's place on your server's progression pecking order. A good deal of guilds that have had BT/Hyjal on farm for months don't care about the attunement lift. Their status symbols aren't measured by the gear they get, but the bragging rights they acquire for mastering the content in relatively short time.

The epiphany I've gotten so far from personal experience and from observing the other cries here is that the outcry is mostly based in the outlandish insecurity of a remote possibility that a guild of "scrubs" could possibly surpass one's own guild on the progression ladder. If that's the case, you shouldn't be crying about keeping things segregated to preserve a misbegotten sense of elitism; you should be looking for a better guild.
