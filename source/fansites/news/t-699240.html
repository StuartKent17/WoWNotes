Icecrown Citadel Raid Buffs Active
The zone-wide buff (http://www.mmo-champion.com/news-2/icecrown-citadel-zone-buff-blue-posts/) is now official.
As many of you may have noticed, the Icecrown Citadel raid buffs have been activated. These buffs increase total health, healing done and damage dealt by 5% for all raid members. In addition, the number of attempts on the final bosses of each wing and the Lich King allotted raid groups on Heroic difficulty has been increased to 35. These buffs -- referred to as Strength of Wrynn for the Alliance and Hellscream’s Warsong for the Horde -- can be deactivated by speaking to your faction’s leader at Light’s Hammer.

Patch 3.3.3 Notes Update
The Patch 3.3.3 Notes have been updated with a couple of extra class changes and clarifications.

PvP
The amount of Honor awarded for an Honorable Kill has been increased by 100% for characters of all levels. This change will effectively double the amount of Honor received from Honorable Kills, or for completing Battleground and Wintergrasp objectives; however, the amount of experience gained from completing Battleground objectives and the amount of Honor rewarded for completing each Wintergrasp quest remain unchanged.

To put things into perspective: you CAN notice differences in moving pictures up to 60Hz, if you really pay attention. More makes sense only if your image is going black-to-white all the time (like in old CRT monitors).

One advantage of the limitation: it saves CPU and GPU resources. If your game runs at maximum framerate, it will essentially run as fast as your machine allows, eating up 100% of your CPU (bit less if you have multicores). Now that the drawing of the graphics is already taking all your resources, other (non-graphic) parts of your game might run into problems.

This is actually a common issue in 3D graphics, which programmers must consider. I don't think they had that problem,, but why waste computing power on some extra frames that you won't be seeing anyway?

P.S. you might note that most of your TFT monitors can't even display more than 60-80.

When LCD shutter glasses are used for stereo displays, the effective refresh rate is halved, because each eye needs a separate picture. For this reason, it is usually recommended to use a display capable of at least 120 Hz, but 200 Hz is optimal. Unfortunately most monitors cannot handle this rate, especially at higher resolutions.

This is where the 200 comes from. So while you can't recognize the difference between 100hz and 200hz you can tell the difference between 60hz and 100hz when playing in stereo.
