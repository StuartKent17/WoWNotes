An announcement about combat changes in Knights of Fallen Empire was posted yesterday, with one of the more interesting features being Level Sync.


Players will be scaled down to the target level of the planets they visit, having their stats, armor, and weapon damage reduced, but keeping all of their abilities.
Players also will earn scaled experience, meaning they that if a player is level 60 and is killing level 30 mobs, they will gain experience appropriate for being a level 60.
This means players can return to older content and play it closer to the intended experience, while still progressing their character.

Oh, SWTOR is doing level sync? That's awesome, given Blizzard's history of taking good stuff from other MMO's and incorporating it in WoW. Hopefully this is received positively so that they rip it off. I wrote years ago that I wanted this exact feature:

- When in a contested zone below your level PvP will be optional, with PvP enabled you are automatically scaled to the target level of that zone, and you cannot fly.
- If you disable PvP you will be your real level and, if you are that high leveled, be able to fly.
- Quests will be changed to always grant you XP as a percentage of the level bar rather than fixed numbers.

The consequence would be this:

1) World PvP will drastically improve as high-level ganking will disappear. 2) You will still be able to fly over the zone if you are going somewhere, or fly around and herb or mine nodes, or boost a friend who is leveling - you just can't engage in PvP.
3) As you level you can pick and choose more which zones to do, with more choices the higher up you get as more and more zones below your level becomes available for you to go to scaled down, but then again you already have multiple starting areas from 1 to choose from.

In my view you don't really lose anything of the current game with this exact implementation. It would add more variety and life to the in-game world.

For me the by far best level sync system I've seen yet comes from FFXIV. In that game you basically remain on your max level in the open world, if you however choose to take part in events (which are primarily meant for the leveling folks) you need to sync down to the event's maximum level first so that you don't vastly overpower it and steal contribution from others. A similar thing could be done with WoW's PvP in the open world, or when questing in specific areas.

Dungeons in FFXIV are always synced (much like WoW's timewalker dungeons) when accessed via tool / random daily dungeon, not only to keep them entertaining but also to make it possible for new players to always find a group for those dungeons. That has some notable impact on queue times as, despite having a much lower number of active subscribers, queues are relatively short for almost everyone and everything in that game, as opposed to some ridiculously long or even impossible queues in WoW.
