That's not what the addon does. It doesn't just track eclipse timers. SquawkAndAwe does that.

I'll give the brief rundown in the hopes those who don't understand will read this and understand.

Eclipse lasts 15 seconds. The opposite Eclipse will not proc while Eclipse is up. Eclipse procs for the opposite spell on CRIT. The Eclipse for Starfire increases Starfire's chance to CRIT 30%. Whether a spell crits or not is decided ON SPELLCAST (not on hit), but the Eclipse proc occurs on hit.

All of the above will lead the logically-minded among you, even who don't know a thing about boomkins, to realize there's a big opportunity here. After the last Starfire you'll be able to cast under the Eclipse timer leaves your hands, you can /cancelaura Ecilpse. That Starfire still gets +30% chance to crit, which raid-buffed is nearly 100%, so you'll proc the Wrath Eclipse immediately upon that Starfire hitting the target. NOT using WiseEclipse or manually using a /cancelaura macro at the proper time means casting more unbuffed Starfires (at least one per rotation, if not more), because you'll need to cast it at least one more time for the Starfire Eclipse to have fallen off on its own by the time it hits the target, and since that Starfire is getting 30% less crit, there is a reasonable chance you'll need to cast more to get one to crit. In addition, due to chain casting, you'll be halfway through ANOTHER Starfire cast when Wrath Eclipse finally procs, meaning you lose some Wrath Eclipse time. Using the addon/cancelaura you can start casting Wrath immediately after the last Starfire of that Eclipse, assuming it'll crit, since its crit rate is above 90%.

Now all of the above in reality amounts to a 3-5% increase in dps over not using the above. But that's still an increase. Boomkins who didn't use an addon or manually /cancelaura will see no changes from that nerf, since both Eclipses can't be up simultaneously anyway.

The reason for the nerf isn't to "keep boomkins down" or anything silly like that. And the addon wasn't an exploit, nor was any of this due to a bug. It's normal game mechanics, NOT a bug. However, it IS unintuitive play (no one can say it's INTUITIVE to cancel a buff early in order to increase your dps) and Blizzard doesn't like unintuitive play. It was changed for the exact same reason as the rogue poison change, to remove an addon from being functionally necessary for maximum dps with the spec.

The difference was that the rogue change was done dps-neutral with rogues USING the addon, and a dps increase to rogues not using the addon. The boomkin change was done dps-neutral with boomkins NOT using the addon, and a dps decrease to boomkins using the addon.

The 2pT8 change was also necessary, since boomkins without access to Heroic 25-man Icecrown gear were going to STILL be using T8.5 gloves and shoulders with all their 25-man normal Icecrown gear. Which is silly. Blizz doesn't like people using one tier old gear (see shaman T9 set bonus change), much less two tiers old.

However, while both changes were good and necessary changes, both of them are nerfs, yes. So the only thing I can hope is boomkins get some kind of buff baked in to make up for them. Because I'd really hate to see boomkins drop down to shadow priest levels at the bottom of the pack. Hell, with the frost mage changes in 3.3, FROST may outdps shadow and balance, and THAT would just be sad.

Please, read the change again, and you'll realize he's right. What the change does is this, and only this: When you proc an eclipse, you can't proc the opposite eclipse for 15 seconds. These 15 seconds are NOT counted from the end of the first eclipse, but from the moment it starts. This means, as long as you don't cancel the first eclipse, then your 15 second cooldown ends at exactly the same time as the first eclipse. Hey, presto, you can proc a solar eclipse the moment your lunar eclipse ends, just like before. What you CAN'T do anymore, is cancel the lunar eclipse mid-starfire-cast and thus get a solar eclipse proc out of the starfire that finishes casting before those 15 seconds are up.

Now, I've never played a balance druid, but it seems to me it should be quite possible to just time the last starfire you cast during a lunar eclipse so that it finishes casting after the lunar eclipse ends, achieving the desired result at the cost of maybe delaying that starfire by a small amount. As far as I can tell, this is what crafty players without the addon have been doing already.
