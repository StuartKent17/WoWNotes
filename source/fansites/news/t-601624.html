Assuming this is all true...

I'm interested to see how they implement the Deathknight and thus how they will make it "exclusive". If they make it in a way that requires you to have levelled X number of level 70 classes, then those who play one class exclusively are going to be at a massive disadvantage to those who levelled alts. I'm not sure if this is a bad thing or not, but in my opinion access to a special ability should be equal for all and no-one should "start" in a better position to another.

Then there is the question about the class itself, is it meant to be "exclusive" or is it going to be relatively easy to unlock; my guess is that it will be easy to unlock - something as simple as getting a character to level 70 and performing a quest chain + group quest in a heroic (similar to the epic Druid flight form). Depending on how exclusive it is, what will it's relative powers be; if they make a class to rule all (like the Jedi in SWG) then eventually the game will be made up of 50% Deathknights and 50% the rest, unless they add more "super classes" to keep people coming, in which case the old classes will gradually get phased out.

Overall I agree with someone above about it being a bad business idea. I think the Deathknight will be reasonably easy to unlock and it'll be no better or worse in relative power than existing classes. If it is better then it's going to cause far more problems for Blizzard than if it's just a "normal" class.

This is obviously, assuming it's not all a big fat fake. I seem to remember a blue post that they would never add another class until they are happy with the balance of the present classes. Well I'm sorry, but it doesn't take a genius to see that the classes of WoW are not balanced; not in PvE or PvP.

When they refer to the Jedi from SWG they are most likely only talking needing to unlock it before you can access it. If they go this route in WoW there are things to realize:

First off, you won't have to skill up random professions or weapon skills or anything like that. The reason why is professions and weapon skills are not one of the primary focus of WoW. In SWG one of the central aspects of the game was you could pick which skills you want and customize your character like that. Whether or not you thought it was a good design is besides the point, it was central to the game.

In WoW, the unlocking of the character slot will most likely take the form of a quest.
