Hunter math:

Per cooldown, your pet will only get 55.6% of the bonus damage it used to. Because of the permanent damage bonus, hunter damage during this cooldown actually ends up being 11% better than what it used to be (although relatively speaking the hunter's damage increase over normal damage is still reduced due to the duration nerf).

That makes it equivalent to a 27.5% damage buff (22.5% less) over 18 seconds for the pet and a 11.1% buff (1.1% more) to the hunter's damage. Meaning ultimately this is a 21.4% nerf to the COOLDOWN damage. This damage boost is active roughly 1/12, or 8.3%, of the fight, resulting in a 1.8% damage loss looking purely at damage numbers during the cooldown. I don't necessarily see this as a bad thing from a PvE perspective, as BM burst DPS was some of the best in the game. (It's just the non-burst damage that falls short.)

However, the hunter gets 10% more damage at all times. Assuming a 50/50 split (and I would guess that the hunter actually does a bit higher percentage than that in reality), that is a 5% increase in DPS. Which means, assuming the hunter only does HALF the damage pre-change, this is a 3.2% overall buff. The higher the actual % of DPS a hunter does, the greater this buff will be.
