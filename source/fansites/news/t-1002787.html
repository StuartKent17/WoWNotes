For anybody still reading these comments. You basically sign a contract to play WoW for 1 year while keeping the same subscription plan you are using currently. (1 month, 3 month, 6 month) Doing so you receive the bonuses of free Diablo 3, MoP Beta access and the Tyrael mount. If you are one who planned on purchasing the D3 Collector's Edition, then you can continue to do so as well as receiving the original D3 free from the Annual Pass. In an attempt to "reimburse" your purchase of the D3:CE and making the commitment to stick to WoW for 12 months, they will credit you with 4 months of "free" WoW game time. So, to lay it down in terms of numbers: (Assuming you pay monthly at $14.99) $179.88 - 12-months/1-month recurring $99 - D3 CE (Assumed price based on SC2 CE) -$59.96 - Credited 4 month subscription time for CE purchase $218.92 - Estimated total (taking into account savings if you purchase CE) So, you could say that by agreeing to the Annual Pass (If you still want to play WoW), you are paying roughly $40 for the D3 CE, which I can see as a pretty good deal. Of course, if you have no intention of playing WoW anymore, buying Diablo 3 (CE or not) is still cheaper and the option you are going for.
