I'm pretty sure that most people don't understand how much animation goes into making a set armor. Its just not take a existing model, slap on a new skin and attach a few points.

For druids you have two races that you have to design: Night Elf and Tauren, male and female.

Now besides that you have to do all the spell casting animation. Moonfire, shapeshifting, hurricane, bloodlust, trinket buffs animation etc. On top of that you have to do attack animations getting ready to attack animations, as well as the actual attack animations, With every weapon type. So thats 1hSwords, Maces, Axes, Daggers, Fists, Two handed swords, Two Hand axes, Two Handed Maces, Stave's, Bow's, Crossbows, Guns, Throwing. To top it all off you have to make sure all the other animations work, you know all the emotes and such that random people do. If the animations don't work, you get these lovely blue and white cubes where the animation is supposed to be.

Basically for Male Night Elf Druids you have to do about 60+ animations with each piece of the set armor. Multiply that by 10 races and 9 classes? Assuming 20 minutes per animation, that is a staggering amount of time needed to develop the designs and armor.

I don't know what fantasy world some people live in, but animation is not easy, and animators are not readily available, and I don't believe that gaming set animation is something that many people in animation wants to go work in.
