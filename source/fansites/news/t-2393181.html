Class Development in Beta
Being in beta now, how locked in are certain aspects of BfA class design? Are you still looking at class design as a whole or just addressing bugs or things that don’t function well? In the Legion beta many issues for class design were flagged by players but for the most part went unchanged for whatever reasons Blizzard had. This led to many severe class overhauls mid expansion. Can we expect that again?
Hello!

I wouldn't call anything "locked" during testing and active development. Our development processes are as iterative as they've ever been, and we're always looking for places where we need to make further changes to meet design goals.

Having said that, we're close to finished with the changes to the rotations and the look and feel for most specs. We’ve still got a few changes coming soon in the Beta to Unholy Death Knights, Frost Death Knights, and Protection Warriors.

We used the Alpha timeframe to make some surgical changes to several other specs, and we're somewhat finished with that stage of development for this expansion.

In the days and weeks to come in the Beta, we're going to keep a focus on how every spec plays with regard to the new itemization systems in Battle for Azeroth. While it's certainly possible that feedback on how Azerite affects a certain spec could lead us to do further development on that spec's rotation, we don't expect to need any big changes to talents that would justify the words "severe" or "overhaul".

Beyond itemization concerns, there may be spots where we’ll do focused problem-solving around rotational issues (examples: “it turns out that simulations say never to use X, but X seems like it should be part of my core rotation” or “my spec is too feast-or-famine, and if I have bad luck with Y procs, I feel useless.”).

As always, thank you very much for testing the Beta and making your experiences testing inform your feedback. Focused feedback is super-helpful.

I don't think it took that advanced thinking to expect us to be where we are with classes the very day they announced the artifact system. It was never going to be a pretty thing at the end of Legion, when it was inevitable to feel like most classes lost mechanics one would come to expect or enjoy. So I hope Blizz can navigate clear from that with the azurite system and such, because right now it does look a bit messy, and somehow the revamped specs look most complete just because they saw a ground up re-evaluation after Legion.

I think Blizz has very talented class designers but i think they just give them too little time before the balancing period sets in, where things has to be locked in. To use Balance as an example, it's got a wonderful kit, it's truly great to play the spec... until you go into combat and fall asleep, just from the arti removal. It's so close, but it needs something more to be truly wonderful, in that combat rotation.

Also, don't get me wrong, it sounds like I hate the Artifacts. I don't, really, but I think it seems like there was too little plan when time would come to retire it; nobody likes to feel like their class got less dynamic and that has to happen if you surgically cut a system that integral to how the class plays. It has a chance to continue in the Azurite system, but it's been a giant question mark for so long that it just won't calm people. So I hope we get an in depth design blog on how that's going to work. If it's mostly boring passives, more like traits, etc. To what extent it will replace arti traits, essentially.

They should just have made the one artifact ability a baseline spell for each spec. This is not rocket science, really. We have learned the respective spell / ability from wielding the artifact, and got to keep the knowledge despite their destruction. Easy as pie. Though maybe some artifact abilities are not as spectacular as others (Sindragosa, Fire Phoenixes etc), I would love to keep them all as a baseline spell, not some kind of "chose talent or lose it completely" bullshit. Since they have started pruning characters I feel that we regress instead of progressing our characters.
