Can anyone explain this to me? The penalty from -23% is now -27%. That means the penalty is now 4% less (because it's negative)? So that is a 4% buff?

No. Its a higher negative, therefor its 4%nerf. Try to look at it this way.
When we take into consideration all formulas that make a spell its base damage is theoretical 0% because no changes are applied to it.

However, since Legion Blizzard introduced "Aura buffs" which are invisible by players and they modify specific spells damage by certain %. These Auras usually have multiple spells which they modify.
That being said +% Aura increases base spell damage by said +% on top of its base (theoretical 0%)
Similar thing applies to -% Aura which reduces base damage of spell by said -% from its base (theoretical 0%).
Its simple as that.

However this this gets funky when -% values are in question, so from base damage (theoretical 0%) -% is considered a nerf, however if theres a change in it and that -% is lessen (smaller number than before) its considered as "buff" because its a less of a nerf.

Example:
-30% << -20% << base spell damage (Theoretical 0%) >> +20% >> + 30%

In this case positive values are clear:
0 + 20% = + 20% so its a 20% buff.
0 + 30% = + 30% so its a 30% buff. And its a +10% buff on previous 20% buff.

Negative values are as follows:

0 - 30% = - 30% so its a -30% nerf on its base damage (theoretical 0%)
0 - 20% = - 20% so its a -20% nerf on its base damage (theoretical 0%)

However, if we look at -20% value, as -30% is more negative than -20% it is actually a 10% buff because base damage (Theoretical 0%) is less reduced by -20% than -30%.

Hope that it helps clarifying things a bit. My eng is not a native language so im unable to explain it better.

TL;DL - look at it as X axis on XY graph, the less negative it is from its previous negative value its actually a buff because its closer to base spell damage (theoretical 0%)
