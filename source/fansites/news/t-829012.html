4-stack : 9751

1-stack : 3032
2-stack : 4852
3-stack : 6671
4-stack : 8491

I have been finding that I can slide a 3AB/(ABr|AM), even very safe 4AB rotations with CC procs, more often as gear increases. With this change, the mana required for a 3AB rotation drops from 10447 to 9097, which is 1350 mana. I'm personally excited to see the rotation implications of the mana conserved by this change alone. With a 2AB rotation, in order to minimalize delay between spells, if you don't proc AM on the first AB, you use ABr to clear after the 2nd. If AM procs on the 2nd, you can use it on the next series, but that's an additional 2090 mana used to clear AB stacks without much, if any, DPS gain.

On the contrary, if you use a 3AB rotation more for base, that's a 64% chance to proc AM to be used instead of a 40% chance. The mana conserved could be used to bump up the rotation closer to 4AB, which provides even more opportunity to proc AM instead of using ABr to clear.

In short, don't look at 1% and think "waaaah, not enough".
