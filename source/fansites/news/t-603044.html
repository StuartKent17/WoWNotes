Can someone tell me why Hemo Dagger rogues fail?

Up 'til now, hemo has sucked for daggers because it has been based on weapon speed according to the formula
Damage = Weapon damage + ( AP/14 ) * Weapon speed, which means that a slow weapon ( speed 2.8 ) will gain about 55% extra bonus damage compared to a 1.8 speed dagger. ( 2.8/1.8 ). And on top of that add the fact that the dagger's base damage is a lot lower than the slower weapon.

Now, let's say we have 1600 ap and Rod of the Sun King from Kael'Thas (http://thottbot.com/i29996)
Our average hemo damage would be: ( 270.5 + ( 1600/14) * 2.8 ) = 590.5 dmg

Let's say we instead play around using the dagger Boundless Agony from Azgalor (http://thottbot.com/i30901)
The damage would then be: ( 180.5 + ( 1600/14 ) * 1.8 ) = 386.2 dmg
Both weapons picked to be about equal in juiciness =)

590.5/35 = 16.87 damage per energy
386.2/35 = 11.03 damage per energy

In terms of energy efficiency, using a low-damage, high-speed weapon for an attack which benefits from the opposite is just bad.

For the 2.3 patch, it will be changed and yes, it WILL remove the part with the weapon speed which means one less factor of crappiness, but no, it's still based on weapon damage.

Yes you can stunlock as well with a hemo spec with dagger as one with another weapon, but the damage you will output will be too low to make it worthwhile.

For daggers there is Mutilate and there is Backstab. Mutilate is awesome for its great damage per energy ratio, combined with the extreme combo point generation, so please, don't go hemo dagger or I'll haunt you forever ;)
