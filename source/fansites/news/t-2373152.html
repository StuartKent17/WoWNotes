On a side note, what's a "MAU" ?

Activision-Blizzard now uses this metric instead of subscribers or game copies sold because the free app developers and free-to-play game developers use this to show their number of players (since they don't get regular monthly payments). They started using it when they bought King. Note: I'm oversimplifying the reasoning somewhat, but in general it's correct.

It's not as useful as when we got quarterly subscriber counts for WoW, but it can show a general trend for a company over time.
