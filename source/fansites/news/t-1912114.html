Not really. The "Make a wish"-company gives first world kids who are sick their final chockolate and creates a TV-Show out of it, while the red cross really saves lives, without show-effect.

Whilst the Red Cross does do good work, there are also a lot concerns about where exactly money donated to them actually goes - and if it's used for aid at all. See: Haiti, 2010. The Make-A-Wish foundation doesn't have this issue - they make it clear where donated money goes - either through annual reports or anecdotal evidence from individuals that they've helped. So, ya know, one is definitely more trustworthy than the other in terms of genuinely helping people.

That said... You can't fully compare Make-A-Wish and the Red Cross. You can't. They're dealing with entirely different things. The Red Cross may be more comprehensive in that its help does cover a wider range of people and situations, but that doesn't mean that the work that Make-A-Wish does is, in any way, less important.

Make-A-Wish lets children who are terminally ill find some joy amidst the shitty hand they've been dealt - it comforts them in a time that is nothing but stress. It also helps survivors of these illnesses find happiness again - they may have lived, but they had their childhood ripped away from them by the disease; they spent years being miserable and in pain and not actually experiencing all the joys they should've been able to. Make-A-Wish intends to condense as much of that joy as possible into one huge, spectacular thing, to show the kids that even though they haven't had a good time so far, the world is full of wonder and potential even still.

They are both important charities, dealing with very different things. You can't compare and contrast them, or say one is "better" than the other - they both do amazing work in their individual fields.

Besides, remember Argi? The battle pet? All of its profits went to - oh, that's right - the Red Cross. It was just under $2mill, I believe. Blizzard is hardly ignoring them.
