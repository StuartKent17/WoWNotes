No, it's not directly profitable. Companies do things that are not directly profitable all the time. It's called marketing. I used to work for SGI. We did 20-30 large computer shows a year. We *paid* for a spot on the floor, *paid* for all of our booth gear, *paid* for employees to go, and *paid* for the swag we gave out. Our direct income was $0 per show. Net losses were in the low millions (per year, not per show). We still still did it every year and were thankful for the chance. It's a great way to get out and connect with the people who buy your stuff. Blizzard probably losses a few million buck on BlizzCon every year. They still do it and it makes them more than they lose in the long run.

BlizzCon makes a profit. If it didn't make a profit or result in an measurable increase in sales - it simply would not happen. Only an irresponsible corporation would waste the shareholder's money in a profitless venture with no chance at return on investment. I'm sorry, I know it sounds cutthroat, but that's how public corporations work. You don't get to do stuff for fun, it's only about maximizing profit and minimizing cost.
