Geez, they are really making these fights tuned to be anti-avoidance tanked and just stam stam stam.Avoidance stacking tanks are so fun to heal. For a minute, you're not required to do anything, then the boss bursts a tank down in less than a second.

I'm not a tank but a healer, and from my point of view, tanks stacking avoidance have forgot what they exist for. The tanks have 2 general goals they must succeed in an encounter by performing and gearing appropriately: maintain the aggro and stay alive (with a help of a healer of course). For the arguments sake, let's say we have a boss that hits for 20k to a certain tank with a swing speed of 1 second. Now, if you have a tank that has 38k hp and 60% dodge, the chance that boss will hit him twice in a row is still possible, even probable considering most fights last over 5 minutes. This boss would kill that tank in 2 blows if he had no heals inbetween the blows. If the tank healer is just in the middle of his cast between those blows, we'd have a dead tank over here. Now, tank nr. 2 has 42k hp and 20% dodge. This tank requires A LOT more healing, but that's hardly any problem considering paladins still have infinite mana if they are geared like they should be. That paladin might even be happy because he's most likely topping the meters because of his tanks crappy avoidance. This latter tank does NOT die in 2 blows, but it'd take 3 to kill him, which would require the tank healer to screw something up pretty bad to get his tank killed.

In short, tanks that have bigger health pools are MUCH easier to heal than avoidance stacking ones. I'm happy that my guild has tanks who realize this.
