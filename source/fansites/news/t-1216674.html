I've always felt that Monks have been really weak healers compared to other healers (druids, paladins and shamans). Pretty much every healing Monk I know has been whining about buffs, especially to healing spheres... but I guess they might just be bad.

Depends on level and circumstances.

For the first 85 levels of the game, mana regen is kinda not an issue very much right now, and in that environment monks can do crazy well.

In addition, if the damage you're receiving is coming at a low enough rate that "fistweaving" (healing via DPSing) is adequate, then monks will seem OP, since they're just going around punching stuff and nobody is dying.

But my experience is that as you hit 85 and go to 90, mana becomes considerably more of an issue. You really have to forget many of the instincts the 15-85 game may have taught you, and PUGers really don't like waiting for healers to drink between pulls.

As for healing spheres, my theory is that this change is to make the glyph become an interesting choice. Before the discussed change to healing spheres, they lasted so long that the glyph would be silly to take, and the only problem with them was idiot DPSers avoiding them instead of using them (and then whining about lack of heals). Now, they're much less useful than before, unless you glyph them, in which case they should become slightly (but not extremely) more useful than before.

(Unless the glyph is changing too? I should log into the PTR on my 90 mistweaver monk and test that, as well as testing mana performance with her current gear under the new rules.)
