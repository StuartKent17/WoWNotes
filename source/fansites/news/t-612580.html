However, considering they are trying to make Spirit a useful stat for all classes (see Lifetap for Warlocks now scaling with Spirit), it seems that Paladins and Shamans are left in the cold - and Paladins can go for Crit, while Shamans only can with a very specific build. So it seems logical that both classes will - during the beta - gain some benefit from spirit - and neither class has a "X% of your mana reg works during combat" talent like Druids and Priests do.

So, going for Illumination and Water Shield would be the logical way to do it, since those ARE the mana-reg talents of those classes.

Though - who knows. Maybe Blizzard decides that spirit will stay a "bad" stat for those classes and not make any of those changes - then all mail and plate "healing" gear will have lots of MP5 instead of spirit. But considering that a multi-stat-item will pretty much always be more powerful (from an item budget point of view), I'm not sure it would be fair. We'll see.

A lot depends on itemization - and considering they want to make as little loot "rot" as possible, making spirit - which is abundant on cloth and leather - a useful stat for Palas and Shamans which can equip those armor classes as a last resort, it would fall in line with Blizzard's current philosphy. Just like Leggings of Eternity, Guise of the Tidal Lurker and Kilt of Immortal Nature are options for Paladins and Shamans in BC as well - even though they're aimed at Druids and Priests.
