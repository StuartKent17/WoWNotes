Beams and LOS Mechanics
Drysc explains why beam spells (warlock life drain and mana drain in this case) still works out of LOS and a bit out of range.
Yes, that's how they're supposed to work.

Generally when attacking you want to be at maximum range, that allows plenty of time between you and the target to get out as many casts as possible. Now for a straight up cast and fly spell, it's cast, it's on it's way, and that's it, it's going to hit. For the channeled spells (I had actually never heard them called "rays" before today, we call the effect a "beam" usually) you cast, and you hold, and that damage can be interrupted or broken. To allow some sort of leeway, some give so that at max range it isn't just instantly wasted all of the time (which would happen, a lot, especially in PvE) it's given a little extra room to grow and follow.

It was like a Q&A with Metzen about how when Starcraft 2 was finished they wanted to begin work on Warcraft IV because he didn't like the way the lore progressed in an MMO environment or something of the sort.

Lore seems very difficult to progress when timelines of events pretty much reset upon themselves in an MMO environment and the unfolding of the next chapter/Expansion is contingent upon events of the current chapter. Sometimes, the current events present an incongruity with the direction that the creative team wants to take the game next and the only solution available to advance the the larger story would be to retcon/remove/recreate portions of the world itself to reflect a sense of real time and the effects of past in-game events and occurences on the world and its inhabitants(i,e., the Naxx redux coming in WotLK, the floating city of Dalaran).

This is a difficult decision to make, as unlike in RTS games where players take a 3rd person perception and watch the world unfold from the view of an outsider, an MMO immerses the player in the world itself and provides a more intimate view of and interaction with the heroes and villains. Players grow attached to specific characters that become necessary sacrifices to the greater good of lore advancement, and the ensuing sentiments are seldom ever pleasant. Furthermore, as evident by player reactions to the impact of both patches and X-Pacs, some people just aren't happy with change in general and prefer things to remain the way they are to retain their personal enjoyment at the expense of other players' experiences and an environmental stagnation. These are problems the creative team doesn't have to face when advancing a story through an RTS or any other linear progressed game.

If anything, I think the Eredar/Draenei retcon and its ensuing backlash is still nagging at Metzen.
