The reason the weapon damage increases so much in MoP is because they are nerfing the attack coefficients roughly by half in MoP. So, for example, LFR Gurth'talak will do 1600 dps instead of 800, but attacks like MS will have the weapon damage portion of the attack scaled down from like 160% to 80%. The damage will be the same overall at the level cap, but this is to balance out crazy damage in low lvl pvp.

The increase in baseline weapon damage has the effect of also greatly increasing autoattack damage and making specials contribute less to your dps. I'd imagine that the attack power needed per 1 dps is also going to increase from 14 AP per 1 dps to 28 AP per 1 dps.

This change, however, will definitely mean a higher dps weapon will always be an upgrade, even if the stats are horrible, to the extent that a 476 str 1h might even be better for a combat rogue in mh than a 463 agi 1h non-dagger.
