I really liked this one though, because it added some depth to Magni as well as some new things about his past that we didn't know before, while still building around his original character. High hopes for the third and final comic in this series.

The primary function of the Jaina comic, aside from giving insight into her thoughts and emotions regarding past events of her life, was to establish the political climate of Kul Tiras and what drives her to return to help her people in spite of/because of everything that has happened... along with showing that no, she wasn't useless during Legion. She just acted independently from any major organizations. As for your feelings regarding her, Jaina's plight won't be universally sympathized with so that's understandable.

I do really like this comic, though. Anything that adds a new facet to a character is fantastic. Now I know that Magni's relationship with Azeroth is mirroring his relationship with his daughter. Furthermore, we realize Azeroth's personality is like that of a scared newborn. I like the subversion of a great cosmic entity being seen in a vulnerable state, a mortal state: confusion, pain, a yearning to know what it is, what it is meant to do, what it is meant to be. It lacks guidance, and Magni, who failed to properly guide his daughter and knows the sting of such a failure, has learned from the experience and will be able to do it right this time. It's a chance for him to redeem himself as a poor father.

I take umbrage with anyone who complains this does not 'progress' the story (not you, by the way, you've been lovely and thought out). A single footstep into a fresh new landscape is better than a thousand miles spent on meaningless, arbitrary change. A proper story must learn to pace itself properly. As small as this story is, it does a lot with what little it gives.
