Upcoming Cataclysm Dispel Mechanics
We wanted to introduce some of the changes to dispel mechanics coming in Cataclysm. Our goals were to make dispelling a little less trivial to do in PvP, and to make sure there is more equity in dispel capabilities among healers in both PvP and PvE.

Within the system, there are currently five types of dispellable (or curable) buffs and debuffs: curse, disease, poison, defensive magic, and offensive magic. An example of defensive magic dispelling would be using a dispel to free a polymorphed ally, while offensive magic dispelling would be utilizing a dispel ability to strip away an enemy’s buff or heal-over-time (HoT) spell. The main distinction between these two types is in whether or not you can target an enemy with your dispel.

In Cataclysm each healing class will be getting three out of the five types of dispels, with one of these always being a defensive dispel magic. This design makes sure that finding a healer with the ability to remove magic isn’t restrictive in building teams for Arenas or rated Battlegrounds. It also allows the encounter designers to assume, when designing dungeon or raid fights, that every group can dispel magic.

In addition, we're making the opportunity cost (what the player could have accomplished with different actions) for dispelling a bit steeper. We think the cost is too low for three reasons: 1) The actual mana cost is low. 2) You never waste a dispel. If you try to dispel a debuff that isn’t there then the dispel just won’t go off. 3) We have spells that remove debuffs with minimal input on the part of the player. In Cataclysm we are raising the mana costs, making it possible to waste mana by casting a dispel when there is nothing to dispel, and removing Cleansing Totem, Abolish Disease, and Abolish Poison from the game. With these changes in mind, we are working to plan dungeon and raid encounters where dispels aren’t in constant demand or spammed in order to be successful, though some need for dispels will still be a part of the design.

As previously mentioned, we are providing three dispel capabilities to all healing classes as follows:

Druids will be able to dispel defensive magic, curses, and poison.
Paladins will be able to dispel defensive magic, diseases, and poison.
Priests will be able to dispel defensive magic, offensive magic, and disease.
Shaman will be able to dispel defensive magic, offensive magic, and curses.

There is some trade-off that is being made in making these changes and we wanted to expand on this further.

Protection and Retribution paladins will lose their current ability to dispel magic.
All shaman will lose dispel disease and dispel poison in exchange for Restoration gaining dispel magic.
Restoration shaman, Restoration druids, and Holy paladins will need to talent into their defensive magic dispels.
Shadow priests won’t be able to remove disease in Shadowform.
Mage, hunter, and warlock will retain their current dispel mechanics.
Body and Soul remains the same, and basically any dispel mechanic not mentioned above is currently planned to remain as it is.
When possible, we’d like to combine dispels into a single action. For example, the druid ability to dispel curses and poisons might be a single spell with a Restoration talent that also allows it to dispel magic. This part of the design isn’t finalized, however.

As with all of our Cataclysm previews, keep in mind that any of these decisions could change when we’re in beta.

From the viewpoint of a restoration shaman with two restoration specs (pve and pvp).

As it is right now... cleansing totem is a major strategic decision made by the shaman, designed to shore up our otherwise incredibly lacking defenses from rogues, hunters, (edit: and devouring plague, and infected wounds) and death knights (and when said classes aren't present, other totems (such as HST or MTT) will be used... though on occasion a shaman might decide that a single rogue (especially if they fail) isn't enough of a reason to use cleansing totem)... but done in a way that also creates a strategic decision for said classes (will the shaman drop cleansing totem? If so, do I need to remove it to take this one or his teammate out?). Altogether it adds a great deal of strategic thinking to the game that it otherwise lacks when confronted with otherwise pretty simple shaman. Removing cleansing totem in pvp will not only adversely effect the shaman's survival when attacked by a rogue, DK, or hunter... but also entirely removes a primary threat to poison/disease using classes... one they notably lack in a form other than mass dispel or single-target dispels.

What do I think about the removal of cleansing totem? Absolutely terrible idea, especially for pvp... but one that has adverse effects in pve as well. It provides the shaman's group with the assurance that poisons and diseases will be cleansed, and the shaman can pay more attention to affecting hitpoint bars and other elements of the fight. While my resto shaman would just love to be able to dispel magic as well... our inability to dispel magic forces us to make more strategic decisions on how to deal with any magic-debuffing spellcasters (Notably warlocks, other shaman (in particular elemental and enhancement, though dispelling frost shock can be rather helpful at times), frost and arcane mages, shadow priests, survival hunters (though all hunters can affect the shaman with dispellable magic), and boomkin)... such as is it bad enough to switch Earth Shield off of my buddy... do i risk getting interrupted if i cast a large heal... will riptide+run be enough for this, or will i have to hex/heal... will that mage sheep this fool or am I about to get wtf-pwnt? Good stratigic questions... much more interesting that having a nearly automatic answer to them being "dispel magic spam".

So far as increasing dispels' mana cost to make it a more difficult decision: Is it that difficult of a decision to use a riptide whenever one is being attacked? To instawolf away for the glory of the horde when one's left ankle was just stabbed by a warrior? To sit there spamming lesser healing wave while one snickers at the boomkin, non-MS hunter, or retadin? Hardly. Is it any more or less difficult of a decision to spam cleanse disease on the move when one's being attacked by a DK indoors and earthbind was just destroyed and frost shock is temporarily on cooldown? Hardly. If you want to make cleansing a difficult decision... make it come at the cost of you having to do the hokey pokey for 5 minutes per cleanse, monitored by a webcam. That'd certainly make cleansing a thing done only when it absolutely must be done. Do I think it's retarded that some people think it is not a challenging decision to use cleanse (a whole global cooldown mon... one which might be better served to frost shock, earthbind, or unleash da fury of de elements, or heal an ally with riptide so that they don't die, or instead cleanse that ally, or perhaps flame shock + lavaburst to get the enemy thinking about their own life a little more, or replace your tremor so this warlock doesn't successfully get a whole fear off on you, or use rocket boots around a pillar and get off a large heal, or hex the fool! So many options mon... it's hardly an 'easy' decision to make... as can be said of many other decisions one makes in this game (go to fel reaver ruins or the mage tower first? Stay on D in the flagroom, play it out in middle, or heal the O team and FC? Chance that stables is unguarded early on, or go mine with 4 buddies?). This whole game is a game of decisions... if a person thins it's the best decision to walk away dispelling your dots, then they do. Adapt to their strategy and outmaneuver/outpredict them.

It does all of the disease-poisen dispelling work for the shaman's group (without the shaman lifting a finger), making them wonderful in raids to have in group with a tank who will get raped by a poison or disease that the healers would otherwise have to stop healing the raid for a gcd to dispell. Wherever the shaman is placed and has their totem down, they've made life much easier for their entire group.
