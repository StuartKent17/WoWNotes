Blue posts
Class-Specific Interface
I don't think it is required. Everyone's definition of required is going to vary quite a bit but very few mods are actually required in my experience.

Building an in-game UI to handle paladin buffs is something we would like to do for Cataclysm (then again, so is "overhaul every aspect of the game.")

It was once a goal to make every class UI the same. Since the DK, and more recently the shaman and to a lesser-extent the druid, and then soon the warlock, we've been moving away from that. (Source (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/26/20442618251-gc-pallypower--required.html))

Gear Score
We actually talked today about adding an item level 300 shirt that did absolutely nothing but mess with mods that attempt to boil down players to gear scores. :)

[...] The ubershirt solution would be pretty easy to bypass unfortunately.

Ultimately, we're really not that interested in trying to shut mods like this down. Players will always have the Armory and to a lesser extent Inspect with which to judge other players.

It's not an easy problem to solve. On the one hand, we can recognize that there is value in being able to determine if that guy you are considering for your pug is much less experienced and talented than he claims to be and is going to drag everyone else down and cause other players to leave. On the other hand, the WoW community seems to have become so obsessed with efficiency and so adverse to wiping that there is, in my opinion, an unreasonable demand for player skill and gear requirements even for relatively easy content. It's one thing if your VoA tank is in all blues. It's another if you're asking for Ulduar gear for your Naxx run.

Many players are perfectly reasonable. However we've all run into That Guy who takes any attempt at measuring his awesomeness in the game (gearscore, achievements, dps meters) way too seriously and looks for the same in others. (Source (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/26/20437376814-gear-score-rant.html))

http://static.mmo-champion.com/mmoc/images/icons/ideathknights.gif Death Knight
Scourge Strike on PTRs
It looks like several players are still posting without reading the entire thread. Until SS is working correctly on the PTR, telling us it has double resists isn't so helpful. I don't want to hit that particular nail too hard because I would hate for players not to report any bugs because they assume we already know about them. But in this case it's not going to be effective -- yet -- to tell us about your SS hits on the PTR.

It's entirely possible we'll end up with e.g. a SS that hits for 80% weapon damage (like Obliterate) and does a lot less Shadow damage per disease. Unholy already ignores disease damage very much at its peril so it's probably okay if this one ability doesn't derive so much benefit from diseases. It will lose a little bit of its synergy with the other +Shadow parts of the tree though. It's just too soon for that decision. We need to see some legit numbers first. (Source (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/28/20433766548-scourge-strike-on-the-ptr--information.html))

http://static.mmo-champion.com/mmoc/images/icons/ishamans.gif Shaman
Reincarnation
As I said in the healing forums, we may drop Reincarnation to 30 base, talented to 15. (Source (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/28/20431796675-reincarnate-gets-the-shaft.html))

http://static.mmo-champion.com/mmoc/images/icons/iwarriors.gif Warrior
Warriors in Cataclysm
In Cataclysm you'll get more talent points. That may open up the reality of an Arms - Fury hybrid build. We're not sure if that's something we're going to support yet or not -- we just haven't gotten there yet. For now, we don't really want to see warrior talent builds that go halfway down both specs. They aren't designed to do so elegantly. Arms is really focused around a single slow weapon and bleeds. Fury is built around dual-wielding and "blendering." At the moment, an Arms - Fury build would feel (to us) a little like a Holy - Shadow build -- possible perhaps, but what's the point? That's what I meant by "get both." To do so, you'd have to ignore the spirit of both trees while just trying to cherry pick all the special abilities.

In short, if warriors need buffs (debatable), we are unlikely to do so by making MS - BT the dominant build. (Source (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/28/20566566529-please-fix-mortal-strike--bloodthirst.html))

They aren't talking about people checking out gear scores and achievements and making reasonable intelligent decisions about who gets into your progression raid. They're talking about "OMG, why is this guy tanking H-Nex with a 2100 GS? He's horrible! Why I had to cast two whole heals on him during that trash pull!" I've seen this myself. My alt is a druid, and her off spec is tanking. Now, as the off-spec of an alt, her tanking gear is unsurprisingly lack luster. It's not awful (and it's getting better), but it's not fantastic either. I've actually had PuGs comment about how she *only* had 31K health (closer to 35K now, but this was a few weeks ago) tanking H-VH.

I mean seriously. It wasn't all that long ago that most people were in half blues-half purples, Naxx25 gear was the shit, we *drooled* over a tank with >30K health in a PuG, and we were doing the SAME content. Half blues-half purples is pretty much acceptable gear up through Naxx 10. If the rest of the raid is strong then carrying someone in ALL blues through Naxx10 is no big deal (My guild got the 8 man achievement a few months ago on a mostly alt run. We had two guys below 2K DPS, and my druid's heal set at the time was as bad or worse than her tank set is now. We has three or four mostly Ulduar geared people that kept things from falling apart). Naxx25 gear acceptability runs at "mostly heroic purple".

Doesn't mean you need to bring a guy in all blues on your ToC run, or even your Ony run, but people are getting ridiculous about gear score.

I don't mean this to sound like one of "Those posts" but it's going to anyways:
What we are seeing is a direct result/action to Blizzard's creation of more accessible and easy content. When content was "hard" or at least getting gear was somewhat difficult, Heroic gear and Naxx/Ulduar gear was impressive. Progression was very straight forward and took some time

But now?

Just hit 80 and walk into ToC, or even H ToC if you have friends who can carry you. You can jump straight into gear comparable to Ulduar normal. Run normal ToC 25/10 , Vault 25/10 and your Heroic Daily. That's roughly 50 badges a week, which is enough to almost 2 pieces of 10 man Tier 9. I'm not saying the content is EASY, but the speed at which you can gear up to current gear levels has never been faster in the history of WoW.

As a direct result of this ease of gearing the expectations of players involving gear has skyrocketed. It's now expected you have a huge "gear rating" it's now expected that your tanks have close to or above 40k health, your healers with 2.5k SP, your DPS all above 6k. Fights have become so easy that people are no longer used to non-one-shots. If you wipe once on Emalon, 6+ people Rage-Quit. If they get, or don't get their gear off Koralon, 5+ people quit. You need achivement links and stat links and gear lists to get into even Heroic Dailies or Naxx.

For every action there is an equal reaction. Because Gear acquisition has become easier and fights have become easier, Player expectations have become higher, and patience has become lower. Everyone wants immediate gear, immediate results and with minimal effort or hassle required.

I blame those people who demand gear scores and armory checks and achievement links a lot. But at the same time I can't fault them for doing it as it's just a natural reaction to the direction of the game.
