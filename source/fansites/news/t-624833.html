Blizzard only addressed the group composition woes insofar as they pertained to severe class stacking i.e. resto shaman. The rework of the buff system only creates an even playing field for the 25man raiding teams as they can easily account for every buff/debuff. In other words, gear provided, all raiding teams start off on the same foot. You are never going to have that in a 10man; you are going to sacrifice certain buffs to gain others.

The 10man raids are a different animal; blizzard never addressed any stacking woes in 10man raids because 1) they were never an important enough issue and 2) this is the first time that 10mans are being set on equal footing with the 25man content. We haven't seen what problems arise with the 10man setting because it hasn't been a self-contained ecosystem as it is today. Few people actually went into ZA with only gear from karazhan, they usually had Gruul/Mags and perhaps some SSC/TK pieces. Guilds that ran ZA usually came from 25man groups where group composition is flexible due to bigger player pool. When we go from Naxxramas to Uldaman, we will begin to see all sorts of interesting issues emerge.

Bottom-line: 10mans are not 25mans. Both should be challenging; both should demand intelligent meta-planning. One should not be dumbed down in favor of the other.
