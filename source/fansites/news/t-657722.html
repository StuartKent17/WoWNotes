you guys who think they're going to be (or should be) BOA need to stop projecting your desires on a change that's probably being done for reasons that aren't yours.

Occam's Razor in motion:

Blizzard Introduces TCG Codes.
Scammers discover TCG Codes are a great way to make a ton of money.
Blizzard Gets Grumpy at devoting man hours to reverting scams involving in-game money for an item someone never gets.
Scammers take it to the next level and even chain-scam accounts using phisher websites.
Blizzard sighs, spends way too many man hours solving the issue of compromised accounts due to TCG Codes.

easy solution: non-binding TCG rewards so you don't have someone scambaiting for a code.

None of this at all has anything whatsoever to do with the fact that you picked the wrong character to spend a $1200 mount.

Sorry.

Lots of these changes are for Blizzard's benefit, not yours. trade-able raid drops are for the ability to let GM's not waste their day on "lol masterlooter screwed up after this guy paid me 1200g to give him a tier piece lol" problems. Tradeable TCG codes are so account admins and GM's don't waste their entire time on TCG related scams.

and to clarify a misconception, the cards are sold by Upper Deck, not Blizzard. Blizzard gets royalty money but considering the margins on TCG's these days they're not hauling in boatloads of money off the TCG, it's just helping the bottom line. This is why Blizzard doesn't feel it necessary to spend thousands of man hours of their budget solving a problem given to them by another company. Royalties probably aren't making up for the GM salary devoted to fixing it.
