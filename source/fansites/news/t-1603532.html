Warlords of Draenor - Garrisons: Town Hall / Great Hall
Today we are taking a look at the Town Hall / Great Hall building. This building is a central part of your garrison, where missions take place and where building configurations are changed.

Upgrading your Town Hall / Great Hall to Level 3 currently costs (http://beta.wowdb.com/quests/36615-my-very-own-castle) 2000 Garrison Resources (http://beta.wowdb.com/currencies/824-garrison-resources) and <span class="money-gold" alt="g">5000</span>.

Town Hall / Great Hall NPCs
Along with the Architect and Mission Specialist, there are a few other interesting NPCs.


Blueprints Vendor - This NPC sells blueprints and prices have changed since we last looked at them (http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/4056-Stormwind-Repairs-Reagent-Bank-Blueprint-Prices-New-Quests-and-Items-Hogger-Pet).

Level 2 Small Buildings - <span class="money-gold" alt="g">750</span>
Level 2 Medium Buildings - <span class="money-gold" alt="g">1000</span>
Level 2 Large Buildings - <span class="money-gold" alt="g">1500</span>
Level 3 Buildings - <span class="money-gold" alt="g">1000</span>

Innkeeper - This NPC sells a few items to help with garrison invasions, as well as the Excess Potion of Accelerated Learning (http://beta.wowdb.com/items/120182-excess-potion-of-accelerated-learning).
Chronicler - This NPC allows you to watch the cutscenes you see while leveling again.

Daily Mission
A choice between a solo mission and group mission (http://beta.wowdb.com/quests?filter-currency=-1&filter-search=assault&filter-reqlvl-min=100) is given to you in your Town Hall / Great Hall every day, with the solo mission rewarding 800 Apexis Crystal (http://beta.wowdb.com/currencies/823-apexis-crystal) and the group mission rewarding 1000 Apexis Crystal (http://beta.wowdb.com/currencies/823-apexis-crystal).

When you arrive at the area, you must fill a progress bar to 100% (http://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2014/october/objectives.jpg) by killing mobs and interacting with objects in the area. Depending on the quest, mobs may give ~1 percent each while objects will give you ~2-3 percent each.

Tank Squish Changes
So basically you're saying that after this change that you just spoke of...

1). We will self-heal a lot more in solo/out of combat (so that Ret isn't more survivable than Prot and it ain't taking us 60+ seconds to heal up after combat)
2). We will self-heal about the same during raid content (so tanks aren't overpowered like in MoP)

Is that about right?
Yes.

Any thoughts on Resolve's scaling at high levels of damage taken? As of right now, we're getting pretty absurd levels of it in raid situations; even the mythic tank dummy with no stacks takes us about 80-85% of the way to the hard cap on our healing. I don't agree with the folks that say to make it linear (since there needs to be something to account for our better gear scaling this expansion), but even I think that Resolve's scaling is pitiful at high levels of damage taken.
Vengeance was a huge additive increase on a trivial base amount.

Resolve is a multiplier on a very significant base amount that grows with gear. As your gear improves, that multiplier needs to grow slower in order to produce the same effect. Your gear improving will make those smaller increases in Resolve more meaningful. (Blue Tracker (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/topic/336651-upcoming-tank-squish/) / Official Forums (http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/14058407204?page=13#249))

For Horde, I believe that the art on the building is fine, but the location for the garrison is just depressing.

Frostfire is actually quite barren and beautiful. The reason it might seem depressing is it's almost as barren inside as it is outside the garrison. All the buildings are placed in relatively the same area, so there's no real balance to the area, an invasion can break in and bam, all your buildings accessible right at the front gate, and such. There's no real signs people live there either aside from the obvious "Lok'tar Ogar" setup of the few buildings there. The Alliance feels like it's an actual fort that turns into a fortress, with a good layout, tents, supplies neatly gathered around, and then the flavor, which is a beer garden, a painter in the background behind a building, some little areas to relax, as well as train and work.

The Horde meanwhile are almost entirely militaristic in their design at the moment, like the dev team was taking inspiration from Garrosh's True Horde rather than Thrall's. Thrall's had tents here and there, fur throws at least for somewhere non rocky to stand, tarps here and there for cover. The Horde Garrison by contrast is snowy, rocky, and the tents that are there in the end are either really your profession buildings, or a few on the side that really don't function as tents. There's nothing filling the outside of the buildings area either, like the Alliance side. Alliance has spare supplies, some chairs, and little details that show people have worked there, Horde.... barren. The Tavern could have done with a tarp covering something like more supplies on the side, and such, but... details. The Horde has a grand location either way, what makes it so depressing, at least to me, is how barren the dev team made it. Orcs are not known for their comfortable culture, but the Dev Team decided to make it "Orc Orc Orc" with just a spice of the Horde's races near the end of the profession buildings. Get some Sin'dorei and Taurahe designs in the side for areas that make it seem like more than just militarized orcs live there. After all, even Honor Hold and Thrall'gar (I think thats the place in Hellfire equal to Honor Hold for Horde) have tents, people working those tents and supplies, and extra things going on around the place.

And that's a little damn depressing that a half-burnt and half destroyed, last-ditch fort built in a red, sweltering, apocalyptic peninsula has more of a "People exist here" feel than the Horde Garrison.
