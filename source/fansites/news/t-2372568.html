Battle for Azeroth - Level 110 Boost Details
If you use your Level 110 boost, your character will:


Start with item level 870
Have the Broken Shore into complete, but all of the remaining quests incomplete
Have only one Artifact weapon, which is Level 35 with 3 x item level 870 Relics
Have only the hub for each zone (including the Broken Shore) flight paths unlocked
Have professions (including First Aid) boosted to 700, if the character is Level 60+
Have your Order Hall unlocked, but no progress on the campaign or followers.
Have Embersilk Bags (http://www.wowdb.com/items/54443-embersilk-bag)
Have none of Argus complete and be at the intro quests



Doomsayer's Robes Toy Discovered
The Secret Finding Discord discovered the Doomsayer's Robes (http://www.wowdb.com/items/134831-doomsayers-robes)!

Kill Gordon MacKellar (http://www.wowdb.com/npcs/130828-gordon-mackellar) in Stormwind or Charles Gastly (http://www.wowdb.com/npcs/130911-charles-gastly) in Orgrimmar for a chance at getting the toy. The respawn time is roughly 6 minutes and there are often groups, as the tagging of the NPC can be shared.


https://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2018/january/doomsayer.png (http://www.wowdb.com/items/134831-doomsayers-robes)


Battle for Azeroth - System Requirements
New system requirements were recently posted and we took a look at how they compare to the old ones. If you are ready to upgrade you can take a look at our Computer Forums (http://www.mmo-champion.com/forums/290-Computer) or Build of the Month (https://www.mmo-champion.com/build-of-the-month/).

Mac requirements are listed on the here (https://us.battle.net/support/en/article/137431).


http://media.mmo-champion.com/images/news/2018/january/bfareqs.jpg (https://us.battle.net/support/en/article/137431)

Minimum

Operating Systems: Windows 7 / Windows 8 / Windows 10 changed to Windows 7 64-bit
Intel CPU: Intel Core 2 Duo E8500 increased to Intel Core i5-760
AMD CPU: AMD Phenom II X3 720 increased to AMD FX-8100
Drive Space: 45 GB increased to 70 GB.
Nvidia GPU: NVIDIA GeForce GT 440 increased to NVIDIA GeForce GT 560 2GB
AMD GPU: AMD Radeon HD 5670 increased to AMD Radeon HD 7850 2GB
Intel GPU: Intel HD Graphics 5000 increased to Intel HD Graphics 530 (45W)
Memory: 2 GB increased to 4 GB


Recommended

Intel CPU: Intel Core i5-3330 increased to Intel Core i7-4770
AMD CPU: AMD FX-6300 increased to AMD FX-8310
Nvidia GPU: NVIDIA GeForce GTX 750 Ti increased to NVIDIA GeForce GTX 960
AMD GPU: AMD Radeon R7 260X increased to AMD Radeon R9 280
Memory: 4 GB increased to 8 GB

I was a bit surprised by the I7 recommendation. The only differences between an I-5 and an I-7 are I-7 has hyper threading and a larger L3 cache. There are maybe just a handful of games that support hyper threading and WoW isn't one of them. I-5's generally handle games better due to handling threads more efficiently. I-7's are geared more like heavy processing like Video Editing Graphical Design and CAD. In Gaming I-5 will do just as well but in saying that get the I-5 with the best performance for the buck. You are better off spending the 100 bucks more that an I-7 cost on either an SSD Video Card or Ram.


Personally I use an I-5 6500 Skylake GTX1070 32GB of Ram and a 256 SSD I don't have any issues running WoW. In my Other machine I run a Xeon with similar specs they run almost identical. I've ran WoW with the Pentium dual core based on the Skylake Chip it ran WoW fine as well. Unless there are drastic changes in BoA I still feel an I-7 is a bit Over Kill.
