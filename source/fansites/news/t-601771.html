Like it or not some builds are always going to be under itemised in some instances. This is deliberate and balanced. It is strange that you tell the enhancement shaman to make do with rogue gear while at the same time insisting that feral druids cannot make do with the same gear for DPS even though rogue gear tends to work much better for feral DPS than it does for shaman DPS. As for pillar of ferocity its only serious lack is hit rating, which if you are BT geared you can get lots of from other item slots. Are you seriously loling at the almost 200 attack power upgrade on the pillar of ferocity because you cant get 25 hit rating from other slots?

The reason there is not a lot of boomkin items etc comes down to two simple things, firstly, druids themselves are a relatively rare class and moonkins in particular are much rarer. Most guilds will for example have no more than one moonkin in a raid and many won't have any at all. Expecting for that spec to be itemised to the same degree as, for example, mages is ridiculous.

To use your weapon example, there is a feral DPS staff in Mount Hyjal, one in serpentshrine cavern, one in karazhan, (one that drops in both SSC and kara). Pretty much every endgame instance has a feral staff for the approximately 1 feral druid in a standard raid. Contrast this with caster items, BT and Hyjal between them have 1 spell damage sword, 1 dagger, 1 staff. Kara has 1 of each, and between SSC and the eye there is 1 dagger and 1 staff.

So there are 4 feral staffs for the 1, maybe 2 feral druids in the guild and 8 caster weapons for the 8-10 caster DPS most guilds have which, for most guilds leads to the frustrating situation of having to shard some feral items while your casters are still using weaker gear. There are always going to be specs not very well catered for in particular instances, warrior dps armor was very lacking in the early instances but somewhat redressed in BT and Hyjal. It is what typically happens, as they bring out new instances they attempt to fill the gaps in items left over from the others. You cannot have every class and spec fully itemised in every instance, and this holds doubly true if you are playing a lesser used class or spec.

You say tier 6 feral armor is a small upgrade, it grants a druid a bigger armor increase and a bigger stamina increase than warrior gets from going tier 5 to tier 6. In many ways badge items are the correct way to itemise for less played specs like moonkin, because the alternative is to have a lot of guilds complaining about all the moonkin items they end up sharding.

Lets say you contrast druids with rogues. You have feral tank, feral dps, moonkin, resto druid all of which require different itemisation. Then you have rogues which require pretty similar itemisation regardless of spec.

Just looking at black temple you have 15 leather drops (ignoring tokens).
5 are healing focussed.
2 are spell damage items.
1 is feral tank focussed.
7 are melee dps items, rogue focussed.

If you look at this you have 8 expressly druid items, 7 specifically focussed on rogues. Somewhere a comprimise needs to be made. You cannot itemise for all 4 druid specs equally with rogues, or you would have to have 7 healing, 7 spell damage, 7 feral tanking, 7 feral dps and 7 rogue dps items, in effect having 4 times as much druid gear as you have rogue gear. Since druid is already a lesser played class than rogue they are already batting above their weight as far as total items for the class. So there are as many druid items as there are rogue items in BT, but in order not to have an abnormally large amount of druid items cluttering the loot tables the decision has to be made that druids will have to fill their itemisation gaps with gear that is primarily focussed on another class, i.e. cloth healing or spell damage gear, rogue focussed leather gear.

The only real way to resolve this I can see is to just tokenise everything, but that is apparently something blizzard are reluctant to do. I can understand how it is desirable for there to be a full set of gear focussed on whatever spec you play, but the unfortunate reality is that given the current mechanisms of looting that would result in a lot of guilds getting items they dont want or need because every boss has 4 seperate druid items on their loot table.

It's a lose, lose situation, you either annoy the druids or annoy everyone else and short of just tokenizing all the druid gear I don't see how it can be resolved.
