Zul'aman
Warcry posted their preview (http://wow.warcry.com/news/view/75268) of Zul'aman. Nothing really new but there are still some informations (I may have missed them in previous interviews)
The 4 Animal Gods will be : Nalorakk (Bear), Lynx (Halazzi), Dragonhawk (Jan'Alai) and Eagle (Akil'Zon).
The 2 other bosses will be Jin'zakk (http://www.wowwiki.com/Hexxlord_Jin%27Zakk) and Zul'jin (http://www.wowwiki.com/Zul'jin) (Final Boss)
The dungeon is splitted in 4 wings. You should be able to complete each wing within an hour with a decent group.
Blizzard is working on something called "Logical loot". Previously, the loot tables were somewhat random. They're not going back and fixing years of content, but now, players can expect creatures to drop things that one would expect. If that bad guy is holding a big, shiny axe, then odds are his loot is a big shiny axe.



For the full article, head to Warcry (http://wow.warcry.com/news/view/75268)

Am I the only one that thinks there is not necessarily going to be another riding skill increase?

Just because the other skills (FA, LW, Eng., BS, etc.) go up to 375, this doesn't mean Riding has to top out with the same number. After all, combat skills stop at 350 (5 per level as it always has been). Those two numbers used to match up at 300, and with the expansion, they increased each according to its own pattern.

Before the expansion, there was no level to riding skill at all, the faster mounts were just way more expensive, but that stunk for people who wanted multiples, so they patched a few months prior to the expansion introducing the skill ranks and settling most of the cost into that, maintaining the necessary investment to upgrade your mount, but not so much to diversify.

Now we have riding at 300, combat at 350 & trades at 375. I wouldn't count on combat & trade skills being brought back into synch for any reason, so why does riding have to rise with a new expansion. Some kind of swimming mount might be interesting or neat, but is it necessary (especially when they can just put in something like a submarine to run like zeppelins & boats).

My bet is trades skills will go to 450 (provided level cap raises - and why wouldn't it with new zones? - to provide new gear/toys/enchants), combat skills to 400 at level 80, and riding will stay the course at 300, and someday they'll do the massive recoding they would need to let us fly in Azeroth.
