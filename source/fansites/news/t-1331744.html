Actually the trademark for the dark below has been linked back to the government website and it is real.

The only way it is linked back to uspto.gov (the trademark office website) is that someone filled out the online application form and saved it. The application was never submitted to the Trademark Office officially, as to do so, you have to sign the application under some severe penalties (perjury, etc.). That's why the application does NOT show up in the official trademark office database (TESS) when you search for it or for marks owned by Blizzard Entertainment.

Also, after looking at the "Dark Below" image from yesterday, the top of it indicates "TEAS Plus" - which means that the description of goods and services has to be selected directly from the USPTO's manual (i.e., you can't use your own description) but the description of the "Dark Below" mark is not one that's found in the manual.

TLDR: "Dark Below" is fake and is not a real trademark application filed by Blizzard.
