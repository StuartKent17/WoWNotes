Patch 4.2: New PvP Mounts for Rated Battlegrounds
For more information on Patch 4.2 mounts, check out our preview.



Patch 4.2 Mounts Preview (http://www.mmo-champion.com/content/2321-Patch-4.2-Mounts-Preview-Comics)


In order to best accommodate players under the unique play style of Rated Battlegrounds, we’ll be offering all-new PvP ground mounts -- the Vicious War Wolf (Horde) or Vicious War Steed (Alliance) -- in a unique way. If you want to sit your knickers on these sweet new rides come patch 4.2, Rage of the Firelands, you must earn achievements for winning either 75, 150, or 300 Rated Battlegrounds.

One account-bound mount will be awarded for each of these achievements earned, so if you win 300 Rated Battlegrounds with a character, you’ll be afforded the opportunity to give that character and two of your alts each a wolf or steed. Since these mounts are tied to each achievement and not to your placement on the competitive ladder, they will be awarded instantly rather than at the end of each season as with Arena mounts. Your Rated Battleground wins tracked for these achievements are cumulative and are not reset with each season, so you can work toward the reward at your own pace.

I would not hold your breath in waiting for a potential newly-implemented, top-tier RBG reward to replace the two in question. I say that from a historical perspective on how Blizzard has treated issues like this one, specifically relating to the High Warlord / Grand Marshal weapons.

Way back when the original honor system was in place, it was changed so that anyone could grind and purchase the HWL/GM weapons - weapons that had only been available to 1-2 players per faction at a time in any given week. Those initially competing for the weapons invested months of time before the system was relaxed, after which anyone could purchase the weapons after only hours of play. This hit competitive PvPers hard because the product of the accomplishment became a handout. And while the change was likely meant to infuse excitement into the coming arena system and appreciated by the huge majority, it alienated those who had invested a great deal to rank at the best possible level. Eventually (much later), they were given their highest-earned title as a permanent and replacement reward, but the harm was already done.

Granted, this is a seemingly different Blizzard overseeing the product that is WoW. Perhaps they will handle the current issue differently? Maybe. Personally, though, I wouldn't turn "blue" over it.
