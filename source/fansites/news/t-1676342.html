Not everyone here know exactly what "New Anti-Aliasing Options - MSAA, SSAA, SSAA + CMAA" means, can you or someone else explain those terms?
(for stupid people like me ofc)
Thanks!
MSAA stands for multi-sample anti-aliasing, it sets 2, 4 or 8 sample points per pixel where it'll try to smoothen jagged lines by kind of "blending" the colors at those sample points. This is what we had before Warlods of Draenor. Example (http://images.anandtech.com/doci/4061/EQAA.png)
SSAA means Supersample anti-aliasing and from my understanding, it basically renders the game at a higher resolution and then scales it down which somehow works to smoothen jaggies. It's pretty heavy but the results are usually good.
CMAA is the post-processing anti-aliasing option introduced in Warlords and usable in DX11 only. The way I understand it, it's a sort of filter that tries to smoothen jaggies, and it's around equivalent to 2x MSAA.
