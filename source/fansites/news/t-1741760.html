Dev Stats - Blue Tweets
Today we are looking at the frequency of developer tweets with the 3 week moving average of the number of tweets every week. The @WarcraftDevs (https://twitter.com/WarcraftDevs) account replaced (https://twitter.com/WarcraftDevs/status/535234869274873856) most of the developer's individual accounts from November 19, 2014.

Using a moving average smooths out the data. Keep in mind we are only including tweets used in news posts, which means that extremely busy news weeks will show less tweets than there really were, such as around Blizzcon or when we fell behind on posting and skipped ahead to catch up. Looking at the overall number of developer tweets every week isn't a great metric, as most of the developers didn't keep their tweeting confined to just WoW.

My first to 100 was an Alliance, though I do also have a hunter who I am trying to level up, and I've been trying to immerse him into the experience again (because it's Horde, so it's different), and to be truthful, this does help me do the content at the time I am doing it, though now my problem with my hunter is finding the motivation to even get on him and level him in the first place, because thinking about reading so many more quests, as interesting as the story may be, kind of puts me off, and it's just so time consuming, it's kind of stressful to an extent.

Really, with just one character at level cap, I've been having an adequate amount of fun as of far, enjoying what end content there is, soloing old raids, hunting achievements, etc. I know a lot of people have been bored with the game at the moment, with the content drought and all, though luckily, I've been finding enough content to keep me occupied that I really haven't been feeling that yet.
