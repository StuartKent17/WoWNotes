Combustion
There are two slightly entwined problems here. One is Combustion just not keeping up with other mage talents. That's a problem we want to fix. While there are plenty of examples of talents that aren't as popular as they should be, we consider Combustion to be a pretty core talent to the Fire mage experience, and thus we consider it a bigger problem. (And no, we aren't ready to announce a change yet.)

The other is that character stats are much higher than we originally predicted. This is less of a big deal for things like Str and Sta that can just keep growing, but it gets messy with the ratings like crit and avoidance. While more crit is generally always better, you do run into weird scenarios when players can realistically think about 100% crit chances (if not now, perhaps in Icecrown).

The reason the stats got so high is that the Ulduar model of gear (where hard modes offer higher ilevel) was something we added after ship and therefore after the core combat mechanics of Lich King were already in place. We designed the system with lower numbers from gear than we are ultimately going to end up offering. You might be able to guess at the highest ilevels in Icecrown and those are higher than we originally planned on them being. The alternative was that hard modes didn't offer better loot. :(

Anyway, we can tweak the combat formulae to some extent, but short of "Icecrown Radiance" that drops your crit by 10%, some of these issues aren't going to go away until the level cap increases and we can adjust all of the combat ratings again. (Source (http://blue.mmo-champion.com/28/19595732241-why-does-combustion-have-a-3-min-cooldown.html))

Blaming it on extra ilevels is an excuse. Normal ToC gear is higher ilevel than Hard mode Ulduar. They knew when Wrath released, they'd be adding Ulduar, ToC, and Icecrown before the next expansion.


But the hard mode gear is adding an extra "tier" of items to each zone.

Think of it this way: Shipped 10-mans were around ilvl 200, shipped 25-mans around ilvl 213, with hard modes (Sarth+2 loot and the like) +13. Ulduar then shifted this up 13 (weapons an extra 6). But because of Ulduar hard mode loot, added an *extra* +13 to the tier. So now they either had the option to set ToC normal modes at Ulduar hard mode level or another level even beyond that. They decided to go beyond that by half a tier. So now each tier is adding 26 (or at least 20 for half-tier) ilvls instead of 13 like they would without hard modes.

Non-hard-mode plan (probably their original "vision") for 25-mans: ilvl 213 at ship, 226 in Ulduar, 239 in Coliseum, 252 in Icecrown.

New plan: ilvl 213 at ship, up to 239 in Ulduar, up to 258 in Coliseum (ignoring cloaks at 272), up to 298 in Icecrown (most likely 272 will be the normal modes, 285 hard modes, 298 for extremely special loot like the cloaks).

So we're looking at 33-46 ilvls of inflation in Wrath's ultimate dungeon due to hard modes. And we already see 19 ilvls of inflation in Trials not even including the cloaks. I can certainly see where this might throw their original plans for Icecrown crit levels out of whack.
