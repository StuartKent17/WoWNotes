I think you missed my point entirely... I'm not saying it's perfect or that people don't have legitimate complaints.

It's more like, there is NO perfect because you can't please everyone.

For example, Person A wants Warlocks to be able to easily beat rogues, Person B wants rogues to faceroll warlocks. Blizzard says "we'll make them competitive with each other" but ultimately that doesn't satisfy Person A or Person B. Obviously that's an example where their expectations are unrealistic in a game environment where the goal is all classes being competitive and no hard counters.

Take another, more reasonable example though:
Person A wants to have access to truly challenging small group content that is also rewarding in gear (AKA, not challenge modes). They are a former heroic raider and only the absolutely most difficult content will present much of a challenge to them.

Person B wants access to those same rewards but doesn't know many people in WoW. The content is too difficult to do with LFD so Blizzard doesn't make it available, their gear isn't high enough to get into PuGs on their server because people only take the best geared people they can find for simplicity.

Person A wants the content to be hard, Person B wants that same content to be easier and more accessible. These are both VALID feedback, but you CAN'T please both. The closest Blizzard can come to it is making two separate difficulties, but then people want the gear to be lesser on the lower difficulty so that they feel rewarded for going through the challenge, then people in the lower difficulty still complain because they want to be able to access the best gear.

It's an endless cycle, and it all boils down to when you have people with nearly diametrically opposed viewpoints you cannot fully satisfy both, you can only hope to make compromises that each will find acceptable.

As I said, this is not unique to WoW, if you want a great example of it look at politics. You can't please everyone and it is foolish to try. All I was getting as it that WoW players are very entertaining in how they handle Blizzard doing things that cater to players other than themselves.
