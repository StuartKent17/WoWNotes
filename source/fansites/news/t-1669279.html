Armory Stats - Professions
It is another quiet holiday week, so today we are taking a look at the professions of Level 90 characters at the end of Mists of Pandaria and Level 100 characters today. There are 3.7 million characters and 2.5 million accounts that make up this sample. Keep in mind data collection started recently, so there may be some characters that we haven't come across yet.

Warlords of Draenor also changed how professions work, significantly reducing the incentive to cap your profession right away by not gating items with a required high skill level. In Mists of Pandaria, 95% or more of players capped most professions, but this has significantly changed.

If you have any requests for other stats be sure to let us know in the comments, as a quiet week is a good time to fulfill requests!

2014-12-31, 05:01 PM
There is a great disparity between the crafting professions, which is directly tied to the issues with gathering professions. The exceptions would be jewelcrafting and enchanting, which suffer from a lack of resources in one way or another. This has been my experience between my main and 12 max-level alts (mind you, these are all estimates and not exact figures):

Materials

Mining
Between 13 toons, ranging from level 1 to level 3 Excavations, time spent on gathering ore takes between 30 to 45 minutes combined, approximately 5 to 15 minutes each.

Herbalism
Between 7 toons who currently have the garden unlocked, level 1 to level 3 respectively, time spent on gathering herbs takes about 20 minutes combined, 2 to 5 minutes each. Each toon has Fiona working in the garden and grows a specific herb (i.e., Gorgorond Flytrap, Frostweed, Starflower, etc.).

Skinning
I only have 2 skinners in my coterie of commanders that spend anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour farming 200-225 skins. I timed myself on my last farm run for leather in Nagrand and it took an hour to get about 250.

Cloth
Sumptuous furs take a lot of time to gather. I have a system down where I send out my skinning alt to Nagrand to farm beasts, which seems to be the most efficient way to get the fur I need. During the same hour it took for me to get 250 skins, I got 110 sumptuous furs. This process is repeated 2 to 4 times a week in order to keep up with tailoring demands for my main alone. Only one alt benefits as well, but only as an after thought. My third tailor gets nothing. So I switched from tailoring to mining, since that toon is also a jewelcrafter.

Crafting

Tailoring
The Hexweave daily cool-down requires 20 sumptuous furs and 10 Gorgorond flytraps, with 5 sumptuous furs required for each work order. I run out of furs every two days or so, hence my farming above (see Cloth). Even the barns cannot keep up with demand. However, epic items sell for the same price, and in some cases lower, than Blacksmith weapons on the Auction House. Hence, my entire focus is on my main and upgrading his gear.

Leatherworking
Burnished Leather requires 20 skins and 10 flytraps, much like tailoring. Since skins are more readily available via skinning in Nagrand and I only have one leatherworker, I have to farm leather 2 times a week. Even so, I run out of the skins very quickly and have been forced to make a third and even a fourth trip out to Nagrand. On paper, the mats required seem easy to obtain, but in practice it is actually drawn out, with many occasions where I do not have the required materials for the daily cool-down and/or work orders.

Jewelcrafting
Currently, the only way to get gems is through the daily jewelcrafting cool-down and/or the Gem Boutique, not via the ore which was traditionally the only means to get gems. The mats required for cool-downs and recipes makes this a rather arduous and monotonous process. Consequently, my raiding main only has green-quality gems in Warforged sockets. There's no point in making the blue-quality gems at this juncture when considering that the pieces I'm wearing will become obsolete in the coming weeks. At this rate, I don't think I will have blue-quality gems in pieces until the next raid tier.

Enchanting
I was one of the fortunate ones who benefited from the guaranteed Luminous Shards, so I have quite a few from those first few days of leveling, which I use for the daily Temporal Crystal cool-down. In contrast, the dearth of Draenic Dusts has remained even after the fix. My alts have fed my main, as I have an Enchanters Study in nearly all of my toons' garrisons for that reason. Using my main and 12 alts quest rewards, I manage to maintain a regular flow of work orders and daily cool-downs without a break. Note: I do not disenchant items that are worth 35 gold and above for obvious reasons.

Blacksmithing
No issue where, except I have more materials than I could ever need. I now have over approximately 7,000 of each ore across all my toons. I have two Blacksmiths and they are well-oiled machines pumping out piece after piece of armor and weaponry. And as you can imagine, I am making some good dough because of it. I find this to be the most lucrative profession in the game to date.

Alchemy
Since herbs are no issue, I have managed to make over 100 Greater Draenic Intellect Flasks to feed my main and still have over 200 Alchemical Catalyst in the bank. I have two alchemists, but only one who is producing the flasks and pots I need for my raiding main. Because I am able to get what I need from one toon, my second alchemy alt pretty much does little to nothing with her profession.

Inscription
Inks are not a problem when you have 7 or more toons with the Herb Garden in their garrison. It was a struggle at first, but that was resolved the moment I leveled my herbalist-alchemist toon. I now have more than enough inks to get the daily cool-down and work orders completed on a regular basis. In fact, I found this profession nearly as lucrative as Blacksmithing, specifically in relation to the weapons and trinkets that can be crafted and sold.

Engineering
Once again, with the plethora of ore available to me, I have no issue maintaining a regular stream of work orders and fulfilling the daily cool-down. Since I consider this a fun profession more than a necessary one, given my main is a tailor-enchanter priest, I am neither content nor ambivalent about the changes made to professions in WoD.

Summary

In summary, there is clearly a disparity between professions that range from the mundane to utter frustration. The corollary is the inordinate time necessarily spent in garrisons and/or gathering materials makes for a disjointed experience, where what should be a secondary part of the game (e.g., professions) is now on the main stage requiring the attention of a high maintenance, and rather jealous, mistress. I agree that the professions in Mists were a bit over the top easy, but this expansion seems to have taken professions to a whole new level and one that isn't necessarily good for the average player. I don't think it's entirely bad, but there certainly is room for improvement.

My suggestion would be to lower the yield of ore in the garrison mine, leave the garden as it is, but increase the yield of materials when farmed outside in the world (by 2 to 5 each), thus rewarding those who explore the world on a regular basis. Barns need to produce more skin and fur, even more than what can be farmed outside in the world. This should solve the scarcity issue. As far as enchanting, more time needs to pass before an objective assessment can be made. The change from getting shards to dusts was definitely a step in the right direction. And in regards to jewelcrafting, let us prospect all that ore collecting in our banks for gems, for the love of Christ! There's no reason we should need ONLY the daily cool-down and/or work orders to get the one and only gem used to cut green-quality gems. It just seems ridiculous. I can see using Taladite Crystals for blue-quality or epic gems, but not something that should be more readily available.
