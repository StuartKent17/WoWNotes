Action Cam Removal
Recently, Blizzard posted a message stating they were going to be disabling action cam for the time being. Apparently, "more than a few players" were accidentally enabling the action cam feature.

I'm completely befuddled as to how this would happen. It's a console command, therefore you have to go completely out of your way to enable it. It's not something you could click in the interface by mistake.
To clarify: This experimental mode is/was apparently being enabled in some cases through no direct action of users. The concern isn't that people could somehow accidentally type "/console actioncam full" and then be bewildered regarding how to disable it. The concern is that people have logged into 7.0.3 and found some of those features defaulted on, with no indication to that effect, and the game does not provide any clear direction regarding how to disable it.

"Action cam" will return. It was an in-development feature that ended up being accessible to players in one of our beta builds, and when we saw how much fun players were having with it, we decided to keep it around as a hidden toggle rather than disabling it immediately. It seemed harmless at the time. But there is now clear evidence to the contrary. The mode needs some extra safeguards, and we need to make it easier for players to know whether portions of it are enabled and if so, that it's an experimental feature, and how to disable it. That isn't possible without a real patch (that is to say, not a hotfix, but rather a new patch number beyond 7.0.3).

I think directors cuts require reshoots which cost a ton of money. So it doesn't bother me unless legendary is withholding a Completed directors cut.

Director's cut almost never reshoot material - the costs to reassemble a cast and crew, sets, costumes, etc. months or years after is so expensive it's not even worth considering. Usually what Director's Cuts are, are new edits with material unused the first time (stuff cut for time), and maybe some voice over changes and such. I can't recall a single Director's Cut that involved newly shot scenes - if there are any, I'd love to hear which ones, out of curiousity's sake. Star Wars is the only movies i can think of where new material that didnt exist was added, but it was mostly stupid things like adding Jabba or alien beasts in backgrounds.

The issue is, with a movie like this, that's so CGI heavy, is they have to film and render the scenes in the first place, and if the scenes didn't make the cut the first time, it would cost millions to go back, reassemble a production team to shoot live action, hope the actors are available, film the scenes, then go to ILM and have them rendered, and then all the post-production work to weave them in with existing shots - it's simply not economically feasible. You'be be remaking a big chunk of the movie, or making a large part of it from scratch. I don't see many movie studio's greenlighting something like that. Even the LOTRs Director's Cuts simply used footage and CGI that had been cut for time reasons.

If Duncan doesn't have the shots to make the story bigger, than that's just what it is, unless he pays for the rendering and everything out of his own pocket (and the studio agrees to it). The extra scenes probably don't have transitional scenes to make them make sense, and give him material to work the flow with, because they didn't shoot them the first time. It's one the risks you take making CGI heavy films, unless you're George Lucas and own a production company like ILM, and full rights to the movies.
