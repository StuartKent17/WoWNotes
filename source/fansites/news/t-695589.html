Blue post - Cataclysm Goals
I think some of you are misinterpreting a couple of the Cataclysm goals.

We're not "overhauling the combat system." We are changing some lame stats to be more interesting (like Intellect) and removing some that just added complexity to the game without adding depth (like armor pen, which is pretty much just damage modified by how much physical damage you do). We are adding more health across the board, but that's to add a few seconds in between the time you take damage and the time you die, which we think will improve both PvE and PvP.

We're also not trying to "dumb down stats." Complexity and depth are two different things. Depth is really important to us. Without it, the game starts to get predictable and stale. Complexity can be a route to depth, but it doesn't have to be. The game of go has very simple rules but almost limitless depth (you can use chess if you aren't familiar with go). The game tic-tac-toe has very simple rules and almost no depth. It's hard to come up with examples that lots of players will get for games with too much complexity and not enough depth, mostly because those games either never become widely popular or don't endure. Ever heard the phrase "quick to learn but a lifetime to master?" That's depth.

Yes, we are messing with talent trees, but a simple skimming of the forums should suggest why. There are too many talents that have little depth but are mandatory (most of the talents right about the 51-pointer). There are possibly interesting talents that you can't afford because of the passive dps / healing / tanking increasers. There are also modifications we need to make just because you'll have 5 more talent points. There are also talents that we have modified along the course of LK in the name of closer spec balance that are now out of budget. We're not deleting any talent trees and starting over. We're trying to make them more interesting and more fun.

You can say the same thing about Haste, Crit, Expertise, Hit, Strength, Agility... ANYTHING.


The problem with this comparison is that you're looking at the different stats as they are now and comparing it to how armor penetration will work in Cataclysm. Strength and Agility are core stats, just like Spirit and Intellect for casters, it is their job to be a straight buff without any other interesting mechanic, which is exactly what is armor-pen is doing right now. So yes they do the same thing, that is the problem.

Hit and Expertise are very similar to each other but are different from every other stat in that they stop providing any value at all after you are capped. This makes for interesting gear decisions because you have to get as close to the cap as possible without going to far over in order to maximize your stat allocation. This creates "depth" in the game play because stacking hit to infinity is not in your best interest. While this might sound similar to armor-pen at first, the intention is different. Hit is meant to be something that everyone needs, but only in small amounts because it's not hard to cap it if you try.

Haste and Crit are meant to be the secondary stats in Cataclysm, along with Mastery. They both provide a benefit, but in an interesting way that makes you think about stacking only one. For casters, haste increases your damage by letting you cast more spells over the same amount of time, this lets you have more chances to proc on cast things but has the drawback of being mana intensive. Crit, on the other hand, lets you do more damage but relies on the RNG a lot if you are in a world where someone's total critical chance is around 30% instead of 60-70% because of gear inflation. Critical hits also proc certain effects similar to how 'on cast' procs are benefited by haste.



Their comment is fucking retarded, just because ArP has been dumbed down by spreadsheets and "What does EJ say about it?" doesn't mean that it's any less "complicated" than any other stat in this game.

The math is actually really involved with ArP, and its effect changes based on the Armor of your target AND how much you have. It's the only stat that makes what you already have get better as you get more of it (Haste, Crit, etc are all the opposite of that).

ArP is not lacking in depth compared to the rest of this game.


One of the reasons Blizzard has stated for removing armor-pen is that it is too complicated to understand without doing some out of game calculations. (Or having someone else do them for you in the case on EJ Forums.) While forum browsing for the best possible spec and stats might be normal for a lot of end game players, myself included, it is not for everyone and is not required. Making the reasons for choosing a certain stat easier to see does not make the actual decision easier, it still requires you to know the mechanics of your class. This is a good thing for the game as, hopefully, more players will intuitively know or be able to figure out which stats to choose, raising the performance level of the average player base.

Armor-pen lacks depth for the reason you listed to support it having depth, it simply gets better the more you with no drawbacks or other thought put into the choice. This is something only the base stats should do for your class, and is the same reason for Attack Power and Spell Power being consolidated into said base stats. Also, while you may argue that it has a cap and this somehow makes it interesting, in reality it would be incredibly difficult if not impossible to hard cap it if stats had progressed at the intended rate instead of without compensating for hard modes. (As it will be in Cataclysm) The cap on armor-pen is the same as the haste cap in this regard, there is a point when stacking haste is no longer beneficial because your spells become shorter than the global cooldown, something that is not likely to happen unless you use a haste buff during Bloodlust/Heroism.

I apologize for that being so long, but I felt like people weren't really understanding the reasons for the stat changes and complaining without being informed. This is not meant as an attack or argument against the person I quoted, more of an explanation I think some people were looking for.
