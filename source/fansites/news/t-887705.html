Obviously people here don't have a clue of how animated 3D models are produced. As far as production is concerned, yes, this is a new 3D model. It has new features, a new texture, obviously needed work from artists. What is reused is the rigging, the invisible skeleton that goes inside the model. This is used to animate the models, by setting the movement frame by frame, using the positioning of "bones" and "joints".

New models are relatively easier to make, specially for a game like WoW, where models got to have a decent polygon count to not overwhelm gamers with older computers. Videogames usually have simple models and put the most amount of art and detail in the textures, which are much lighter. Anthicyst's texture is pretty new and shiny, and looks like it did take some work, as well as the model itself, even though it was probably based on an existing model.

What is completely UNREASONABLE to demand are new rigs every single patch. A good rig, with all it's animations, can take MONTHS to be done. And then you have all the work to wrap the model around it and make it animate in a way that doesn't distort the polygons or look weird. That's why all dragons use the same rig, even though they have different models. Sometimes the job is so well done that you don't notice. For example, I remember having read somewhere that dwarves and tauren use the same skeleton, even though they have different animation sets. And then there's the more obvious one, like male tauren and broken draenei, or ogres and moonkin, but it doesn't mean they share the same models.

In conclusion, model =/= texture =/= rig, learn to animate in 3D before calling other people lazy.
