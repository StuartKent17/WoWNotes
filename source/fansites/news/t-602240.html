PVP Gear
I still have some doubts - quite a few - if there should be any gear progression in arenas at all. But that would require a different concept from the successful gear based logic that is present in the remaining of the game. But the issue seems not so much to derive from top end gear being available in the arenas, but from being welfare epics.
Resiliance was a step in the right direction, but it seems that further separation is needed :P.

KZ
Anyway, although KZ is a somewhat interesting instance - too much annoying trash and other not so interesting features to my taste though - guild/progression wise it is a bad thing. For starters I don't consider it a truly 10 man dungeon - to do all bosses you benefit a lot from changing party composition - so I put it more at 12 man dungeon. Then it is, imho, a bad entry point - ZG difficulty lvl for "casual light raiding" would be more adequate - not one shotting people. Finally - and this is the worst part - the way it divided guilds that were setting up for 25 man was just terrible. Bad idea altogether.

Professions
Tailoring is a bit too good for the games own good. While saying it is on T6 lvl is quite a bit of overstatement, fact is that a tailor should bypass most/all T4 gear - except if it was an "excess drop". TBH I don't have a real problem with that (tailor = T4), except from the lock in it causes. I dropped herbalism to get tailoring and made it almost a requirement to be a tailor. Problem is how to make professions interesting and worthwhile without making them compulsory and overadvantageous?

i'd give a few pointers to Blizzard (not that they should need any) - first of them is to restructure the way leveling goes so that they become cheaper to lvl - the end game epics should be expensive to make, not the leveling vendorized items that no one really wants.
Another is to make most epic items tradable - expensive to make, but not BOP. That would cut on the lock in. Personally I didn't find the mats required for the tailored set to be over the top - adequate for such quality items, although a slight nudge from farmed mats (primals...) to "unique" (heroic mode drops) would be welcome.
Finally introduce expendable addons to the profession made items - usable charges that consume some kind of item. Tailored sets, tailor made "charges", LW sets, LW made... and so forth. A bit like the mana injector requires mana pots. The "charges" itself should be inexpensive, but would create a secondary market to those professions - something that only alchemy and - to an extent - enchanting have.

Other TBC stuff

Actually I find it hard to pin down what went wrong with TBC. Yes, there are all those little things combined but to be truthful I have that nagging sensation it is not only those objective point, but something a bit more elusive. In short TBC - particularly raiding - is less fun. I think that the only fight that I felt a common "Wow - it is down!Yes!!!" feeling similar to pre TBC was in Mag. Everyone else was more a "Uuuf.. he is down" (Lootreave didn't even granted that :P).

Maybe with TBC Wow became too much "professional" to its own good.
